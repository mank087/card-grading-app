## TASK 1: IMAGE QUALITY ASSESSMENT

**MANDATORY: You MUST provide `overall_score` as a number from 0-10.**

Evaluate the photograph quality using this **objective scoring rubric**:

### Step 1: Score Each Quality Factor (0-10 scale)

1. **Resolution Score** (0-10):
   - **10**: Crystal clear, can see fine details like card texture
   - **7**: Standard smartphone photo, all text readable
   - **4**: Lower resolution, some small text difficult to read
   - **0**: Heavily pixelated, unusable

2. **Lighting Score** (0-10):
   - **10**: Even, natural lighting across entire card
   - **7**: Adequate lighting, minor shadows acceptable
   - **4**: Uneven lighting but card still visible
   - **0**: Too dark or overexposed

3. **Angle Score** (0-10):
   - **10**: Perfectly straight-on, no perspective distortion
   - **7**: Slight tilt (under 5 degrees), minimal distortion
   - **4**: Noticeable angle, corners may be difficult to assess
   - **0**: Severe angle, card edges warped

4. **Clarity Score** (0-10):
   - **10**: Razor sharp focus throughout
   - **7**: Sharp focus, slight blur at edges acceptable
   - **4**: Moderate blur, some details unclear
   - **0**: Completely out of focus

5. **Glare/Obstruction Penalty**:
   - **-2 points**: Minor glare on non-critical areas
   - **-5 points**: Significant glare obscuring corners/edges
   - **-3 points**: Obstructions (sleeves, fingers, etc.)

### Step 2: Calculate Overall Score

```
Overall Score = AVG(Resolution, Lighting, Angle, Clarity) - Glare/Obstruction Penalty
```

**Example Calculation:**
- Resolution: 7, Lighting: 8, Angle: 9, Clarity: 8 = AVG = 8.0
- Minor glare penalty: -2
- **Overall Score: 6** ‚Üí Medium Confidence

### Step 3: Determine Confidence Tier

| Overall Score | Confidence Tier | Grade Uncertainty |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **9-10** | **high** | **¬±0.5** |
| **7-8** | **high** | **¬±0.5** |
| **5-6** | **medium** | **¬±1.0** |
| **0-4** | **low** | **¬±1.5** |

**CRITICAL: Default to "high" for typical smartphone photos with no major issues!**

**Classification Rules:**

1. **On-card factory autograph** (`is_alteration: false`, `has_handwriting: false`)
   - Signature is PRINTED as part of the original card design
   - Part of the card's artwork/graphics
   - Examples: Pre-printed facsimile signatures on vintage cards
   - **has_handwriting = false because it's printed, not hand-signed**

2. **Certified authenticated autograph** (`is_alteration: false`, `has_handwriting: true`)
   - Hand-signed by manufacturer (Panini, Topps, etc.)
   - **MUST have authentication markers:**
     - Hologram sticker on card
     - "AUTHENTIC AUTOGRAPH" or similar text printed on card
     - Serial number indicating autograph (e.g., "/25" or "AUTO" designation)
     - Official authentication logo (PSA/DNA, JSA, Beckett, manufacturer logo)
   - If NO authentication markers visible ‚Üí it's uncertified!
   - **has_handwriting = true because it's hand-signed**
   - **authentication_markers_found = ["list all markers you see"]**

3. **Uncertified added autograph** (`is_alteration: true`, `has_handwriting: true`) **‚ö†Ô∏è CARD IS ALTERED ‚ö†Ô∏è**
   - Hand-signed with pen/marker BUT no manufacturer authentication
   - No hologram, no "AUTHENTIC" text, no authentication sticker
   - Could be real signature, but not verified by manufacturer
   - **This is an ALTERATION - card cannot be graded!**
   - **has_handwriting = true**
   - **authentication_markers_found = []** (empty because no markers found)

4. **None** (`is_alteration: false`, `has_handwriting: false`)
   - No signature visible
   - **has_handwriting = false**
   - **authentication_markers_found = []** (empty because no autograph)

**IMPORTANT: If you detect `uncertified_added`, you MUST also mark this in the authenticity_assessment defects!**
