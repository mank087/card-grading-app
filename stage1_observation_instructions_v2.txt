## TASK 1: IMAGE QUALITY ASSESSMENT

For **both front and back** images, score each factor 0-10:

### Resolution (0-10)
- **10**: Crystal clear, can see fine details like card texture
- **7**: Standard smartphone photo, all text readable
- **4**: Lower resolution, small text difficult
- **0**: Heavily pixelated

### Lighting (0-10)
- **10**: Even, natural lighting across entire card
- **7**: Adequate lighting, minor shadows OK
- **4**: Uneven but card still visible
- **0**: Too dark or overexposed

### Angle (0-10)
- **10**: Perfectly straight-on, no distortion
- **7**: Slight tilt (under 5Â°), minimal distortion
- **4**: Noticeable angle, corners may be unclear
- **0**: Severe angle, edges warped

### Clarity (0-10)
- **10**: Razor sharp focus throughout
- **7**: Sharp focus, slight blur at edges OK
- **4**: Moderate blur, some details unclear
- **0**: Completely out of focus

### Glare Penalty (0-5)
- **0**: No glare
- **2**: Minor glare on non-critical areas
- **5**: Significant glare obscuring corners/edges

### Corner Visibility
Set `all_corners_visible: true` ONLY if you can clearly see all 4 corners of the card.

### Calculate Overall Score (REQUIRED)

**This is a mechanical calculation - you MUST perform it:**

```
overall_score = (resolution + lighting + angle + clarity) / 4 - glare_penalty
```

**Example:**
```
Resolution: 8, Lighting: 9, Angle: 9, Clarity: 8, Glare Penalty: 2
overall_score = (8 + 9 + 9 + 8) / 4 - 2 = 34/4 - 2 = 8.5 - 2 = 6.5
```

### Determine Confidence Tier (REQUIRED)

Based on the overall_score:
- **High**: overall_score â‰¥ 7.0
- **Medium**: overall_score 5.0 to 6.9
- **Low**: overall_score < 5.0

### Reshoot Recommendation
Set `reshoot_recommended: true` if:
- 3+ quality factors below 5
- Critical areas obscured by glare
- Any corners not visible
- overall_score < 5.0

## TASK 2: CENTERING MEASUREMENT

**ðŸš¨ CRITICAL: Every card has DIFFERENT centering. Do NOT reuse ratios from previous cards!**

### For FRONT and BACK separately:

**Step 1: Identify Reference Points**
- Look for printed borders, frame lines, design boundaries
- If borderless: use photo crop edge, text box edge, logo boundary
- Document what you used: "outer black border to card edge" or "photo area to white border"

**Step 2: Visual Measurement**
- Compare left border width to right border width â†’ estimate ratio
- Compare top border width to bottom border width â†’ estimate ratio
- Express as XX/YY where XX + YY = 100

**Step 3: Determine Worst Ratio**
- Compare all 4 ratios (front L/R, front T/B, back L/R, back T/B)
- Pick the one farthest from 50/50
- This is the "worst_ratio"

**Step 4: Set Confidence**
- **High**: Clear borders, all corners visible, straight-on angle
- **Medium**: Borders visible but some glare or slight angle
- **Low**: Unclear borders, corners obscured, or borderless design

**ðŸš¨ WARNING: Front and back centering are RARELY identical. If you measure identical ratios, provide explicit justification!**

## TASK 3: DEFECT OBSERVATIONS

**For each defect you observe, create an observation object.**

### Observation Guidelines

**1. Location Must Be Specific**
- Use corner positions: `front_top_left_corner`, `back_bottom_right_corner`
- Use surface areas: `front_surface_upper_left`, `back_surface_center`
- Use edges: `front_edge_top`, `back_edge_right`

**2. Type Classification**

**Corners:**
- `corner_whitening` - White paper core visible at corner tip
- `corner_chip` - Missing piece of corner
- `corner_fray` - Fiber separation at corner
- `corner_bend` - Corner bent but not creased
- `corner_crease` - Permanent fold line in corner

**Edges:**
- `edge_wear` - Whitening along edge
- `edge_chip` - Small notch in edge
- `rough_cut` - Uneven factory cut

**Surface:**
- `surface_scratch` - Linear mark on surface
- `surface_scuff` - Area with dulled finish
- `surface_stain` - Discoloration or foreign substance

**Print/Manufacturing:**
- `print_speck` - Ink dot or spot
- `print_misregistration` - Color layers misaligned
- `roller_line` - Manufacturing line from printing process

**3. Description (UNIQUE TO THIS CARD)**

**ðŸš¨ FORBIDDEN - DO NOT USE THESE TEMPLATED PHRASES:**
- âŒ "White paper core visible at the very tip"
- âŒ "approximately 0.5mm in size"
- âŒ "clearly contrasting with the dark border"
- âŒ "Horizontal scratch running 15mm across"
- âŒ "Tiny ink spot located 3mm from edge"
- âŒ "Microscopic scratch visible under direct light"

**âœ… INSTEAD - Reference THIS Card's Specific Design:**

**4. Estimated Size (MM)**

Provide your best estimate:
- Corner defects: diameter in mm (0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, etc.)
- Scratches: length in mm (5, 10, 15, 25, etc.)
- Scuffs: approximate area dimension (5x5mm, 10x10mm, etc.)
- Print specks: diameter (0.2, 0.5, 1.0, etc.)

**If you can't estimate, set to 0.0 and mark confidence as "low"**

**5. Visibility**
- `microscopic` - Barely visible even with magnification
- `faint` - Visible only under angled light or close inspection
- `visible` - Clearly visible in normal lighting
- `obvious` - Immediately noticeable
- `severe` - Dominant feature affecting card appearance

**6. How Distinguished from Glare**

**You MUST explain how you know it's a real defect:**
- âœ… "Visible from multiple angles, does not move with light"

- âœ… "Consistent across different lighting conditions in both images"
- âœ… "Shows fiber disruption at corner edge"
- âœ… "Under the glossy surface layer, appears as embedded ink"

**If you can't distinguish from glare â†’ Mark confidence as "low"**

**7. Confidence**
- **High**: Clear defect, visible in good lighting, can measure accurately
- **Medium**: Defect visible but size/extent uncertain
- **Low**: Might be glare, lighting artifact, or image quality issue
