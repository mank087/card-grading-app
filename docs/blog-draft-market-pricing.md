## Content (Markdown)

Every card you grade on DCM comes with something most grading services don't offer: **live market pricing** pulled from real, active listings across major online marketplaces.

Whether you're grading a base set Charizard or a Mahomes Silver Prizm rookie, we think knowing what your card is actually selling for is just as important as knowing its condition. Here's how we make that happen.

## Where the Data Comes From

We pull pricing data from active listings on major online collectibles marketplaces. This isn't old data sitting in a spreadsheet — our system checks live listings and updates constantly so you're seeing what cards are actually listed for *right now*.

For each card, we search for matching listings using the card's name, number, set, year, and any variant information (like parallel types, alternate art, or special editions). This lets us find the right version of your card, not just any card with the same name.

## Smart Search: Finding the Right Match

Card data is messy. A "2024 Panini Prizm Patrick Mahomes Silver Prizm #1" might be listed a dozen different ways across sellers. To handle this, we use a **progressive search strategy** that tries multiple approaches:

1. **Specific search first** — We start with the most detailed query possible: player name, parallel type, card number, year, and set name. This catches exact matches.

2. **Broadening if needed** — If we don't find enough listings (at least 3), we loosen the search step by step — dropping variant details, then set info, until we find comparable cards.

3. **Card-type aware** — The search strategy adapts based on whether you're grading a sports card, Pokemon, MTG, Lorcana, or One Piece card. Each card type has different naming conventions and we account for that.

This approach means even niche cards — vintage pulls, obscure parallels, foreign language prints — still get pricing data when it's available.

## Filtering Out the Noise

Raw marketplace search results always include junk: magazines, team lots, repack boxes, and other non-card items that skew pricing. We automatically filter results to remove items that clearly aren't individual cards, keeping only listings that match your specific card.

The result is cleaner, more reliable pricing.

## What You See: Four Key Price Points

For every card, we show you four numbers:

- **Lowest** — The cheapest active listing. Good for knowing the floor.
- **Median** — The middle value across all listings. This is our **primary pricing metric** because it's resistant to outliers (one seller listing a $5 card for $500 doesn't throw it off).
- **Average** — The mean across listings. Useful as a secondary reference.
- **Highest** — The most expensive active listing. Shows the ceiling.

We also show you how many active listings we found. More listings = higher confidence in the pricing. You'll see a confidence indicator right on the card — "High" confidence means 10+ listings were found.

## Pricing Stays Fresh

Card prices change constantly. A card that's $20 today might be $40 next week after a big game or a viral TikTok. Here's how we keep things current:

- **Instant on grade** — The moment your card is graded, we kick off a price lookup in the background. By the time you view your results, pricing is usually already there.
- **Auto-refresh** — Cached prices automatically refresh after 7 days. When you load your collection, stale prices update in the background without you doing anything.
- **Manual refresh** — Hit the refresh button on any card to pull completely fresh live data with individual listings and a price distribution chart.
- **Weekly snapshots** — We record price snapshots weekly so you can track trends over time with our price history charts.

## Raw vs. Graded: Understanding the Difference

The prices we show are based on **active marketplace listings for raw (ungraded) cards** matching yours. This gives you a baseline for what the card is worth in its current form.

But grading adds value. A raw card worth $20 might sell for $50-$100+ in a PSA 10 slab. That's where our **professional grade estimates** come in.

Every DCM grade includes estimated equivalent grades from the major grading companies — PSA, BGS, SGC, and CGC. These estimates factor in your card's overall grade, centering measurements, and specific condition details like corner sharpness and surface quality. Each company weighs these factors differently (SGC is strictest on centering, CGC is more lenient), and our estimates reflect that.

By combining your live market pricing with professional grade estimates, you can get a solid sense of what your card might be worth both raw and if you decide to submit it for professional grading.
