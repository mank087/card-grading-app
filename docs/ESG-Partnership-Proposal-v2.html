White-Label Grading Partnership Proposal

Dynamic Collectibles Management (DCM) & Evolution Sports Grading (ESG)

Prepared January 30, 2026  |  Confidential

1. Executive Summary

DCM Grading proposes a white-label partnership with Evolution Sports Grading (ESG) to provide AI-powered card grading services that operate under ESG's brand. Collectors will send physical trading cards to ESG's warehouse, where ESG staff will photograph and submit cards for grading using DCM's existing mobile and desktop application. The resulting grades and condition data will be displayed on ESG-branded verification pages accessible via QR codes on ESG's custom slabs.

This partnership allows ESG to offer professional, AI-powered grading services to their customers without building grading technology from scratch, while maintaining full brand ownership of the collector-facing experience. ESG staff will use DCM's proven upload and grading interface—the same tool used to grade thousands of cards today—with a shared team credit pool so all ESG staff draw from a single balance.

2. How It Works

End-to-End Workflow

Collector mails card to ESG warehouse
↓
ESG staff photographs front & back using DCM's app (phone or desktop)
↓
Card submitted for AI grading (credits drawn from ESG's shared team pool)
↓
DCM returns grade, subgrades & condition data
↓
ESG prints custom label with grade & QR code
↓
ESG slabs the card in their branded case
↓
Card shipped back to collector
↓
Collector or buyer scans QR code
↓
Lands on ESG-branded page displaying grade details

What the Customer Sees

When a collector (or potential buyer) scans the QR code on an ESG slab, they are taken to a clean, ESG-branded verification page that displays:

  • Card image (front and back)
  • Overall grade (1–10 scale)
  • Subgrade breakdown (Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface)
  • Condition summary and notable observations
  • Card details (name, set, year, card number)
  • Unique serial number for authentication

The page will not include market pricing, eBay listings, or downloadable reports—keeping the experience focused on grade verification.

3. Technical Approach

Since all development and technical implementation will be handled by DCM's engineering team, we recommend a fully managed, turnkey solution. DCM will build, host, and maintain every component of the platform on ESG's behalf—ESG does not need any technical staff or web development resources.

DCM builds and hosts the entire ESG grading experience end-to-end:

Why This Approach

Fully managed = zero technical burden on ESG. DCM handles all software development, hosting, security, and maintenance. ESG's only technical requirement is configuring a single DNS record (with step-by-step guidance from DCM). This allows ESG to focus entirely on what they do best: receiving cards, slabbing, and building relationships with collectors.

Key advantages of the managed approach:

  • No website development needed — ESG does not need to build, host, or maintain any web pages
  • No technical staff required — DCM provides all engineering and ongoing support
  • Works with any hosting provider — compatible with GoDaddy, Squarespace, or any domain registrar
  • QR codes never break — DCM controls uptime and guarantees page availability
  • Always up to date — ESG automatically benefits from platform improvements and new features
  • Scalable — handles 10 or 10,000 cards per month with no changes needed

  • Verification page availability: QR code landing pages will be available 24/7. DCM monitors page health and responds to outages proactively.
  • Scheduled maintenance: Planned downtime will be communicated to ESG at least 48 hours in advance. Maintenance will be scheduled during off-peak hours (12:00 AM–6:00 AM EST) whenever possible.

9. Data Ownership & Retention

Ownership

  • ESG-branded grading data: ESG owns all collector-facing grading data generated under ESG branding, including card images submitted by ESG staff, grades, subgrades, condition notes, and serial numbers.
  • DCM platform data: DCM retains ownership of anonymized and aggregated grading data (e.g., grade distributions, model performance metrics) for the purpose of AI model improvement, internal analytics, and platform benchmarking. No ESG-identifiable or collector-identifiable data will be shared externally.

Data Retention

  • Card images, grading data, and verification page content will be retained for the duration of the partnership and for a minimum of 12 months following termination.
  • Credit transaction records and audit logs will be retained for a minimum of 24 months following termination for billing reconciliation purposes.

Data Export & Portability

  • ESG may request a full export of all grading data at any time during the partnership via the partner dashboard (CSV/Excel format) or by written request to DCM.
  • Upon termination, DCM will provide ESG with a complete data export within 30 days of the termination effective date, including: card images, grades, subgrades, condition notes, serial numbers, card metadata, and staff attribution.
  • Export format: CSV and/or JSON data files, plus a ZIP archive of card images.

Verification Pages After Termination

  • Upon termination, ESG-branded verification pages will remain active for a 90-day wind-down period to ensure QR codes on slabs already in circulation continue to function.
  • After the wind-down period, pages will display a neutral message indicating the verification service is no longer active, rather than returning an error.
  • ESG may negotiate an extended hosting arrangement for verification pages beyond the wind-down period at a reduced rate.

10. Intellectual Property & Model Protection

  • License to use output: ESG receives a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable right to use DCM's grading output (grades, subgrades, condition notes) under ESG's brand for the duration of the partnership.

  • ESG brand ownership: ESG retains full ownership of its brand identity, trademarks, logos, and slab designs. DCM's use of ESG branding is limited to rendering verification pages as specified in this agreement.

Grading Nature Disclaimer

All grades produced by DCM's AI system are professional opinions generated by automated evaluation of submitted card images. Grades are not guarantees of card value, condition, or authenticity. Grading results may vary based on image quality, lighting conditions, and photographic angle. Both parties acknowledge that no grading system—human or automated—produces identical results 100% of the time.

Regrades

  • ESG may request a regrade of any card by resubmitting new photographs. Regrades consume one credit from ESG's shared pool.
  • If a grading error is attributed to a DCM system malfunction (not image quality), DCM will regrade the card at no cost and credit ESG's pool accordingly.

Dispute Escalation

  1. ESG internal review: ESG reviews the submitted images and grade to determine if the dispute is related to image quality or card handling.
  2. DCM technical review: If ESG believes the grade is incorrect despite adequate image quality, ESG submits a dispute to DCM with the card serial number and supporting details.
  3. Resolution: DCM reviews the grading data and responds within 5 business days with a determination (grade stands, regrade issued, or credit refunded).

Liability Limitations

  • DCM shall not be liable for any physical damage to cards, shipping losses, or slab defects.
  • DCM's total liability for grading-related claims shall not exceed the total fees paid by ESG in the 3 months preceding the claim.
  • Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages arising from this partnership.

12. Fraud & Abuse Safeguards

To protect the integrity of both the ESG and DCM brands, the following safeguards will be implemented:

  • Duplicate image detection: DCM's system compares submitted images using perceptual hashing to flag potential duplicate submissions of the same card for multiple grades.
  • Serial number uniqueness: Each graded card receives a unique serial number within the ESG namespace. The system enforces uniqueness and prevents duplication.
  • Staff audit logs: All grading activity is logged with staff account attribution, including timestamps, card details, and credit usage. Logs are accessible to ESG's admin account.
  • Suspicious activity monitoring: DCM reserves the right to flag, pause, or investigate grading activity that exhibits unusual patterns (e.g., rapid-fire identical submissions, systematic image manipulation, abnormal volume spikes).
  • Account suspension: DCM may temporarily suspend an individual ESG staff account pending investigation if fraud is suspected. DCM will notify ESG's admin immediately and work collaboratively to resolve the issue.

13. Scalability & Performance

Grading Turnaround

  • High-volume periods: During peak submission periods, processing times may extend but will not exceed 5 minutes per card under normal circumstances.

Grading Methodology

DCM uses a proprietary three-pass AI consensus system to ensure grading accuracy and consistency:

  • Pass 1 & Pass 2: Two independent AI evaluations analyze the card's centering, corners, edges, and surface condition
  • Pass 3 (Consensus): A third evaluation reviews both prior assessments, resolves discrepancies, and produces the final grade
  • Grade Scale: Whole number grades from 1 to 10
  • Subgrades: Individual scores for Centering, Corners, Edges, and Surface
  • Condition Notes: Detailed observations on any defects or notable characteristics

This system eliminates single-evaluator bias and delivers consistent, repeatable results across all card types.

Card Identification & Database Enrichment

Beyond grading, DCM automatically identifies and enriches each card using internally maintained TCG databases:

  • Proprietary card databases — DCM maintains local databases for Pokemon (English & Japanese), Magic: The Gathering, Disney Lorcana, and One Piece TCG, sourced from official TCG registries and continuously updated
  • Automated card matching — After AI grading, the system cross-references the card's name, set, and number against the internal database to pull verified details: full card name, set name, release year, card number, rarity, and variant type
  • Image-to-data pipeline — The AI reads text directly from the card image (OCR) to extract the card number, set symbol, and identifying marks, then matches against the database for accurate identification
  • Sports card identification — Sports cards are identified using AI vision to extract player name, team, card manufacturer, set, year, and card number directly from the card face and back
  • Variant & parallel detection — The system distinguishes between card variants (holo, reverse holo, full art, alternate art, foil, parallel, manga art, enchanted, etc.) to ensure the correct version is identified and recorded
