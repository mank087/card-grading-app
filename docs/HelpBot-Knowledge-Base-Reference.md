## 1. Getting Started (4 questions)

**Q: What is DCM Grading?**
A: DCM Grading is an AI-powered card grading service that uses DCM Optic™ technology to evaluate your trading cards in under 60 seconds. Upload front and back photos, and our three-pass consensus system delivers a professional-quality grade instantly.

**Q: How does DCM Grading work?**
A: Upload front and back photos of your card. Our system runs three independent evaluations using DCM Optic™, then combines them using consensus rules. You get a final grade (1–10), eight component scores, a consistency rating, and detailed defect notes—all in about 60 seconds.

**Q: What types of cards can DCM grade?**
A: DCM supports Sports cards (baseball, basketball, football, hockey, soccer), Pokémon, Magic: The Gathering, Disney Lorcana, One Piece TCG, and other trading cards. Select your card type when uploading for the most accurate evaluation.

**Q: How long does grading take?**
A: Most cards are graded in 30–60 seconds. Cards with complex surfaces or multiple defects may take slightly longer. You'll see a progress indicator while grading is in process.

## 2. Grading & Scores (8 questions)

**Q: What grading scale does DCM use?**
A: DCM uses a 10-point scale: 10 = Gem Mint, 9 = Mint, 8 = Near Mint-Mint, 7 = Near Mint, 6 = Excellent-Mint, 5 = Excellent, and lower grades for increasing wear. Grades are whole numbers from 1–10.

**Q: What are component scores?**
A: Each card receives 8 component scores: Centering, Corners, Edges, and Surface for both front and back. The front is weighted 55% and the back 45%. These combine into your final overall grade.

**Q: What is three-pass consensus grading?**
A: Three independent AI evaluations analyze your card separately. A defect must be confirmed by at least 2 of 3 passes to count. This eliminates false positives, reduces variance, and produces more reliable grades than a single evaluation.

**Q: What does the consistency score mean?**
A: The consistency score shows how closely the three passes agreed. High consistency (±0.5) means strong agreement. Moderate (±1.0) means minor differences. Low (>1.0) means notable disagreement—consider re-grading with better photos.

**Q: What centering standards does DCM use?**
A: DCM follows industry-standard centering measurements. For Gem Mint (10), the front must be within 55/45 and the back within 75/25. More lenient centering thresholds apply for lower grades.

**Q: What are grade caps?**
A: Certain structural defects automatically cap your grade regardless of other scores. Creases, corner lifts, tears, and severe bends each have maximum grade thresholds that cannot be exceeded.

**Q: What does "evidence-based grading" mean?**
A: Every defect DCM reports must be backed by observable evidence in your photos. Each finding includes the defect type, location, severity, and confidence level. No guessing—if we can't see it, we don't deduct for it.

**Q: What if a card scores higher or lower than I expect it to?**
A: The DCM system is dependent on the images uploaded to be clear of visual issues like blur and glare to ensure the highest accuracy possible. If a grade is very far off from what is expected, try to re-grade that card with better lighting and a neutral contrasting background. Also, be sure to properly enter any defects or issues with the card that the photos may not see like warping, scratches, angular defects, etc.

## 3. Photo Tips (6 questions)

**Q: How should I photograph my cards?**
A: Use natural, diffused lighting (no flash). Place the card flat on a contrasting background. Fill the frame with the card. Ensure sharp focus and avoid shadows. Remove from holders if possible. Photograph both front and back.

**Q: What is the image confidence rating?**
A: Image confidence (A–D) indicates how well DCM could see your card. A (95–100%) means excellent visibility. B (85–94%) is good. C (70–84%) means partial obstruction. D (<70%) means significant issues—consider re-photographing.

**Q: Can I grade cards in sleeves or top loaders?**
A: Yes! Penny sleeves work well. Top loaders are acceptable but may cause minor reflections. Thick magnetic holders can reduce image clarity. For best results, remove the card from its holder before photographing.

**Q: Can DCM grade cards already in graded slabs?**
A: Yes, DCM can grade slabbed cards from any mail-away grading company. We detect the slab and grade the card independently. Note that thick slab plastic may reduce image clarity, which could affect the image confidence rating.

**Q: What image formats are supported?**
A: DCM accepts JPEG, PNG, HEIC, and WebP formats. For best results, use images at least 1000×1400 pixels. Phone cameras work great—just make sure the photo is in focus and well-lit.

**Q: Can I upload from my phone?**
A: Yes! DCM is fully mobile-friendly. You can take photos directly from your phone camera and upload them right away. The mobile experience is optimized for easy grading on the go.

## 5. DCM vs Mail-Away Grading (4 questions)

**Q: How does DCM compare to mail-away grading companies?**
A: DCM provides instant AI-powered grades aligned with professional industry standards, but at a fraction of the cost and time. Use DCM for pre-screening before submitting to a mail-away grading company, or as an independent assessment for personal collecting and trading.

**Q: How accurate are DCM grades?**
A: DCM uses three-pass consensus, evidence-based grading, and industry-aligned standards to maximize accuracy. While no photo-based system is identical to physical inspection, DCM provides a reliable assessment that aligns closely with mail-away graders.

**Q: What if my DCM grade differs from a professional grade?**
A: Differences can arise from image limitations (photo vs. physical inspection), natural subjectivity in grading, different standard interpretations, and grading shifts over time. Consider re-grading with better photos, or use DCM as a complement to mail-away grading services.

**Q: Why should I use DCM instead of traditional grading?**
A: Speed (60 seconds vs. weeks), cost (as low as $0.50 per card vs. $20–$150+), convenience (grade from home), transparency (see exactly what was found), and unlimited re-grades. DCM is ideal for pre-screening, personal collections, and quick assessments.

## 6. Special Cases (5 questions)

**Q: Can DCM grade autographed cards?**
A: Yes! Manufacturer-authenticated autographs (e.g., Topps Certified) are graded normally. For cards with unverified or hand-signed autographs, DCM grades the card condition but assigns N/A for authentication.

**Q: What happens if my card has been altered?**
A: Cards with alterations (trimming, recoloring, rebacking, re-cornering) receive an N/A designation. DCM detects common forms of alteration and will flag them in the grading report rather than assigning a numeric grade.

**Q: Does DCM grade vintage cards?**
A: Yes! DCM accounts for era-appropriate standards and vintage manufacturing variations. Older cards are evaluated with context for typical centering, print quality, and wear patterns of their era.

**Q: Can DCM determine Fake Cards?**
A: DCM looks for manufacturer authentication, copyrights, and related authenticity markings. Counterfeit or fake cards can be hard to spot and DCM may not always accurately determine the authenticity of a card. Check with your local card store or convention for true authenticity analysis.

## 8. Printing Labels (5 questions)

**Q: How do I print labels for my graded cards?**
A: Go to any card's detail page and click the download button to access label options. You can download a Foldable Slab Label (for magnetic one-touch holders), a Full Grading Report (PDF), a Mini Report (JPG), or card images with grade overlays. For bulk printing, visit your Collection page to print up to 18 labels per sheet.

**Q: What labels should I use for magnetic one-touch slabs?**
A: Use Avery 6871 labels (2-3/8" × 1-1/4", 18 per sheet). Print the Foldable Slab Label from your card's detail page. The label is designed to fold over the top edge of a magnetic one-touch holder. Peel the label, align it with the top edge of the slab, and fold it over so the front shows the grade and the back has a QR code for verification.

**Q: What labels should I use for top loaders?**
A: Use Avery 8167 template labels for top loaders. These print out front and back labels that can be applied to a top loader. The labels have a card identifier, grade, and QR code that links to the full card details page.

**Q: Can I print labels for multiple cards at once?**
A: Yes! Go to your Collection page and use the batch label printing feature. Select up to 18 cards, arrange them on a 3×6 grid with drag-and-drop, and download a single PDF sheet. If you have more than 18 cards, the PDF automatically paginates across multiple pages. Use Avery 6871 label sheets for best results.

**Q: How do I apply a label to my slab or top loader?**
A: For magnetic one-touch slabs: Print on Avery 6871, peel the label, center it along the top edge of the holder, and fold it over—the front displays the grade and card info, the back has the QR code. For top loaders: Print the Avery 8167 front and back labels and apply them to the top loader. Make sure the labels are straight and fully adhered before displaying.
