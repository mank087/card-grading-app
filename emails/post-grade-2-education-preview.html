Sub-Grade Breakdown

What each score actually measures

Every grade is built from four independent dimensions. Here’s how yours scored and what we look at for each one.

Centering

How balanced the borders are, front and back

{{centering_score}}

DCM Optic™ measures the ratio of border width on all four sides. Even a small shift (55/45 or worse) can knock points off.

Corners

Sharpness of all eight corners

{{corners_score}}

Each corner is examined at high zoom across three passes. Whitening, fraying, blunting, or chipping on a single corner can cap this score.

Edges

Clean lines along every edge

{{edges_score}}

We trace the full perimeter on front and back. Roughness, nicks, factory edge wear, and color loss along the cut all show up here.

Surface

Print quality, scratches, scuffs, dings

{{surface_score}}

The biggest sub-grade by detection area. Print lines, roller marks, scratches, scuffs, dimples, and holo wear all show up here.

Why You Can Trust This Grade

Three independent passes. One unbiased result.

DCM Optic™ doesn’t grade your card once. It grades it three times, independently, in a single analysis run. The three scores are reconciled, outliers get flagged, and the consensus becomes your final number.

That’s why two photos of the same card produce the same grade. No grader having a bad day, no human bias, just consistent analysis built for collectors.
