**DEFECT TYPE DEFINITIONS:**

**1. CORNER WHITENING**
- What it is: White or light-colored exposed card stock at corner tips
- Visual cues: Missing ink reveals lighter paper underneath
- How to detect: Color deviation from expected border/edge color
- Severity examples:
  * Microscopic (<0.1mm): Barely visible white tip, requires close inspection
  * Minor (0.1-0.3mm): Small white area visible upon close look
  * Moderate (0.3-1mm): Clearly visible white corner, easily seen
  * Heavy (>1mm): Obvious exposed stock, significant whitening

**2. EDGE CHIPPING / ROUGHNESS**
- What it is: Small nicks, chips, or rough texture along card edges
- Visual cues: Uneven edge line, exposed lighter base stock, white dots
- How to detect: Compare edge color against adjacent printed areas
- Severity examples:
  * Microscopic (<0.1mm): Individual micro-chips or dots
  * Minor (0.1-0.3mm): Small chips or 1-2 white dots visible
  * Moderate (0.3-1mm): Visible chipping or multiple dots
  * Heavy (>1mm): Extensive material loss, heavily damaged edge

**3. SURFACE SCRATCHES**
- What it is: Linear marks on card surface from contact/friction
- Visual cues: Light-reflecting lines, breaks in glossy finish
- How to detect: Look for lines under reflected light
- Types:
  * Hairline Scratch: <0.2mm wide, <5mm long, visible only in reflected light
  * Visible Scratch: â‰¥0.2mm wide or â‰¥5mm long, visible in normal lighting
- Severity examples:
  * Minor: Single hairline scratch, <5mm long
  * Moderate: Visible scratch 5-10mm long, or 2-3 hairline scratches
  * Heavy: Multiple scratches, >10mm long, or deep scratches

ARTIFACT DETECTION: REFLECTIONS VS. ACTUAL DEFECTS
======================================================================
**CRITICAL:** Distinguish between actual card defects and photographic/environmental artifacts.

**COMMON FALSE POSITIVES TO AVOID:**

**1. SLEEVE/CASE EDGE REFLECTIONS**
- What it looks like: Bright white line or area at corners/edges, often symmetrical
- How to detect:
  * If ALL 4 corners show identical "whitening" â†’ LIKELY sleeve reflection, NOT card defect
  * True handling wear affects 1-2 corners asymmetrically (bottom corners most common)
  * Sleeve reflections appear as uniform brightness across corners
- Action: If symmetric pattern detected, re-examine corners for actual fiber exposure vs. light reflection

**2. GLOSS DIFFERENTIAL REFLECTIONS**
- What it looks like: Bright spots or areas on glossy/foil cards
- Common on: Prizm, Contenders, Chrome, Optic (matte-gloss contrast cards)
- How to detect:
  * Brightness appears consistent with card's glossy coating
  * Appears in natural light reflection positions
  * No visible fiber exposure or material loss
- Action: Check for actual exposed stock underneath. If no lighter paper visible, it's reflection.

**3. LIGHTING GLARE VS. WHITENING**
- Whitening (defect): Exposed lighter card stock, fixed position, shows fiber texture
- Glare (artifact): Smooth bright area, no texture, would change position if card tilted
- Key differentiator: Whitening shows COLOR CHANGE (white/cream exposed stock vs. printed color)
  * Glare shows BRIGHTNESS CHANGE (same color, just brighter)

**VALIDATION RULE: SYMMETRIC DEFECT CHECK**
If you detect identical defects on all 4 corners or all 4 edges:
1. ğŸš¨ STOP - This pattern is highly suspicious (real wear is asymmetric)
2. Re-examine each area for sleeve reflection, case edge, or glare
3. Only confirm as defects if you see clear fiber exposure or material loss
4. When in doubt: Describe what you see but note "may be photographic artifact"

**CRITICAL REMINDER FOR GEM MINT CARDS (9.5-10.0):**
- Microscopic marks (<0.1mm) on multiple corners can STILL be 9.5
- 3-4 corners with microscopic whitening (<0.1mm each) = STILL 9.5 if total <0.2mm
- Only downgrade to 9.0 if individual defect >0.1mm OR total exceeds 0.2mm
