Autograph Verification
üö® CRITICAL: Distinguish between manufacturer-certified autographs and unverified signatures.

‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT: An **autograph** is a SIGNATURE (cursive name or initials). Do NOT confuse with:
- Numbers (price markings like "$5", "100", "9.5")
- Inventory codes (alphanumeric like "A23", "SKU-401")
- Checkmarks, symbols, or abbreviations
‚Üí These are **Handwritten Markings**, not autographs (assess in next section)

WHAT IS AN AUTOGRAPH:
- **Cursive signature** of player/celebrity name
- **Stylized initials** in signature form
- **Personal inscription** with signature (e.g., "Best wishes, John Smith")
- Must resemble a SIGNATURE, not just random writing

MANUFACTURER AUTHENTICATION INDICATORS (any of these = VERIFIED):
- **Holographic sticker** with company logo (Panini, Topps, Upper Deck, etc.)
- **"Certified Autograph"** or "Authenticated Autograph" printed text on card
- **Serial number linked to autograph** (e.g., "/25 Auto" indicates limited autograph run)
- **COA (Certificate of Authenticity)** reference number printed on card
- **Autograph card designation** in set checklist (card specifically marketed as auto)
- **On-card auto with authentication hologram** visible

UNVERIFIED AUTOGRAPH INDICATORS (FLAG these as N/A):
- **Signature present** (cursive name or initials) but **no manufacturer authentication**
- Signature appears to be **added after production** (ink over protective coating)
- **TTM (Through The Mail)** or **IP (In Person)** autograph without manufacturer involvement
- Signature in unusual location (over stats, in margins, on border)
- **If uncertain whether marking is signature** ‚Üí Flag as "Unverified Autograph" (safer interpretation)

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
- If manufacturer-authenticated ‚Üí State: "Autograph: Verified - [describe authentication method]"
- Example: "Autograph: Verified - Panini hologram and 'Certified Autograph' text visible"
- If unverified autograph ‚Üí Flag: "Unverified Autograph - [describe signature location and why unverified]"

- If no autograph ‚Üí State: "No autograph present"
- **If only numbers/codes/symbols** ‚Üí State: "No autograph present" (assess in Handwritten Markings section)

Handwritten Markings & Non-Manufacturer Alterations
üö® CRITICAL: Carefully inspect BOTH sides for any non-manufacturer markings before beginning condition evaluation.

MANDATORY INSPECTION AREAS (check all areas systematically):
**Card Back (most common location):**
- Borders and white space (top, bottom, left, right margins)
- Around player statistics and bio information

- Card number area

**Card Front (less common but check thoroughly):**
- White borders around image
- Any blank space in design
- Corners and edges

HANDWRITTEN MARKING TYPES TO DETECT:
1. **Price Markings**: Numbers (often with $ or decimal), common values: "$5", "2.50", "100"
2. **Grading Notes**: Letters/numbers like "NM", "8", "9.5", "PSA 10"
3. **Inventory Codes**: Alphanumeric codes, often in corners
4. **Owner Initials**: Letters, initials, names, dates
5. **Checkmarks or Symbols**: ‚úì, X, circles, stars
6. **Any pen/pencil/marker writing** in any color

VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS OF HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS:
- **Ink appearance**: Pen ink (blue, black, red most common), pencil graphite
- **Line quality**: Hand-drawn lines vs. printed (uneven, variable thickness, human imperfection)
- **Font inconsistency**: Handwriting style vs. uniform printed text
- **Color mismatch**: Ink color different from manufacturer printing
- **Texture difference**: Ink sitting on top of surface vs. printed into cardstock
- **Location oddness**: Writing in margins, blank spaces, or over design elements where manufacturer would never print

MANUFACTURER PRINTING (DO NOT FLAG THESE):
- Printed card numbers, player stats, copyright text
- Pre-printed serial numbers in foil or ink
- Manufacturer logos, set names, legal text
- Bar codes, QR codes
- Any text with uniform font and professional printing quality
