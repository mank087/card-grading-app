**ORIENTATION DETECTION (Do this first):**

Determine if the card is portrait, landscape, or square:

- **PORTRAIT** (most common): Card is taller than wide (height > width)
  - Standard sports cards, most TCG cards
  - Vertical layout with player/character framed vertically

- **LANDSCAPE**: Card is wider than tall (width > height)
  - Horizontal sports action shots, some TCG cards
  - Wider viewing area, horizontal framing

- **SQUARE**: Nearly equal dimensions (within 10% difference)
  - Some vintage cards, promotional cards
  - Treat as portrait for analysis purposes

Set `card_orientation.detected_orientation` to: "portrait" | "landscape" | "square"

â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•

**CORNER DEFECTS:**

Whitening â†’ Visible white or lighter color at corner where ink/color is missing
- Look for: White tips, exposed card stock, color lighter than surrounding area
- Common on: Dark borders and edges where damage shows clearly

Rounding â†’ Corner has lost its sharp 90-degree angle, appears curved
- Look for: Curved corner shape instead of sharp point
- Usually accompanied by whitening

**EDGE DEFECTS:**

Chipping â†’ Small nicks or missing pieces along the edge
- Look for: White dots, rough spots, irregularities in the edge line
- Often shows as: Multiple small white spots along edge

Roughness â†’ Edge texture that looks uneven or fuzzy
- Look for: Edge that doesn't look clean-cut, appears textured or frayed
- Compare: Rough edge vs smooth clean-cut edge

**SURFACE DEFECTS:**

Scratches â†’ Lines or marks on the card surface
- Look for: Light-catching lines, streaks, or marks in the finish
- Severity: How visible and how long/wide

Creases â†’ Fold lines or bends in the card
- Look for: Lines that break the surface, visible folds, raised areas
- **CRITICAL**: ANY crease significantly impacts grade (usually caps grade at 4.0 or below)
- Check both sides: Creases often more visible from one side

Print Defects â†’ Factory printing issues
- Look for: Print dots, misregistration, color shifts, foil texture issues
- Note: These are original to the card, not damage

Stains/Discoloration â†’ Spots, marks, or color changes
- Look for: Darker or lighter areas, spots, yellowing, moisture marks

**HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS (CRITICAL - AUTOMATIC N/A GRADE):**

âš ï¸ **CRITICAL CHECK - PERFORM THIS FIRST** âš ï¸

ALWAYS check BOTH sides (especially the back) for ANY handwritten markings:
- Pen, pencil, or marker writing NOT part of original card
- Examples: Numbers ("100", "23"), prices ("$5"), initials ("JB"), checkmarks
- Common locations: Back corners, margins, blank areas

**HOW TO IDENTIFY:**
- Ink quality differs from card printing (ballpoint, marker, pencil texture)
- Writing style doesn't match card's printed text
- Often slightly raised, shiny, or different color than print
- NOT aligned with card design elements

**IF YOU SEE HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS:**
- This is an ALTERATION
- Grade MUST be: N/A (null - NOT GRADABLE)
- Document in defects.back.surface.other (or front if on front)
- Set severity: "heavy"
- Description must clearly state: "Handwritten [content] in [color] ink on [location] - NOT part of original card"

**DO NOT CONFUSE WITH:**
- Printed serial numbers (e.g., "/100" stamped in foil) = Part of card (OK)
- Handwritten "100" in pen = Alteration (N/A grade)
