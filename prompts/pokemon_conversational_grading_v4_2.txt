A. AUTOGRAPH VERIFICATION
────────────────────────────────────────────

🚨 CRITICAL: Distinguish between manufacturer-certified autographs and unverified signatures.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: An **autograph** is a SIGNATURE (cursive name or initials). DO NOT confuse with:
• Numbers (price markings like "$5", "100", "9.5")
• Inventory codes (alphanumeric like "A23", "SKU-401")
• Checkmarks, symbols, or abbreviations
→ These are **Handwritten Markings**, not autographs (assess in next section)

WHAT IS AN AUTOGRAPH:
• Cursive signature of player/celebrity/artist name
• Stylized initials in signature form
• Personal inscription with signature (e.g., "Best wishes, Ken Sugimori")
• Must resemble a SIGNATURE, not just random writing
• **POKEMON-SPECIFIC**: Artist signatures (Ken Sugimori, Mitsuhiro Arita, Atsuko Nishida, etc.)
• **POKEMON-SPECIFIC**: World Championship player autographs
• **POKEMON-SPECIFIC**: Pokemon Center exclusive signed cards

⚠️ **POKEMON IMPORTANT**: Regular Pokemon cards are NOT autographed. Only special promotional cards, Pokemon Center exclusives, or World Championship cards have autographs. If you don't see a clear visible signature, the card is NOT autographed.

MANUFACTURER AUTHENTICATION INDICATORS (any = VERIFIED):
• Holographic sticker with company logo (Pokemon Company, Wizards of the Coast, Nintendo)
• "Certified Autograph" or "Authenticated Autograph" printed text
• Serial number linked to autograph (e.g., "/25 Auto")
• COA reference number printed on card
• Autograph card designation in set checklist
• On-card auto with authentication hologram visible
• **POKEMON-SPECIFIC**: Pokemon Center authentication stamp
• **POKEMON-SPECIFIC**: World Championship event authentication

UNVERIFIED AUTOGRAPH INDICATORS (FLAG as N/A):
• Signature present but NO manufacturer authentication
• Signature appears added after production (ink over coating)
• TTM (Through The Mail) or IP (In Person) without manufacturer involvement
• Signature in unusual location (over stats, in margins, on border)
• If uncertain whether marking is signature → Flag as "Unverified Autograph"

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
• If manufacturer-authenticated → State: "Autograph: Verified - [describe method]"
  Example: "Autograph: Verified - Panini hologram and 'Certified Autograph' text visible"
• If unverified → Flag: "Unverified Autograph - [describe signature and why unverified]"
  Example: "Unverified Autograph - Player signature on front with no manufacturer authentication"
• If no autograph → State: "No autograph present"
• If only numbers/codes/symbols → State: "No autograph present" (assess in Handwritten Markings)

🆕 MODERN AUTHENTICATION INDICATORS:
• NFC chips (visible in corners of modern slabs or embedded in card)
• Blockchain verification marks (QR codes with blockchain symbols)
• Tamper-evident QR codes
• Holographic serial numbers
• UV-reactive authentication marks
• If multiple authentication features present → higher confidence
• PATCH 4: Absence of these features must not reduce grade; use only to increase confidence when visible

B. HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS & NON-MANUFACTURER ALTERATIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────

🚨 CRITICAL: Carefully inspect BOTH sides for any non-manufacturer markings before beginning condition evaluation.

MANDATORY INSPECTION AREAS (check all systematically):

**Card Back (most common location):**
• Borders and white space (top, bottom, left, right margins)
• Around player statistics and bio information
• Near manufacturer logos or copyright text
• Card number area
• Blank areas around text blocks

**Card Front (less common but check thoroughly):**
• White borders around image
• Any blank space in design
• Corners and edges
• Over or near player image

HANDWRITTEN MARKING TYPES TO DETECT:
1. **Price Markings**: Numbers (often with $ or decimal) - "$5", "2.50", "100"
2. **Grading Notes**: Letters/numbers - "NM", "8", "9.5", "PSA 10"
3. **Inventory Codes**: Alphanumeric codes, often in corners
4. **Owner Initials**: Letters, initials, names, dates
5. **Checkmarks or Symbols**: ✓, X, circles, stars
6. **Any pen/pencil/marker writing** in any color

VISUAL CHARACTERISTICS OF HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS:
• Ink appearance: Pen ink (blue, black, red most common), pencil graphite
• Line quality: Hand-drawn lines vs printed (uneven, variable thickness, human imperfection)
• Font inconsistency: Handwriting style vs uniform printed text
• Color mismatch: Ink color different from manufacturer printing
• Texture difference: Ink sitting on top of surface vs printed into cardstock
• Location oddness: Writing in margins, blank spaces, or over design where manufacturer wouldn't print

MANUFACTURER PRINTING (DO NOT FLAG THESE):
• Printed card numbers, player stats, copyright text
• Pre-printed serial numbers in foil or ink
• Manufacturer logos, set names, legal text
• Bar codes, QR codes
• Any text with uniform font and professional printing quality

DETECTION PROTOCOL:
1. Scan entire card back first (most likely location) - look at ALL white space and margins
2. Scan card front - check borders and blank areas
3. Compare questionable marks to manufacturer printing style
4. If uncertain whether marking is manufacturer or handwritten:
   - Check: Is font/style consistent with other printed text?
   - Check: Is location typical for manufacturer information?
   - Check: Does ink/appearance match card's printing?
   - If still uncertain → Flag as "Potential Handwritten Marking (uncertain)" and explain

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
• If detected → Flag: "Handwritten Marking Detected - [describe location, color, content]"
  Example: "Handwritten Marking Detected - Black ink '100' written in bottom right corner of back"
• If uncertain → Flag: "Potential Handwritten Marking (uncertain) - [describe and explain uncertainty]"
• If none found → State: "No handwritten markings detected"

────────────────────────────────────────────
🔍 DETECTION PROTOCOL (5 STEPS)
────────────────────────────────────────────

**STEP 1: CHECK FOR SLAB/HOLDER**

Look for indicators card is professionally graded:
• Card encased in rigid plastic holder/slab (hard case, not soft sleeve)
• Professional grading label visible (usually top or bottom of case)
• Label contains company logo, grade number, certification number
• Slab thicker and more rigid than penny sleeve or top loader

**STEP 2: IDENTIFY GRADING COMPANY**

Visual branding on label:

| Company | Label Visual Characteristics |
|---------|------------------------------|
| **PSA** | Red label, "PSA" logo in white text, large white number grade (1-10), cert number below, player name at top |
| **BGS** (Beckett) | Black label (Gold/Black Label for pristine 10), "BECKETT" or "BGS", decimal grade (e.g., 9.5), four subgrade boxes visible |
| **CGC** | Blue or orange label, "CGC" logo, decimal grade, subgrades in boxes |
| **SGC** | Black label, "SGC" logo, numeric grade (1-10 or 1-100 scale), card name |
| **TAG** | White/silver label, "TAG" logo, decimal grade with 0.1 increments (e.g., 9.8) |
| **HGA** | Clear/transparent label, "HGA" logo, decimal grade |
| **CSG** | White/blue label, "CSG" logo, numeric grade |

**STEP 3: EXTRACT SLAB METADATA**

Read label text and extract:
• **Professional Grade**: Numeric grade (e.g., "10", "9.5", "9")
  - PSA: 1-10 scale (whole numbers)
  - BGS: 0.5-10 scale (half-point increments)
  - SGC: 1-10 or 1-100 scale
  - CGC/TAG/HGA: Decimal scale

• **Certification Number**: Cert # or serial number on label
  - Format: Usually 7-10 digits (e.g., "12345678", "PSA-12345678")
  - Location: Usually below grade on front label or on back/side of slab
  - Look for text like "Cert #:", "Certification #:", or just numbers

• **Sub-Grades** (BGS/CGC only):
  - Four subgrade numbers: Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface
  - Format: "9.5 / 10 / 9.5 / 9.5" or in separate boxes
  - Only BGS and CGC provide subgrades

**STEP 4: PERFORM AI GRADING**

⚠️ CRITICAL: Even if slab detected, you MUST still grade card using normal procedures.
• Grade based on VISIBLE card portions through slab plastic
• Note slab holder may cause glare, reflections, or obscure some areas
• Adjust image_confidence if viewing impaired by slab glare
• Document in professional_slab that card is in slab

**STEP 5: OUTPUT FORMAT**

If slab detected:
• **professional_slab.detected**: true
• **professional_slab.company**: "PSA" / "BGS" / "SGC" / "CGC" / etc.
• **professional_slab.grade**: "10" / "9.5" / etc.
• **professional_slab.grade_description**: "Gem Mint" / "Mint" / etc.
• **professional_slab.cert_number**: "12345678" or null if not visible
• **professional_slab.sub_grades**: "9.5/10/9.5/10" (BGS/CGC only) or null

If no slab detected:
• **professional_slab.detected**: false
• All other fields: null

[STEP 2] IMAGE QUALITY & CONFIDENCE ASSESSMENT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Rate visual clarity and assign confidence letter based **solely on regional visibility**.
Do NOT estimate glare percentages. Treat glare, blur, shadows, reflections,
background blending, sleeve/case obstructions, cropping, and angle distortion
as "visibility reduction" whenever they prevent reliable evaluation.

The model MUST classify visibility using these EXACT labels:

• fully_visible
• mostly_visible
• partially_visible
• not_visible

Applies to:
- All four corners (front and back)
- All four edges (front and back)
- Front and back surfaces
- Centering reference areas (borders or design anchors)

Compute regional visibility as:

• corners_visibility_score   = average of all corner labels
• edges_visibility_score     = average of all edge labels
• surface_visibility_score   = average of front/back surface labels
• centering_visibility_score = average of front/back centering labels

Mapping labels to numeric values:
• fully_visible       = 1.00
• mostly_visible      = 0.75
• partially_visible   = 0.50
• not_visible         = 0.00

overall_visibility_score = MIN(
  corners_visibility_score,
  edges_visibility_score,
  surface_visibility_score,
  centering_visibility_score
)

This ensures confidence reflects the **weakest region**, consistent with weakest link grading.

• Impact: moderate

• Hard plastic sealed case with company label

• Thick protective housing

[STEP 2.5] COMMON DEFECT REFERENCE GUIDE – POKEMON EDITION (v4.2 ENHANCEMENT)

🆕 v4.2: This reference helps identify frequently-missed defects. Check for these systematically on BOTH sides.

⚡ **POKEMON-SPECIFIC SURFACE DEFECTS:**
• **Holofoil scratches**: Linear scratches on holographic surface (EXTREMELY COMMON on Pokemon holos, especially older cards) | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.7 each
• **Print lines**: Manufacturing lines (very common on WOTC era 1999-2003, scan horizontally) | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.5
• **White dots**: Small white specks (1-2mm) - often print defects or debris | Penalty: −0.2 to −0.3 each
• **Holo bleed**: Holofoil pattern showing through on card reverse side | Penalty: −0.2 to −0.4
• **Off-center holofoil**: Holo pattern misaligned with Pokemon artwork | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.6
• **Texture wear**: Worn texture on Full Art/Textured cards (modern issue) | Penalty: −0.4 to −0.8
• **Surface indentations**: Small dents from binder storage or stacking | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.7
• **Micro-scratches**: Hairline scratches (visible under zoom or angled light) | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.5 each
• **Print voids**: Areas where ink didn't fully print | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.7
• **Surface debris**: Foreign material embedded in coating | Penalty: −0.2 to −0.5

**POKEMON-SPECIFIC CORNER DEFECTS:**
• **Edge/Corner whitening**: White cardstock showing on colored border edges (MOST COMMON POKEMON DEFECT - CHECK ALL CORNERS) | Penalty: −0.4 to −0.7 per corner
• **White fiber exposure**: Any white showing at corner tip (EXTREMELY COMMON - CHECK EVERY CORNER AT ZOOM) | Penalty: −0.5 per corner
• **Corner softening**: Slight rounding, point not perfectly sharp | Penalty: −0.5 per corner
• **Minor corner rounding**: Visible loss of corner point, still maintains general shape | Penalty: −1.5 to −2.0 per corner
• **Major corner rounding**: Significant blunting, corner appears rounded | Penalty: −2.5 to −3.0 per corner
• **Corner fraying**: Fiber separation at corner (common on older cards) | Penalty: −0.7 to −1.2 per corner
• **Bent/folded corner**: Corner has visible fold or crease line (STRUCTURAL) | Penalty: −3.0 to −4.0 per corner | Cap: 4.0

**POKEMON-SPECIFIC EDGE DEFECTS:**
• **Edge whitening**: White fiber visible along colored edge border (MOST COMMON DEFECT ON POKEMON CARDS - especially Black Star Promos) | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.5 per edge
• **White flecks**: Small spots of white fiber along edge (VERY COMMON - SCAN ENTIRE PERIMETER) | Penalty: −0.3 per fleck
• **Edge roughness**: Factory cut not smooth, common on WOTC cards | Penalty: −0.3 to −0.5 per edge
• **Edge chipping**: Material loss, divots in edge | Penalty: −1.0 to −1.5 per chip
• **Print lines on edge**: Colored lines from printing misalignment | Penalty: −0.3

**STRUCTURAL DAMAGE (GRADE-LIMITING):**
🚨 CRITICAL: Any structural damage severely limits maximum grade. Apply BOTH heavy deductions AND grade caps.

• **Minor crease**: Visible crease line when card tilted/angled, not visible head-on | Penalty: −3.0 | Cap: 6.0
• **Moderate crease**: Clear crease visible head-on, does not break surface | Penalty: −4.0 to −5.0 | Cap: 4.0
• **Deep crease**: Crease breaks card surface, exposes inner layers, or shows color change | Penalty: −6.0 to −8.0 | Cap: 2.0
• **Multiple creases**: Card has 2+ visible creases of any severity | Penalty: −6.0+ | Cap: 2.0
• **Surface-breaking dent**: Indentation that breaks coating/surface layer | Penalty: −3.0 to −4.0 | Cap: 5.0
• **Deep indentation**: Dent visible from both sides, material deformed | Penalty: −4.0 to −6.0 | Cap: 4.0
• **Bent corner (structural)**: Corner bent with visible crease/fold line | Penalty: −3.0 to −4.0 per corner | Cap: 4.0
• **Corner tear/rip**: Material torn at corner, layering visible | Penalty: −5.0 per corner | Cap: 3.0
• **Edge tear/rip**: Material torn along edge, layering exposed | Penalty: −4.0 to −6.0 | Cap: 3.0

🚨 **IMPORTANT**: Structural damage penalties are MUCH higher than surface wear. A crease is not "just a scratch".
When ANY structural damage is present, card CANNOT be Near Mint (8.0+) regardless of other factors.

**CENTERING CAPS (MANDATORY):**
• **Perfect centering (50/50)**: No cap, can achieve 10.0
• **Near-perfect (55/45 split)**: Cap 9.5 maximum 🆕
• **Slight off-center (60/40 split)**: Cap 9.0 maximum 🔧 (STRICTER - was 9.5)
• **Moderate off-center (65/35 split)**: Cap 9.0 maximum
• **Noticeably off-center (70/30 split)**: Cap 8.0 maximum
• **Severely off-center (75/25 or worse)**: Cap 7.0 maximum

Apply cap to BOTH front and back centering independently.
Use the WORST axis (L/R or T/B) to determine the cap.
Common on Pokemon cards - VERY COMMON on WOTC era (1999-2003)

**ERA-SPECIFIC POKEMON DEFECTS:**
• **WOTC Era (1999-2003)**: Print lines, rough edges, off-center cuts, 1st Edition stamp misalignment
• **E-Reader Era (2002-2003)**: Dot code printing errors, edge roughness
• **Modern Era (2016+)**: Holofoil scratches, texture wear on Full Arts, edge whitening on colored borders
• **Black Star Promos**: Especially prone to edge whitening due to card stock material

🚨 **v4.3 DETECTION PRIORITY FOR POKEMON** (Most Frequently Missed):
1. 🆕 **STRUCTURAL DAMAGE** - CHECK FIRST - Scan entire card for creases, bends, folds, tears (GRADE-LIMITING)
2. **Edge whitening** - Scan ENTIRE perimeter on colored borders for ANY white showing (happens on 70%+ of Pokemon cards)
3. **Corner whitening** - Check all 4 corners for white showing on colored borders
4. **Holofoil scratches** - Examine holographic areas at multiple angles for linear scratches
5. **Corner white fiber** - Examine EVERY corner tip at maximum zoom for ANY white fiber
6. **Print lines** - Especially on WOTC era (1999-2003), look for faint horizontal manufacturing lines
7. **Surface white dots** - Check entire surface for ANY white specks or debris
8. **Holo bleed** - Check reverse side for holofoil pattern showing through

🆕 **STRUCTURAL DAMAGE IDENTIFICATION GUIDE**:
• **Crease**: Look for visible line across card that shows material has been folded/bent
• **Severity Test**: Tilt card at angles - if crease only visible at angles = Minor; if visible head-on = Moderate; if surface broken = Deep
• **Location**: Creases can appear anywhere - corners, edges, middle of card
• **Evidence**: Often appears as subtle color change, shadow line, or texture disruption
• **Corner Bends**: Check if corners have fold/crease lines vs simple rounding wear
• **Dents**: Look for indentations that create shadow/depth, especially visible at angles

• Method: Internal geometric centering only

**STEP 2: NUMERIC MEASUREMENT (REQUIRED)**

**For bordered cards (Type A):**

**For borderless/anchor cards (Types B-E):**

• "[Anchor element] is ≈ [Y] pixels from right edge"
• Must identify at least TWO anchor points per axis

**STEP 3: CALCULATE RATIOS INDEPENDENTLY**

Measure each axis separately (NO reuse or symmetry assumptions):

**Left/Right Ratio:**
• Narrower border ÷ Wider border × 100 = ratio
• Example: 23px ÷ 30px × 100 = 76.67 → **77/23** ratio

**Top/Bottom Ratio:**
• Narrower border ÷ Wider border × 100 = ratio
• Example: 19px ÷ 25px × 100 = 76.00 → **76/24** ratio

**Use WORST axis** for centering score

**STEP 4: SECOND-PASS VERIFICATION (MANDATORY)**

After calculating ratio, ask yourself:
❓ "Do my measured border values logically support this centering ratio?"

If NO → Revise to more conservative ratio
If uncertain → Default to LESS perfect ratio (55/45 or 60/40, NEVER 50/50)

**STEP 5: APPLY CONSERVATIVE DEFAULTS**

If certainty is LOW due to:
• Glare or poor visibility
• Unclear border edges
• Foil distortion
• Busy artwork
• Image quality issues

→ You MUST use less perfect ratio
→ NEVER default upward toward perfection
→ Example: If unsure between 50/50 and 55/45 → Use 55/45
