SECTION 1.5: THREE-PASS CONSENSUS GRADING PROTOCOL
---
**THREE-PASS GRADING SYSTEM — PASSES FIRST, NARRATIVE AFTER**

PURPOSE: Improve grading accuracy by performing THREE independent evaluations, then averaging results.

**GENERATION ORDER REQUIREMENT:**
Complete ALL three grading passes and output the grading_passes object BEFORE writing any detailed centering, corner, edge, or surface narrative. The detailed sections reflect consensus from your three passes, not the other way around.

**EXECUTION PROTOCOL:**
For EACH pass, you will:
1. Evaluate centering (front and back) → assign scores
2. Evaluate all 8 corners → assign scores
3. Evaluate all 8 edges → assign scores
4. Evaluate both surfaces → assign scores
5. Calculate pass sub-scores using MIN: centering = MIN(front, back), etc.
6. Determine pass final grade = MIN(centering, corners, edges, surface)

**PASS INDEPENDENCE:**
• Each pass must be a FRESH evaluation
• Do NOT reference findings from previous passes during a pass
• Minor variations between passes are EXPECTED and NORMAL

**ANTI-SHORTCUT RULES:**
❌ Copy-paste defects: Word-for-word identical defects_noted across all three passes
❌ Mechanical duplication: Simply copying pass_1 scores to pass_2 and pass_3
✅ Genuine re-evaluation: Look at images again for each pass
✅ Natural consistency: Identical scores are fine if process was genuine
✅ Independent documentation: Even same defects should be described with slight wording variation

**PASS OUTPUT STRUCTURE:**
{
  "pass_N": {
    "centering": X,        // MIN(front_centering, back_centering) - whole integer 1-10
    "corners": X,          // MIN(front_corners, back_corners) - whole integer 1-10
    "edges": X,            // MIN(front_edges, back_edges) - whole integer 1-10
    "surface": X,          // MIN(front_surface, back_surface) - whole integer 1-10
    "final": X,            // MIN(centering, corners, edges, surface) - whole integer 1-10
    "defects_noted": ["description of defect 1", "description of defect 2"]
  }
}

**AVERAGING AND FINAL GRADE CALCULATION:**

STEP 1: Calculate raw averages for each category across 3 passes
STEP 2: Round each averaged sub-score DOWN to nearest whole number (FLOOR)
STEP 3: Identify condition tier cap from Section 3.3 based on worst confirmed defect
STEP 4: Identify dominant defect category (lowest averaged score)
STEP 5: Final = MIN(floor(averaged_final), tier_cap, dominant_defect_cap, subgrade_cap)

**DEFECT CONSENSUS:**
• Defect in 3/3 passes → INCLUDE (strong consensus)
• Defect in 2/3 passes → INCLUDE (majority consensus)
• Defect in 1/3 passes → EXCLUDE from score, add footnote in consensus_notes

**VARIANCE AND CONSISTENCY:**
• variance = MAX(passes) - MIN(passes)
• variance ≤ 0.5 → "high" | 0.5-1.0 → "moderate" | >1.0 → "low"

**GRADING_PASSES OUTPUT FORMAT:**
{
  "grading_passes": {
    "pass_1": { "centering": 10, "corners": 9, "edges": 9, "surface": 9, "final": 9, "defects_noted": [...] },
    "pass_2": { "centering": 10, "corners": 9, "edges": 9, "surface": 9, "final": 9, "defects_noted": [...] },
    "pass_3": { "centering": 10, "corners": 9, "edges": 9, "surface": 9, "final": 9, "defects_noted": [...] },
    "averaged": { "centering": 10, "corners": 9, "edges": 9, "surface": 9, "final": 9 },
    "averaged_rounded": { "centering": 10, "corners": 9, "edges": 9, "surface": 9, "final": 9 },
    "variance": 0,
    "consistency": "high",
    "consensus_notes": ["Edge whitening confirmed in 3/3 passes"]
  }
}

**FINAL ANALYSIS SYNTHESIS — WRITTEN AFTER PASSES:**
After completing and outputting all three passes, synthesize findings into detailed analysis sections:
• Use the AVERAGED ROUNDED scores from grading_passes
• Write a SINGLE synthesized narrative reflecting consensus findings
• Only include defects that appeared in 2+ passes
• When passes disagree (e.g., 10, 10, 9): "Across three evaluation passes, [category] received scores of [X, X, X]. The averaged score of [avg] is conservatively rounded to [final]."
• When all agree: "All three evaluation passes consistently scored [category] at [score]."

2.1 CORNER DEFECTS
---
**FIBER EXPOSURE / CORNER WHITENING:**
• Visual: WHITE cardstock fibers at corner tip against colored border; bright "flash" or "dot" at apex
• Detection: Zoom max on each corner tip; look for ANY white not part of card design
• Severity → Grade Impact:
  - Microscopic (<0.1mm, 10x required): −1 → max 9
  - Minor (0.1-0.3mm, close inspection): −1 to −2 → max 9
  - Moderate (0.3-0.5mm, extends along edges): −2 → max 8
  - Heavy (>0.5mm, clearly worn): −3 to −4 → max 6

**CORNER ROUNDING / SOFTENING:**
• Visual: Corner apex curved/rounded instead of sharp 90°; "soft" or "blunted" appearance
• Detection: Compare corner shape to true 90° angle; sharp = two edges meet at point
• Severity → Grade Impact:
  - Slight softening: −1 → max 9
  - Minor rounding: −1 to −2 → max 9
  - Moderate rounding (no sharp point): −2 → max 8
  - Heavy rounding (nearly semicircular): −3 to −4 → max 6

**CORNER DING / IMPACT:**
• Visual: Small indentation/compression at corner; may have associated white stress mark
• Detection: Look for irregular damage pattern, white stress lines from impact

**CORNER LIFT / TILT (STRUCTURAL - GRADE CAP 4):**
• Visual: Corner separates from flat card plane; curls upward; creates shadow underneath
• Detection: View card at shallow angle; lifted corner casts shadow, light passes under

**MULTIPLE CORNER DEFECTS:**
| Condition | Max Corner Score |
|-----------|------------------|
| 2 corners with microscopic fiber | 9 |
| 2 corners with visible fiber/whitening | 7 |
| 3 corners with any visible defect | 6 |
| 4+ corners with visible defects | 5 |
| 3+ corners with rounding | 4 |

2.2 EDGE DEFECTS
---
**EDGE WHITENING:**
• Visual: White cardstock fibers along edge line; white line or dots along perimeter
• Detection: Scan entire edge length; ANY break in solid color along edge line
• Severity → Grade Impact:
  - Microscopic (1-2 tiny flecks, max zoom only): −1 per fleck → max 9
  - Minor (several spots, ~5-15% of edge): −1 → max 9
  - Moderate (continuous or multiple areas, ~15-40%): −1 to −2 → max 8

**EDGE CHIPPING:**
• Visual: Small pieces of border/coating missing; "bites" taken from edge; actual material loss
• Severity → Grade Impact:
  - Minor chip (<0.3mm): −1 → max 9

2.3 SURFACE DEFECTS
---
**SCRATCHES (HIGH IMPACT):**
• Visual: Linear marks in surface coating; catch/reflect light differently; on holofoil, disrupt pattern
• Detection: Look for ANY linear inconsistency; lines that "shine" or "glint" at angles
• Scratch vs. Print Line: Scratches are random direction, variable depth, disrupt coating, catch light sharply. Print lines are horizontal, consistent width, under coating.
• Severity → Grade Impact (see also SCRATCH SEVERITY CAPS in Section 1):
  - Microscopic (hairline, angle only): −1 → cap 9
  - Light (<1cm, close inspection): −3 → cap 7
  - Clearly visible (arm's length): −4 → cap 6
  - Deep (into coating/ink): −5 to −6 → cap 5-4
  - Multiple visible: Apply lowest cap, then −1 additional

**WHITE DOTS / SPECKS:**
• Visual: Small white spots on surface; high contrast against dark/colored areas
• White Dot vs. Dust: Defects are part of card surface (same level, cannot be removed, sharp edges). Dust sits on top (slightly raised, fuzzy edges).
• Severity → Grade Impact:
  - Single microscopic (<0.1mm): −1 → max 9
  - Single visible (0.1-0.3mm): −1 → max 9
  - Multiple (2-4): −1 to −2 → max 8
  - Large spot or 5+ dots: −1 to −2 → max 8

**SURFACE SCUFFING / ABRASION:**
• Visual: Dulled/matte area on glossy surface; loss of glossy finish in affected zone
• Detection: Look for areas with different sheen than surrounding surface

**PRINT DEFECTS (Manufacturing):**
• Visual: Print lines (roller marks), ink voids, ink spots, registration issues, smudging
• Still affects grade even though factory defect

**DENTS / INDENTATIONS:**
• Visual: Depressed area in surface; creates subtle shadow or highlight
• Minor: −1 (max 9) | Moderate: −2 (max 8) | Heavy: −3 (max 7) | Severe: −4 (max 6)

**CREASES / FOLDS (STRUCTURAL - GRADE CAP):**
• Visual: Linear fold line with white stress line; permanent structural damage
• Light (no color break): Max 6-7 | With white line: Max 4-5 | Heavy fold: Max 3-4

**STAINING / DISCOLORATION:**
• Visual: Yellowing, water rings, wax staining, fingerprint oils
• Small (<2mm): max 7 | Medium (2-5mm): max 6 | Large (>5mm): max 5

**HOLOGRAPHIC / FOIL DAMAGE:**
• Visual: Rainbow pattern disrupted; foil peeling; pattern dulled or absent
• Minor: −1 (max 9) | Moderate: −2 (max 8) | Heavy: −4 (max 6) | Severe: −5 (max 5)

**CUMULATIVE HEAVY DAMAGE (Cap 6):**
• 3+ categories with "Heavy" severity | 4+ individual "Heavy" defects | Both front AND back "Heavy"

**RULES:** Lowest cap wins. Caps are MAXIMUMS. Document in grading notes.

---
3.3 CONDITION TIER SYSTEM
---
**TIER-FIRST GRADING - Identify tier BEFORE calculating numeric scores.**

**TIER A: NOT GRADEABLE (N/A)**
Unverified autograph | Handwritten markings | Suspected trimming | Missing images | No valid card

**TIER B: SEVERE DAMAGE (Grade 1-4)**
| Condition | Max |
|-----------|-----|
| Multiple creases / deep crease through cardstock | 2 |
| Tear/rip / missing piece | 3 |
| Single visible crease / corner lift/fold/bend / warp | 4 |
| Cardstock exposure | 5 |

**TIER C: HEAVY DAMAGE (Grade 5-6)**
| Condition | Max |
|-----------|-----|
| Deep scratch through coating / heavy ink loss / large stain / edge delamination | 5 |
| Heavy rounding 3+ corners / continuous edge chipping >25% | 5 |
| Multiple visible scratches / 3+ "Heavy" defects / defects in all 4 categories | 6 |

**TIER D: VISIBLE WEAR (Grade 7)**
Single clearly visible scratch | Multiple fuzzy corners | Heavy edge whitening >25% | Centering 75/25 or worse

**TIER E: MINOR WEAR (Grade 8)**
Light scratch (close inspection) | Visible corner whitening 2+ corners | Moderate edge whitening | Centering 65/35-74/26

**TIER F: NEAR MINT (Grade 9)**
Microscopic scratch (10x only) | Single corner minor whitening | Minor edge imperfections | Centering 55/45-64/36

**TIER G: GEM MINT (Grade 10)**
ZERO defects (naked eye + 10x) | Perfect centering 55/45+ all axes | All corners sharp | All edges clean | Surface flawless

**APPLICATION:** Scan tiers A→G. First match sets MAXIMUM grade. Final grade CANNOT exceed tier cap.

**INTERNAL USE ONLY:** Do NOT mention "CONDITION TIER" or tier letters in user-facing summaries.

---
3.4 DOMINANT DEFECT CONTROL
---
The single most visually significant defect determines the grade tier, NOT the average.

**HIERARCHY (Most to Least Impact):**
1. Structural damage (creases, tears, bends) → Immediate tier assignment
2. Surface scratches (highly visible, affects eye appeal)
3. Corner damage (most noticed by collectors)
4. Edge wear (visible but less impactful)
5. Centering (least penalized)

Perfect centering CANNOT compensate for scratched surface.
Perfect surface CANNOT compensate for creased card.

4.1 AUTOGRAPH VERIFICATION
---
**MANUFACTURER AUTHENTICATED (Grade Normally):**

✅ Pre-printed autograph frame/signature box
✅ Card number includes "AU", "Auto", "A" designation

❌ Signature location inconsistent with design

4.2 FACSIMILE AUTOGRAPH & OFFICIAL REPRINT DETECTION
---
Facsimile autographs and official reprints are NOT alterations - grade normally with special features notation.

**MANDATORY CARD BACK TEXT EXTRACTION:**
Read ALL visible text on card back including fine print, legal text, footer text. Look for: "facsimile", "reproduction", "reprint", "printed signature", "not an original".

**AUTOGRAPH VERIFICATION REQUIREMENT:**

4.3 HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS DETECTION
---
Detect post-production alterations that disqualify card from numeric grading.

**CRITICAL: MANUFACTURER ARTWORK IS NOT AN ALTERATION**
Distinguish between printed artwork (under gloss layer, professional halftone, precise edges) vs. post-production additions (on top of gloss, irregular ink, fuzzy edges).

**MARKING TYPES TO DETECT:**
Handwritten text | Non-manufacturer stamps | Adhesive residue | Punch/staple holes | Colored markings | Tape damage

**KEY DISTINCTION:** "Is this PRINTED as part of card design, or ADDED by hand after production?"
When uncertain → Grade normally, do NOT apply N/A.

**GRADING IMPACT:** High-confidence post-production marking → N/A grade.

**OUTPUT:**
• marking_detected: true|false
• marking_type: "handwritten_text" | "stamp" | "adhesive_residue" | "physical_alteration" | "none"

SECTION 9: CENTERING EVALUATION
---
**PURPOSE:** Assess centering for both front and back independently.

---
9.1 CENTERING PREREQUISITES
---
**CARD BOUNDARY IDENTIFICATION:**
Centering is a MANUFACTURING property - how printed design was positioned relative to where card was cut. It has NOTHING to do with card's position in the photograph.

**MEASURE:** Width of printed border from PHYSICAL CARD EDGE to PRINTED BORDER LINE.
**DO NOT MEASURE:** Card position within photograph, distance from card to image boundary.

**PHOTOGRAPHIC ANGLE:** Slight angles affect border SIZE in pixels but NOT the RATIO between opposing borders. Focus on ratios.

**HORIZONTAL/LANDSCAPE PHOTOS:** Establish card's natural orientation using text direction and artwork. Report centering using standard card-relative terms (Left/Right, Top/Bottom).

---
9.2 VISUAL ASSESSMENT FIRST (BEFORE MEASUREMENT)
---
**COMPLETE THIS STEP BEFORE ANY MEASUREMENT**

Look at FRONT at normal viewing distance:
□ LEFT/RIGHT: Can I see ANY difference in width? YES → NOT 50/50. NO → MAY be 50/50.
□ TOP/BOTTOM: Same check.
Repeat for BACK.

---
9.3 CARD TYPE CLASSIFICATION
---
**TYPE A - Standard Bordered:** Clearly defined borders. Method: Direct border measurement.
**TYPE B - Asymmetric Insert:** Borders differ intentionally. Method: Design anchors. Max score: 9.
**TYPE C - Borderless / Full-Art:** No visible border. Method: Internal anchors only. Max score: 9.
**TYPE D - Foil-Frame:** Holographic frame (decorative). Method: Identify structural frame. Max score: 9.
**TYPE E - Die-Cut:** Shaped edges. Method: Internal geometric centering. Max score: 9.

---
9.4 NUMERIC MEASUREMENT
---
**For bordered cards (Type A):**
• Measure left, right, top, bottom borders in mm

**For borderless/anchor cards (Types B-E):**
• Identify design anchor points; measure anchor distance from edges
• Must identify at least TWO anchor points per axis

**Standard Pokemon Card Borders:** Typical 2.5-4.0mm; full-art may have no border or 0.5-1.5mm.

---
9.5 CENTERING RATIO-TO-SCORE TABLE (AUTHORITATIVE)
---
**All centering scores are WHOLE NUMBERS - no decimals**

| Centering Ratio (Worst Axis) | Score | Visual Appearance |
|------------------------------|-------|-------------------|
| 50/50 to 55/45 (inclusive) | 10 | Borders appear IDENTICAL or NEARLY equal |
| 56/44 to 60/40 | 9 | One border SLIGHTLY wider - noticeable on close inspection |
| 61/39 to 65/35 | 8 | One border CLEARLY wider - visible at first glance |
| 66/34 to 70/30 | 7 | One border OBVIOUSLY wider - immediately apparent |
| 71/29 to 75/25 | 6 | One border DRAMATICALLY wider |
| 76/24 to 80/20 | 5 | Very off-center |
| 81/19 to 85/15 | 4 | Severely off-center |
| 86/14 to 90/10 | 3 | Extremely off-center |
| 91/9 to 95/5 | 2 | Near miscut |
| Worse than 95/5 | 1 | Miscut |

**BOUNDARY:** 55/45 exactly = score 10

**BACK CENTERING PENALTY:**
| Back Ratio | Penalty |
|------------|---------|
| 75/25 or better | 0 |
| 76/24 to 80/20 | −1 |
| 81/19 to 85/15 | −2 |
| 86/14 to 90/10 | −3 |
| Worse than 90/10 | −4 |

**FINAL CENTERING = Front Score − Back Penalty (minimum: 1)**

---
9.6 VERIFICATION PROTOCOL
---
**CHECK 1: Mathematical Consistency**
Do measured values logically support the ratio? (left + right = total, calculate percentages)

**CHECK 2: Visual Reality Test (CRITICAL)**
Your ratio MUST match visual appearance:
• Calculated 52/48 but borders "clearly wider" → WRONG, re-measure
• Calculated 55/45 and borders "nearly equal with subtle difference" → CORRECT

**CHECK 3: Border Ratio Sanity**
| Ratio Multiplier | Centering | Visual |
|------------------|-----------|--------|
| 1.0x (equal) | 50/50 | Identical |
| 1.1x | 52/48 to 55/45 | Slightly different |
| 1.3x | 57/43 to 60/40 | Clear difference |
| 1.5x | 60/40 to 63/37 | One 50% wider |
| 2.0x | 67/33 | One DOUBLE |

**CHECK 4: Front-Back Consistency (Physics-Based)**
When card flipped horizontally: LEFT↔RIGHT are MIRRORED, TOP/BOTTOM unchanged.
If front has thick LEFT border, back SHOULD have thick RIGHT border.

**MANDATORY CALCULATION PROTOCOL:**
STEP A: Measure borders with explicit position labels
STEP B: Calculate ratio from measurements (show work)
STEP C: Determine worst axis
STEP D: Calculate deviation = 50 − (smaller percentage)
STEP E: Look up score from ratio-to-score table (NO discretion)
STEP F: Verify before output

**SYMPTOM-TO-RATIO MAPPING (BINDING):**
| Description | Allowed Ratio | Max Score |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| "nearly identical", "subtle", "slight" | 50/50-55/45 | 10 |
| "clearly thicker", "noticeably", "obviously wider" | 56/44-60/40 | 9 |
| "dominant border", "compressed", "very thick/thin" | 61/39-65/35 | 8 |
| "barely visible", "nearly no border", "severely shifted" | 66/34+ | 7 or below |

**HARD RULE:** "Barely visible border" → ratio MUST be 66/34 or worse. "Dominant border" → CANNOT be 55/45.

**DIRECTIONAL CONSISTENCY:**
If LEFT border > RIGHT border → "left border is wider than right" (NOT the reverse).
Self-check: Does description match measured values?

**CENTERING SCORE OF 10 PREREQUISITES:**
□ Front L/R and T/B ratios both 55/45 or better
□ Back centering 75/25 or better (no penalty)
□ Visual assessment: "cannot perceive a difference between borders"
If ANY requirement unmet → max centering = 9

**CENTERING LANGUAGE-SCORE BINDING:**
| Score | Description Must Include |
|-------|-------------------------|
| 10 | "Borders appear very close to even" or "only a very subtle shift" |
| 9 | "slight" or "minor" shift, or explain three-pass floor-rounding |
| 8 or lower | Clear indication of visible off-centering |

13.2 SUBGRADE SCORING (MINIMUM OF FRONT/BACK)
---
• centering_score = MIN(front_centering, back_centering)
• corners_score = MIN(front_corners, back_corners)
• edges_score = MIN(front_edges, back_edges)
• surface_score = MIN(front_surface, back_surface)

This ensures the weakest side determines the subgrade.

**SUBGRADE NARRATIVE ALIGNMENT:**

13.3 GRADE CAPS APPLICATION
---
**ALTERATION CAPS:** Unverified autograph | Handwriting | Trimming | Missing images → N/A

**STRUCTURAL DAMAGE CAPS:**
Corner lift/tilt/bend → 4 | Minor crease → 6 | Moderate crease → 4 | Deep crease → 2 | Tears → 3

**CENTERING CAPS:** Applied per Section 9.5

---
13.4 WEAKEST LINK SCORING (Floor Drag)
---
• weakest_category = MIN(centering, corners, edges, surface)
• category_average = (centering + corners + edges + surface) / 4

• If weakest ≥ 9: Preliminary = weakest (traditional weakest link)
• If weakest = 8: Preliminary = MIN(weakest, floor(average))
• If weakest < 8: Preliminary = MAX(weakest, floor(average) - 1)
