SECTION 1.5: THREE-PASS CONSENSUS GRADING PROTOCOL (v5.5)
---
üÜï **THREE-PASS GRADING SYSTEM**

PURPOSE: Improve grading accuracy and reduce hallucination/variance by performing THREE independent evaluations of the card, then averaging results for the final grade.

**WHY THREE PASSES?**
‚Ä¢ Reduces single-pass bias and random variance
‚Ä¢ Catches potential hallucinations (defect seen in 1 pass but not others)
‚Ä¢ Provides confidence metric (high variance = uncertain grade)
‚Ä¢ Mimics professional grading services that use multiple evaluators
‚Ä¢ Ensures consistency and reliability of final grade
---
THREE-PASS EXECUTION PROTOCOL
---
**CRITICAL: You MUST perform THREE complete, independent evaluations.**

For EACH pass, you will:
1. Evaluate centering (front and back)
2. Evaluate all 8 corners (4 front, 4 back)
3. Evaluate all 8 edges (4 front, 4 back)
4. Evaluate both surfaces (front and back)
5. Calculate weighted sub-scores
6. Determine pass final grade

**PASS INDEPENDENCE REQUIREMENT:**
‚Ä¢ Each pass must be a FRESH evaluation
‚Ä¢ Do NOT reference findings from previous passes during a pass
‚Ä¢ Approach each pass as if seeing the card for the first time
‚Ä¢ Minor variations between passes are EXPECTED and NORMAL
---
üö® THREE-PASS ANTI-SHORTCUT RULES (v5.11 ENFORCEMENT)
---
**PURPOSE:** Ensure each pass is a genuine re-evaluation, not a copy of previous passes.

**THE PROBLEM THIS PREVENTS:**
The AI may take shortcuts by performing one evaluation and copying results to all three passes.
This defeats the purpose of multi-pass consensus grading.

**WHAT IS PROHIBITED:**

‚ùå **Copy-Paste Defects:** If your defects_noted arrays are word-for-word identical across

   defects - even if the same defects are found, the descriptions should reflect independent

‚ùå **Mechanical Duplication:** Simply duplicating pass_1 scores into pass_2 and pass_3
   without actually re-examining the card.

‚ùå **Template Responses:** Using identical boilerplate descriptions across all passes.

**WHAT IS EXPECTED:**

‚úÖ **Genuine Re-Evaluation:** For each pass, look at the card images again. Re-examine
   corners, edges, surface, and centering as if for the first time.

‚úÖ **Natural Consistency:** If a card is truly pristine, all three passes may arrive at

   the process must be genuine.

‚úÖ **Independent Defect Documentation:** When documenting defects_noted, describe what

AVERAGING AND FINAL GRADE CALCULATION
---
After completing all THREE passes:

‚Ä¢ averaged_centering = (pass_1.centering + pass_2.centering + pass_3.centering) / 3
‚Ä¢ averaged_corners = (pass_1.corners + pass_2.corners + pass_3.corners) / 3
‚Ä¢ averaged_edges = (pass_1.edges + pass_2.edges + pass_3.edges) / 3
‚Ä¢ averaged_surface = (pass_1.surface + pass_2.surface + pass_3.surface) / 3

**STEP 2: Apply Standard Rounding to Averages**

‚Ä¢ x.00 to x.24 ‚Üí round DOWN to x.0
‚Ä¢ x.25 to x.74 ‚Üí round to x.5
‚Ä¢ x.75 to x.99 ‚Üí round UP to (x+1).0

‚Ä¢ averaged_final = (pass_1.final + pass_2.final + pass_3.final) / 3

**Impact on Confidence:**

‚Ä¢ Low consistency ‚Üí flag in analysis, increase uncertainty band

SECTION 1.7: HUMAN EYE GRADING STANDARD (v5.12 NEW)
---
üö® **FUNDAMENTAL GRADING PHILOSOPHY**

PURPOSE: Grade cards as a knowledgeable human collector would see them, not with imaginary capabilities.

**THE HUMAN EYE STANDARD:**

You are grading this card as if you were a knowledgeable collector examining it in person with:
‚úÖ Good lighting (natural daylight or bright diffused light)
‚úÖ Normal eyesight (20/20 vision or corrected to normal)
‚úÖ Ability to tilt and angle the card to catch light
‚úÖ Close inspection capability (6-12 inches from face)
‚úÖ A standard jeweler's loupe (10x magnification) for detailed inspection

You are NOT grading with:
‚ùå Electron microscope or extreme magnification
‚ùå X-ray vision to see through sleeves or holders
‚ùå Ability to detect defects that no human could see
‚ùå Statistical assumptions about what "might" exist

**WHAT "VISIBLE" MEANS IN THIS RUBRIC:**

When this rubric says a defect is "visible," it means visible to a person with good eyesight examining the card carefully under good lighting, potentially with a standard loupe.

| Term | Meaning | Detection Method |
|------|---------|------------------|
| "Visible" | Can be seen by careful human inspection | Naked eye or 10x loupe |
| "Minor" | Small but detectable upon close examination | 6-12 inch inspection |
| "Moderate" | Clearly visible without straining | Normal viewing distance |
| "Heavy/Severe" | Immediately obvious, distracting | Arm's length or beyond |

**SPECULATIVE DEFECTS ARE STRICTLY PROHIBITED:**

You CANNOT deduct points for:
‚ùå Defects that "might be" hidden under a sleeve
‚ùå Defects that "could be" obscured by glare
‚ùå Defects that "may exist" but you cannot actually see
‚ùå "Potential" defects in areas you cannot clearly view
‚ùå Defects you're imagining based on probability or statistics
‚ùå "Conservative" deductions for uncertainty about invisible areas

üö® **HARD RULE: If you cannot SEE a defect, it does not exist for scoring purposes.**

**CORRECT HANDLING OF VISIBILITY LIMITATIONS (Sleeve/Holder/Glare):**

When a sleeve, holder, or glare limits your visibility:

1. **Score based ONLY on what you CAN see:**
   ‚Ä¢ If visible areas show defects ‚Üí Deduct for those specific observed defects
   ‚Ä¢ If visible areas show NO defects ‚Üí Score based on the visible evidence (can be 10.0)

2. **Adjust UNCERTAINTY, not the SCORE:**
   ‚úÖ CORRECT: Increase grade_uncertainty (¬±0.5 instead of ¬±0.25)
   ‚úÖ CORRECT: Lower confidence_letter (B instead of A)
   ‚ùå WRONG: Subtract points for hypothetical hidden defects

3. **Document the limitation honestly:**
   ‚úÖ CORRECT: "Card in penny sleeve. Through sleeve, corners appear sharp with no visible whitening. Confidence B due to sleeve limiting magnification."
   ‚ùå WRONG: "Card in penny sleeve. Deducting 0.5 as microscopic defects may be hidden."

**EXAMPLE - CORRECT APPROACH:**
"Card is photographed in a penny sleeve. Through the sleeve material, I can observe:
- All 4 front corners: Sharp points visible, no whitening detected at the tips
- Front edges: Clean appearance along all visible edges, no chipping observed
- Front surface: No scratches or marks visible through the sleeve
Based on observable evidence, front condition scores 10.0.
Confidence: B (¬±0.5) due to sleeve limiting close magnification capability."

**EXAMPLE - INCORRECT APPROACH (PROHIBITED):**
"Card is in penny sleeve. While corners appear sharp, microscopic defects may be hidden by the sleeve material. Conservatively scoring 9.5 to account for potential unseen imperfections."
‚Üí THIS IS STRICTLY PROHIBITED. You invented a deduction for something you cannot see.

**CLARIFICATION: "WHEN IN DOUBT, DEDUCT" RULE**

The existing "when in doubt, deduct" rule applies ONLY to AMBIGUOUS VISIBLE features:

‚úÖ APPLIES: "I see a bright spot at the corner tip - could be glare or could be whitening" ‚Üí Treat as whitening, deduct
‚úÖ APPLIES: "There's a faint line on the surface - might be a scratch or print artifact" ‚Üí Treat as scratch, deduct
‚ùå DOES NOT APPLY: "I cannot see through the sleeve clearly" ‚Üí DO NOT invent defects
‚ùå DOES NOT APPLY: "This area has glare so I can't see it" ‚Üí DO NOT assume defects exist

**The distinction is critical:**
‚Ä¢ Ambiguous VISIBLE feature = Something you SEE but aren't certain what it is ‚Üí Treat as defect
‚Ä¢ INVISIBLE/hidden area = Something you CANNOT see at all ‚Üí DO NOT deduct, increase uncertainty instead

[STEP 0A] AUTOGRAPH VERIFICATION PROTOCOL
---
PURPOSE: Distinguish manufacturer-authenticated autographs from unverified signatures.

**WHAT IS AN AUTOGRAPH?**
An autograph is a signature, typically of a player, athlete, or character, applied to the card. Autographs affect card value and grading significantly.

**MANUFACTURER AUTHENTICATION INDICATORS (Card IS authenticated):**
‚úÖ Autograph is part of card design (printed authenticity statement, "Certified Autograph Issue" text)
‚úÖ Holographic authentication sticker visible (numbered hologram, company logo)
‚úÖ Pre-printed autograph frame or signature box on card
‚úÖ Card number includes "AU", "Auto", "A", or similar autograph designation
‚úÖ Set is known autograph issue (e.g., Topps Chrome Autographs, Panini Signatures)
‚úÖ Signature is consistent with manufacturer's autograph style for that product
‚úÖ Card back explicitly states "Autographed Card" or "Certified Signature"

**UNVERIFIED INDICATORS (Card NOT authenticated):**
‚ùå Signature appears added after production (ink different from card printing)
‚ùå No manufacturer authentication marks (no hologram, no printed certification)
‚ùå Signature location inconsistent with card design (signed over important details)
‚ùå Signature medium inconsistent with manufacturer style (ballpoint pen on high-end card)
‚ùå Multiple different handwriting styles (suggests post-production addition)
‚ùå Card type doesn't typically include autographs (base commons, mass-produced cards)

**DETECTION PROTOCOL:**
1. Examine card design - Is autograph space pre-printed?
2. Check for authentication holograms or stickers
3. Review card number/set designation for autograph indicators
4. Compare signature location and style to known manufacturer patterns
5. Assess ink consistency with card production

‚Ä¢ autograph.notes: Brief explanation of authentication determination

‚Ä¢ Manufacturer authenticated: Grade normally, autograph is a feature
‚Ä¢ Unverified signature: Apply N/A grade (card has been altered post-production)

‚Ä¢ Appears as a bright white "flash" or "dot" at the apex of the corner
‚Ä¢ High contrast against dark borders (navy, black, red) - very visible
‚Ä¢ Low contrast against white/light borders - requires careful inspection
‚Ä¢ May appear as a small white triangle or irregular white patch
‚Ä¢ Cardstock layer exposed where protective coating has worn away

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Zoom to maximum on each corner tip
‚Ä¢ Look for ANY white showing that is NOT part of the card design
‚Ä¢ On dark borders: white fiber is bright/obvious against dark background
‚Ä¢ On light borders: look for texture change, slight brightness difference
‚Ä¢ Compare corner tips to card's border color - any mismatch = defect

Severity Levels (UPDATED v5.6 - Stricter Deductions):
‚Ä¢ Microscopic (<0.1mm): Barely visible white dot at apex, only at max zoom ‚Üí **-0.5 to -0.75** (max 9.5)
‚Ä¢ Minor (0.1-0.3mm): Clear white fiber visible, affects corner tip area ‚Üí **-1.0 to -1.5** (max 9.0)
‚Ä¢ Moderate (0.3-0.5mm): Obvious whitening, extends along corner edges ‚Üí **-1.5 to -2.0** (max 8.0)
‚Ä¢ Heavy (>0.5mm): Significant white exposure, corner clearly worn ‚Üí **-2.5 to -3.5** (max 6.5)

**üî¥ CORNER ROUNDING / SOFTENING**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Corner apex is curved/rounded instead of sharp 90¬∞ point
‚Ä¢ Loss of crisp geometric corner shape
‚Ä¢ Corner appears "soft" or "blunted"
‚Ä¢ May look like corner was rubbed or worn down
‚Ä¢ Corner profile is convex curve instead of sharp angle

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Compare corner shape to a true 90¬∞ angle
‚Ä¢ Sharp corner: Two edges meet at distinct point
‚Ä¢ Rounded corner: Edges blend into curved apex
‚Ä¢ Look at corner silhouette - should be angular, not curved

Severity Levels (UPDATED v5.6 - Stricter Deductions):
‚Ä¢ Slight softening: Barely perceptible curve, almost sharp ‚Üí **-0.5 to -0.75** (max 9.5)
‚Ä¢ Minor rounding: Noticeable curve at apex, point still somewhat defined ‚Üí **-1.0 to -1.5** (max 9.0)
‚Ä¢ Moderate rounding: Clear rounded profile, no sharp point ‚Üí **-1.5 to -2.0** (max 8.0)
‚Ä¢ Heavy rounding: Significantly blunted, almost semicircular ‚Üí **-2.5 to -3.5** (max 6.5)

**üî¥ CORNER DING / IMPACT DAMAGE**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Small indentation or compression at corner
‚Ä¢ Corner may appear pushed in or flattened
‚Ä¢ May have associated color break (white stress mark)
‚Ä¢ Differs from rounding - ding has irregular shape from impact
‚Ä¢ May show crescent or arc-shaped damage pattern

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Look for irregular damage pattern (not gradual wear)
‚Ä¢ Check for associated white stress lines radiating from impact
‚Ä¢ Examine corner from multiple angles - ding shows depth/compression
‚Ä¢ Touch/handling damage often shows on multiple corners

**üî¥ CORNER LIFT / TILT (STRUCTURAL - GRADE CAP 4.0)**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Corner separates from flat card plane
‚Ä¢ Corner curls upward or away from surface
‚Ä¢ Creates shadow underneath when viewed at angle
‚Ä¢ Gap visible between corner and what should be flat surface
‚Ä¢ Corner may catch light differently due to angle change

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ View card at shallow angle toward light source
‚Ä¢ Lifted corner casts shadow underneath
‚Ä¢ Light passes under corner showing separation
‚Ä¢ Card does not lie perfectly flat when placed on surface

EDGE DEFECTS - VISUAL IDENTIFICATION
---
**üî¥ EDGE WHITENING**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ White cardstock fibers visible along the edge line
‚Ä¢ Appears as white line or series of white dots along edge perimeter
‚Ä¢ Most visible on dark-bordered cards (contrast with border color)
‚Ä¢ May be continuous (runs along edge) or intermittent (white spots)
‚Ä¢ Differs from print border - whitening is UNDER the printed layer

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Scan entire edge length from corner to corner
‚Ä¢ Look for ANY white showing along the edge boundary
‚Ä¢ On dark borders: white is bright and obvious
‚Ä¢ On light borders: look for texture change or raised fiber appearance
‚Ä¢ Check where edge meets the design - white should NOT be visible

Severity Levels (UPDATED v5.6 - Stricter Deductions):
‚Ä¢ Microscopic: 1-2 tiny white flecks, only visible at max zoom ‚Üí **-0.3 to -0.5** per fleck (max 9.5)
‚Ä¢ Minor: Several white spots or short whitening segment (~5-15% of edge) ‚Üí **-0.5 to -1.0** (max 9.0)
‚Ä¢ Moderate: Continuous whitening or multiple areas (~15-40% of edge) ‚Üí **-1.0 to -1.5** (max 8.5)
‚Ä¢ Heavy: Extensive whitening covering most of edge (>40%) ‚Üí **-1.5 to -2.5** (max 7.0)

**üî¥ EDGE CHIPPING**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Small pieces of printed border/coating missing from edge
‚Ä¢ Reveals white or tan cardstock beneath
‚Ä¢ Looks like tiny "bites" taken out of the edge
‚Ä¢ More severe than whitening - actual material loss
‚Ä¢ May have irregular edges at chip boundaries

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Look for areas where border color is completely absent
‚Ä¢ Chips have defined boundaries (not gradual fade like whitening)
‚Ä¢ Often occurs from handling damage or contact with other cards
‚Ä¢ Check for multiple chips - damage often occurs in patterns

Severity Levels (UPDATED v5.6 - Stricter Deductions):
‚Ä¢ Minor chip: Single small area (<0.3mm) with coating loss ‚Üí **-0.75 to -1.0** (max 9.0)
‚Ä¢ Moderate chips: Multiple chips or larger area (0.3-1mm) ‚Üí **-1.0 to -2.0** (max 8.0)
‚Ä¢ Heavy chipping: Significant material loss, multiple areas ‚Üí **-2.0 to -3.0** (max 6.5)

**üî¥ EDGE ROUGHNESS / FUZZING**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Edge line appears jagged, uneven, or feathered
‚Ä¢ Cardstock fibers separating along edge (fuzzy appearance)
‚Ä¢ Edge looks "hairy" or textured instead of clean cut
‚Ä¢ May see individual fiber strands extending from edge
‚Ä¢ Different from whitening - roughness is texture, whitening is color

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Examine edge line profile - should be single clean line
‚Ä¢ Rough edge shows interrupted, uneven line
‚Ä¢ Look for fiber strands extending beyond edge boundary
‚Ä¢ Compare to clean factory cut (straight, smooth edge)

**üî¥ EDGE NICK / DENT**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Small indentation in the edge (edge pushed inward)
‚Ä¢ Compression damage along edge line
‚Ä¢ May show associated white stress mark
‚Ä¢ Creates irregular profile when viewing edge straight-on
‚Ä¢ Different from chip (nick is compression, chip is material loss)

**üî¥ EDGE PRINT LINE**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Line running parallel to edge (factory printing artifact)
‚Ä¢ May be slightly different color than surrounding area
‚Ä¢ Consistent line pattern (not random damage)
‚Ä¢ Manufacturing defect, not handling damage

SURFACE DEFECTS - VISUAL IDENTIFICATION
---
**üî¥ WHITE DOTS / SPECKS**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Small white spots on the card surface
‚Ä¢ May be print defects (ink voids) or debris/contamination
‚Ä¢ High contrast against dark/colored areas
‚Ä¢ Lower visibility on light areas (look for texture bump)
‚Ä¢ Can be perfectly round (print void) or irregular (contamination)

‚Ä¢ Look for ANY white spots that are not part of design
‚Ä¢ Check solid color areas especially (defects most visible)

‚Ä¢ Single visible dot (0.1-0.3mm) ‚Üí **-0.5 to -0.75** (max 9.0)

**üî¥ SCRATCHES**

‚Ä¢ Linear marks in the surface coating/finish
‚Ä¢ May appear as fine hairlines (micro-scratches) or deeper grooves
‚Ä¢ Catch light at certain angles - appear bright or dark depending on angle
‚Ä¢ On holographic/chrome: disrupts rainbow pattern, very obvious
‚Ä¢ On matte: may appear as glossy line (coating disturbed)
‚Ä¢ On glossy: may appear as dull line or catch light

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Tilt card under light at various angles
‚Ä¢ Scratches catch light and become visible as lines
‚Ä¢ On refractor/chrome: look for pattern disruption in holographic effect
‚Ä¢ Check high-traffic areas: center, edges, anywhere handling occurs
‚Ä¢ Long scratches may cross entire surface

Severity Levels (UPDATED v5.6 - Stricter Deductions):
‚Ä¢ Micro-scratch: Hairline, only visible at angle with light ‚Üí **-0.5 to -0.75** (max 9.5)
‚Ä¢ Minor scratch: Clearly visible line, <1cm length ‚Üí **-1.0 to -1.5** (max 9.0)
‚Ä¢ Moderate scratch: Obvious scratch, 1-3cm or deeper ‚Üí **-1.5 to -2.5** (max 7.5)
‚Ä¢ Heavy scratch: Deep groove, long scratch, or multiple ‚Üí **-2.5 to -4.0** (max 6.0)

üö® **SCRATCH IMPACT NOTE:** Scratches are HIGH-IMPACT defects. Any scratch visible without

**üî¥ SURFACE SCUFFING / ABRASION**

‚Ä¢ Area of surface that appears dulled, matte, or worn
‚Ä¢ Differs from scratch (area vs line)
‚Ä¢ Loss of glossy finish in affected zone
‚Ä¢ May show directional texture from rubbing
‚Ä¢ Common on card surfaces from sliding against other cards

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Look for areas with different sheen than surrounding surface
‚Ä¢ On glossy cards: scuffed area appears matte or dull
‚Ä¢ On matte cards: scuffed area may appear glossy or slick
‚Ä¢ Check card center and edges (common contact points)

**üî¥ PRINT DEFECTS (Manufacturing)**

‚Ä¢ Print lines: Horizontal lines from roller marks in printing
‚Ä¢ Ink voids: Small areas where ink didn't adhere (tiny white spots in colored areas)
‚Ä¢ Ink spots: Extra ink deposits (colored dots where shouldn't be)
‚Ä¢ Registration issues: Colors slightly misaligned (fuzzy edges on design)
‚Ä¢ Smudging: Ink spread beyond intended area

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ Print defects are consistent with manufacturing (not handling)
‚Ä¢ Check print quality of text and detailed areas
‚Ä¢ Look for color registration at design boundaries
‚Ä¢ Examine solid color areas for voids or spots

**üî¥ DENTS / INDENTATIONS**
Visual Appearance:
‚Ä¢ Depressed area in card surface
‚Ä¢ Creates subtle shadow or highlight depending on lighting
‚Ä¢ May be from pressure damage (stacking, objects placed on card)
‚Ä¢ Surface bows inward at damage point
‚Ä¢ May or may not have associated crease

How to Detect:
‚Ä¢ View card at shallow angle with raking light
‚Ä¢ Indentation creates shadow in the depression
‚Ä¢ Run finger gently over surface (feel the dip) - but don't touch collectibles!
‚Ä¢ Examine card surface reflection - dent disrupts smooth reflection

**üî¥ CREASES / FOLDS (STRUCTURAL - GRADE CAP)**

‚Ä¢ Linear fold line where card was bent
‚Ä¢ Often shows white stress line along fold
‚Ä¢ Card surface broken at crease point
‚Ä¢ May be subtle (light crease) or severe (hard fold)
‚Ä¢ Permanent structural damage to cardstock

How to Detect:

‚Ä¢ Heavy crease/fold: Max grade 3.0-4.0

**üî¥ STAINING / DISCOLORATION**

‚Ä¢ Area of card with color different from original
‚Ä¢ May be yellowing (age), water damage, or contact stains
‚Ä¢ Wax staining: Oily residue from wax pack (yellowish spots)
‚Ä¢ Water staining: Ring marks or wavy discoloration patterns
‚Ä¢ Fingerprint oils: Visible fingerprint pattern in certain light

**üî¥ HOLOGRAPHIC / FOIL DAMAGE**

‚Ä¢ Foil scratches: Linear damage in holographic layer

[STEP 3A & 4A] CENTERING EVALUATION

STEP 1: MANDATORY CARD TYPE CLASSIFICATION
---
Before measuring, classify card into ONE category:

**TYPE A - Standard Bordered Card**
‚Ä¢ Clearly defined borders on all four edges
‚Ä¢ Straight rectangular shape with consistent borders
‚Ä¢ Borders are structural (integral to card design), not decorative
‚Ä¢ Method: Direct border measurement (most accurate)
‚Ä¢ Examples: Most vintage cards, modern base cards with borders

**TYPE B - Asymmetric Bordered Insert**
‚Ä¢ Borders differ intentionally left/right or top/bottom
‚Ä¢ Includes modern inserts (Dominators, My House, Fireworks, etc.)
‚Ä¢ Method: Design anchors required (borders unreliable)
‚Ä¢ Maximum centering score: 9.0 (unless exceptional anchor evidence)
‚Ä¢ Examples: Insert cards with asymmetric design elements

**TYPE C - Borderless / Full-Art Card**
‚Ä¢ No visible border OR artwork extends to card edges
‚Ä¢ Method: Internal layout anchors ONLY (no borders to measure)
‚Ä¢ Maximum centering score: 9.0 (unless strong anchors prove near-perfect)
‚Ä¢ Examples: Full-art Pokemon cards, borderless MTG cards

**TYPE D - Foil-Frame / Pattern-Frame Card**
‚Ä¢ Holographic or geometric frame (decorative, not structural)
‚Ä¢ Borders may appear "busy" or distorted by foil design
‚Ä¢ Method: Identify STRUCTURAL frame (ignore foil patterns)
‚Ä¢ Maximum centering score: 9.0 (if reflective distortion present)
‚Ä¢ Examples: Chrome/Prizm cards with holographic borders

**TYPE E - Die-Cut / Non-Rectangular Card**
‚Ä¢ Shaped edges, removed corners, jagged/rounded cuts
‚Ä¢ Method: Internal geometric centering only
‚Ä¢ Maximum centering score: 9.0 (traditional border centering impossible)
‚Ä¢ Examples: Die-cut inserts, shaped cards

**STEP 10B: Apply Floor Drag Rule**

‚Ä¢ If weakest_category ‚â• 9.0:
  ‚Üí **Preliminary Grade = weakest_category** (traditional weakest link)

‚Ä¢ If weakest_category = 8.0 to 8.5:

  ‚Üí This prevents high grades when one category is notably weak

‚Ä¢ If weakest_category < 8.0:
  ‚Üí **Preliminary Grade = MAX(weakest_category, category_average - 1.0)**
