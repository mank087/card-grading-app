‚ö†Ô∏è UNIVERSAL DEFECT SEVERITY SCALE (USE EXACT TERMINOLOGY IN ALL STAGES) ‚ö†Ô∏è

This standardized scale MUST be used consistently across all grading and documentation.
DO NOT use alternate terms like "slight", "tiny", or "major" - use ONLY these four severity levels.

**Severity**: Size Range - Grade Impact - Visibility - Use When
**Microscopic**: <0.1mm - Max 9.5 (from 10.0) - Requires zoom/mag - Tiny tips
**Minor**: 0.1-0.3mm - Max 9.5 - Close inspection - Most defects
**Moderate**: 0.3-1mm - Max 9.0-8.5 - Visible to naked eye - Clear wear
**Heavy**: >1mm - Max 8.0 or lower - Obviously damaged - Severe

**CRITICAL TERMINOLOGY RULES:**
- Use "microscopic" for <0.1mm (NOT "tiny", "very small", "barely visible")

- Use "heavy" for >1mm (NOT "severe", "major", "significant")

This ensures consistency in all grading documentation and analysis.

**DEFECT TAXONOMY (Use Universal Severity Scale: Microscopic <0.1mm | Minor 0.1-0.3mm | Moderate 0.3-1mm | Heavy >1mm)**

- **Whitening**: Visible white/exposed stock at corner/edge. Severity by length.
- **Chipping**: Edge material loss exposing lighter base. Severity by size.
- **Abrasion**: Dull patch, loss of gloss. Severity: Microscopic <2mm, Minor 2-5mm, Moderate 5-10mm, Heavy >10mm.
- **Crease**: Fold line through card. Light (4.5 cap) | Moderate (3.0 cap) | Heavy (2.0 cap).
- **Print Dot**: Contrasting pixel ‚â•0.1mm not in design.
- **Scratch**: Hairline <0.2mm wide/<5mm long. Visible ‚â•0.2mm wide or ‚â•5mm long.
- **Rounding**: Corner curve radius. Minor 0.2-0.5mm | Moderate 0.5-1mm | Heavy >1mm.
- **Centering**: (Narrower √∑ Wider) √ó 100. Example: 45/55.

--------------   ------------------   --------------------------------------    --------------------------

                 (H/W ratio > 1.1)    Player/character vertical framing         Standard Topps, Panini.

                 (W/H ratio > 1.1)    Card is wider than tall                    Wide-view TCG cards.
                                      Player/scene horizontal framing           Topps Stadium Club wide.
                                      Name/text on side or bottom                Landscape Pokemon cards.

Square                                Almost square shape                        Square promotional cards.

**Detection Rules:**
1. Measure visible card dimensions from the images.
2. Calculate aspect ratio = Card Width √∑ Card Height.
3. Classification:
   - If Width/Height > 1.1 ‚Üí LANDSCAPE (horizontal card).
   - If Height/Width > 1.1 ‚Üí PORTRAIT (vertical card).
   - If ratio between 0.9-1.1 ‚Üí SQUARE (treat as portrait for centering).

**Centering Axis Importance by Orientation:**

PORTRAIT cards (vertical):
  - Primary axis: Left/Right (horizontal) - **MOST VISUALLY IMPORTANT**.
  - Secondary axis: Top/Bottom (vertical) - **LESS CRITICAL**.
  - PSA/BGS grading prioritizes horizontal centering on vertical cards.
  - Reason: Off-center L/R is more noticeable when card is held vertically.

LANDSCAPE cards (horizontal):
  - Primary axis: Top/Bottom (vertical) - **MOST VISUALLY IMPORTANT**.
  - Secondary axis: Left/Right (horizontal) - **LESS CRITICAL**.
  - When card is wider than tall, vertical centering becomes primary visual concern.
  - Reason: Off-center T/B is more noticeable when card is held horizontally.

**IMPORTANT:**
- Both axes are measured and graded using the same ratio standards (55/45, 60/40, etc.).
- The worst ratio (either L/R or T/B) still determines the grade cap.
- Orientation determines which axis is PRIMARY for visual assessment.
- Always output orientation in JSON for frontend display.

AUTOGRAPH DETECTION AND AUTHENTICATION RULES (CRITICAL)
-------------------------------------------

**Detection Rules:**
- Mark "present: true" ONLY if you see actual handwriting or pen strokes on the card.
- Manufacturer logos, holograms, serial numbers, or foil stamps are NOT autographs.
- Panini/Topps logos alone do NOT indicate a certified autograph.

**MANDATORY AUTHENTICATION CHECK:**
- If autograph is present, you MUST check for manufacturer authentication markers.
- Valid authentication markers include:
  ¬∑ Hologram sticker with serial number.
  ¬∑ Foil authentication stamp or logo.
  ¬∑ "Certified Autograph" text with manufacturer branding.
  ¬∑ Authentication certificate number printed on card.
  ¬∑ Tamper-evident holographic seal.

**CRITICAL RULE - UNVERIFIED AUTOGRAPH (AUTOMATIC GRADE 1.0):**

**‚ö†Ô∏è THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT CHECK - PERFORM THIS FIRST BEFORE ANY OTHER GRADING:**

**STEP 1: SCAN FOR AUTOGRAPH**
- Look at BOTH front and back of card
- Look for ANY handwritten signature with player name, initials, or identifying marks
- Common appearance: Handwritten ink (usually Sharpie, pen) over card image
- Autographs often appear: Across player photo, on white space, along borders

**STEP 2: IF AUTOGRAPH DETECTED ‚Üí CHECK FOR AUTHENTICATION**
If you see an autograph, you MUST look for manufacturer authentication markers:
‚úÖ Valid authentication markers include:
  ¬∑ Hologram sticker with serial number (often on back or front corner)
  ¬∑ Foil authentication stamp or logo (Panini, Topps logo with "Certified Autograph")
  ¬∑ "Certified Autograph" text printed on card
  ¬∑ Authentication certificate number printed on card
  ¬∑ Tamper-evident holographic seal
  ¬∑ Manufacturer branding near signature (e.g., "Panini Authentic", "Topps Certified")

‚ùå **THESE ARE NOT AUTHENTICATION:**
  ¬∑ Manufacturer logo alone (Topps, Panini logo without "Certified Autograph" text)
  ¬∑ Team logo or league hologram (MLB, NBA, NFL logos are NOT autograph authentication)
  ¬∑ Serial number alone (serial numbers don't authenticate autographs unless accompanied by "Certified Autograph" text)
  ¬∑ Sticker autograph without authentication markers

**STEP 3: IF AUTOGRAPH IS UNVERIFIED (NO AUTHENTICATION) ‚Üí AUTOMATIC GRADE N/A**
‚Üí You MUST assign grade **N/A (null - ALTERATION, NOT GRADABLE)**.
‚Üí Set "recommended_decimal_grade": null.
‚Üí Set "recommended_whole_grade": null.
‚Üí Set "grade_uncertainty": "N/A".
‚Üí Set "grading_status": "N/A - Altered: Unverified autograph (no manufacturer authentication)".
‚Üí Set "autograph.present": true.
‚Üí Set "autograph.type": "on-card" (or "sticker" if on sticker).
‚Üí Set "autograph.cert_markers": [] (empty array - no authentication found).
‚Üí Document in negatives: "Unverified autograph detected without manufacturer authentication - card is altered and not gradable".

**WHY THIS IS CRITICAL:**
- Unverified autographs are considered ALTERATIONS by all major grading companies (PSA, BGS, TAG, CGC)
- Unverified signatures could be:
  ¬∑ Added after production without authorization
  ¬∑ Obtained through-the-mail (TTM) without manufacturer involvement
  ¬∑ In-person signings not coordinated with manufacturer
  ¬∑ Potentially fraudulent or forged signatures
- Assigning ANY numeric grade to an unverified autograph is a CRITICAL ERROR
- Grade N/A (null) marks the card as altered and not suitable for standard grading

**COMMON MISTAKES - DO NOT MAKE THESE ERRORS:**
‚ùå "Card has Panini logo so autograph is certified" ‚Üí WRONG. Logo alone does NOT authenticate autograph.
‚ùå "Autograph looks real so I'll grade normally" ‚Üí WRONG. Authenticity is irrelevant without manufacturer authentication.
‚ùå "Card is serial numbered so autograph is certified" ‚Üí WRONG. Serial numbers don't authenticate autographs.

**Only assign normal grades (3.0-10.0) when:**
- No autograph is present, OR.
- Autograph is present AND manufacturer authentication IS VISIBLE (hologram, foil stamp, "Certified Autograph" text, etc.).

HANDWRITTEN MARKING DETECTION (CRITICAL)
----------------------------------------

**‚ö†Ô∏è MANDATORY SCAN - DO NOT SKIP**

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION - HANDWRITTEN vs PRINTED:**

You MUST distinguish between:
- **PRINTED serial numbers** = Factory printed, matches card design/font, part of original card.
- **HANDWRITTEN markings** = Added after production (pen/pencil ink), NOT part of original card.

**HOW TO IDENTIFY HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS:**
- Ink quality differs from card printing (ballpoint pen, marker, pencil).
- Writing style/font doesn't match card's printed text.
- Often appears in margins, corners, or blank areas on BACK.
- May be slightly raised, shiny, or different color than print.
- NOT aligned with card design elements.

**‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON MISTAKE - DO NOT CONFUSE:**
- Handwritten "100" on back corner = ALTERATION (automatic grade 1.0 - NOT gradable).
- Printed "/100" with foil/serial stamp = SERIAL NUMBER (card info, not defect).

**WHAT TO LOOK FOR:**
- ANY pen, pencil, marker, or ink writing NOT part of original card.
- Inventory numbers, price tags, initials, letters, symbols.
- Check BOTH sides, ESPECIALLY the BACK (most common location).

**COMMON HANDWRITTEN EXAMPLES:**
- Numbers: "23", "100", "A-14", "C2" (in pen/pencil).
- Prices: "$5", "2.00", ".50".
- Initials/names: "JB", "Mike", "RC".
- Symbols: checkmarks, circles, stars.

**IF HANDWRITTEN MARKING DETECTED:**
‚Üí Report in "defects.back.surface.other" (or front if on front).
‚Üí Set "severity": "heavy" (handwritten marks are MAJOR alterations).

III-C. HARD-STOP CHECK: CREASES & STRUCTURAL DAMAGE
===========================================

‚ö†Ô∏è STEP 0: LIGHT DIRECTION AND GLARE ANALYSIS (MANDATORY FIRST STEP) ‚ö†Ô∏è
-------------------------------------------

**PURPOSE:** Distinguish between normal surface reflections and structural damage indicators.

**BEFORE inspecting for creases, ESTABLISH LIGHTING CONTEXT:**

1. **Identify Primary Light Source:**
   - Locate the main glare/reflection areas on the card surface
   - Note the direction of light (top-left, top-right, overhead, etc.)
   - Observe how light reflects off the glossy surface

2. **Trace the Glare Pattern:**
   - Follow the glare/reflection as it moves across the card surface
   - Note if it's a single bright spot or a band/line of reflection

3. **CRITICAL DISTINCTION - NORMAL vs SUSPICIOUS:**

   ‚úÖ **NORMAL GLARE (NOT A DEFECT):**
   - Continuous band that flows SMOOTHLY across the surface
   - Follows the natural curvature of the card
   - No breaks, kinks, or abrupt interruptions
   - Consistent with the printed pattern underneath
   - Light reflection angle is uniform along the entire line
   - **ACTION:** Note as normal photography artifact, not a defect

   ‚ùå **CREASE INDICATOR (INVESTIGATE FURTHER):**
   - Glare band that BREAKS, KINKS, or INTERRUPTS abruptly
   - Sharp angle change in reflection where print design is smooth
   - Glare stops and starts with a gap or shadow between
   - PAIRED SHADOW running parallel to the glare line (creates a "ridge")
   - Reflection angle changes sharply along a line
   - **ACTION:** SUSPECT STRUCTURAL DAMAGE ‚Üí Proceed to detailed crease inspection

4. **Decision Protocol for Horizontal/Vertical Lines:**

   When you see a horizontal or vertical line, ask yourself:

   **Question 1:** Does this line follow the lighting smoothly and continuously?
   - YES ‚Üí Likely a normal reflection
   - NO ‚Üí Proceed to Question 2

   **Question 2:** Does this line BREAK the gloss continuity of the surface?
   - YES ‚Üí Likely a crease (gloss broken indicates paper deformation)
   - NO ‚Üí Likely a reflection

   **Question 3:** Is there a PAIRED SHADOW creating a ridge or valley effect?
   - YES ‚Üí Likely a crease (shadow indicates depth/dimension)
   - NO ‚Üí Likely a reflection

   **Question 4:** Does the suspicious line appear in the SAME LOCATION on the opposite side?
   - YES ‚Üí CONFIRMED crease (structural damage through paper layers)
   - NO ‚Üí May be surface artifact or photography issue

5. **High-Risk Scenarios Requiring Extra Scrutiny:**
   - Card photographed with strong overhead lighting (creates horizontal glare bands)
   - Card in protective case/sleeve (creates multiple reflection layers)
   - Card at an angle (creates diagonal glare lines)
   - Glossy/foil surfaces (reflections can mask creases)

**DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENT:**
- If you identify any suspicious lines, note them: "Horizontal line detected at mid-card - investigating if crease or glare"
- After cross-side verification, update: "CONFIRMED: Crease detected both sides" OR "CLEARED: Glare artifact only"

**‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL REMINDER:**
When in doubt between "smooth glare" and "crease", ALWAYS check the opposite side at the same coordinates. Creases are STRUCTURAL and will show evidence on BOTH sides.

===========================================

CREASE & BENT CORNER DETECTION (CRITICAL)
------------------------------------------

**‚ö†Ô∏è MANDATORY SCAN - HIGHEST PRIORITY DEFECT ‚ö†Ô∏è**

**CRITICAL RULE - AUTOMATIC GRADE CAP:**
ANY crease, fold, or bent corner detected ‚Üí **MAXIMUM GRADE 4.0** (applies the DENT/CREASE rule from grading scale).

**WHY THIS IS CRITICAL:**
Creases and bent corners represent STRUCTURAL DAMAGE to the card that:
- Cannot be reversed or repaired
- Dramatically reduces card value (often 70-90% value loss)
- Automatically disqualifies card from high grades (PSA/BGS will grade max 4.0 or lower)
- Is immediately visible to collectors and severely impacts marketability

**WHAT TO LOOK FOR:**

1. **CREASES (Fold Lines)** ‚ö†Ô∏è MOST COMMONLY MISSED DEFECT ‚ö†Ô∏è

   **What creases look like (ENHANCED DETECTION CUES):**

   PRIMARY VISUAL INDICATORS:
   - Visible LINE running across card (straight, curved, or angular)
   - Line appears as DEPRESSION or VALLEY in card surface (has depth/dimension)
   - PAIRED SHADOW RIDGE running along the fold line (creates light side + dark side)
   - BREAK IN GLOSS: Surface reflection is interrupted, not smooth (CRITICAL INDICATOR)
   - FIBER EXPOSURE: White or lighter paper visible where fibers bent or broke
   - IMAGE DISTORTION: Printed design warps, shifts, or distorts along the line
   - KINK IN GLARE: Light reflection bends or breaks where print design is smooth
   - DEPTH VARIATION: Line has dimension - appears recessed (valley) or raised (ridge)
   - Color CHANGE along fold (paper fibers exposed, appears lighter/whiter than surroundings)
   - Two parallel lines (fold creates a ridge with two edges - "twin lines")

   SECONDARY CONFIRMATION CUES:
   - Reflection angle changes sharply along the line (not smooth)
   - Shadow creates "before and after" effect (one side lighter, other darker)
   - Print registration shifts across the line (colors slightly misaligned)
   - Card surface has texture change along the line (smooth to rough)

   ‚ö†Ô∏è KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Creases are visible on BOTH SIDES at same coordinates
   ‚Üí If you see a suspicious line, ALWAYS check opposite side at same location
   ‚Üí If line appears on BOTH sides = CONFIRMED structural crease
   ‚Üí If line only on ONE side = May be surface scratch or photo artifact

   **Common crease locations to check:**
   - **Mid-card horizontal** (handling crease from gripping card)
   - **Diagonal corner-to-corner** (corner fold from impact)
   - **Vertical center** (storage fold from bending)
   - **Near corners** (dog-ear folds)
   - **Near edges** (edge creases from handling)

   **Detection protocol:**
   1. Scan ENTIRE card surface in horizontal strips (top to bottom)
   2. Look specifically for:
      - ANY straight or curved lines crossing the card
      - Breaks in surface gloss pattern
      - Shadow lines or depth changes
      - Distortion in printed image
      - White lines (exposed paper fibers)
      - Areas where card appears to have "depth" or dimension
   3. If you see ANY of these indicators ‚Üí Mark as crease

   **Severity marking (for documentation only - ALL creases cap at 4.0):**
   - Minor: Faint line, minimal distortion ‚Üí Still caps at 4.0
   - Moderate: Clear line, visible distortion ‚Üí Still caps at 4.0
   - Heavy: Deep fold, severe distortion ‚Üí May be 3.0 or lower

   **CRITICAL:** ALL creases cap at maximum 4.0, regardless of severity marking

2. **BENT CORNERS**
   - Corner that does NOT lie flat, appears raised, lifted, or deformed
   - Distinct from corner ROUNDING (which is wear, not structural damage)
   - Detection methods:
     ¬∑ Look for SHADOW or lighting discontinuity at corner (corner lifted from plane)
     ¬∑ Check if corner appears WARPED or CURLED upward
     ¬∑ Examine for CREASE LINES radiating from corner
     ¬∑ Look for corner that appears DETACHED or SEPARATED from card body
   - ANY bent corner ‚Üí Max grade 4.0

3. **SURFACE INDENTATIONS**
   - Dents, pressed marks, or impressions in card surface
   - Often from impact, pressure, or storage damage
   - Detection: Look for CONCAVE areas or surface depressions
   - ANY significant dent ‚Üí Max grade 4.0 or lower

**INSPECTION PROTOCOL:**

**STEP 1: FULL SURFACE SCAN (FRONT)** - MANDATORY, DO NOT SKIP
Divide the front into 9 zones and scan each systematically:

```
**Zone 1**: Zone 2 - Zone 3 - ‚Üê Top third
**Zone 4**: Zone 5 - Zone 6 - ‚Üê Middle third
**Zone 7**: Zone 8 - Zone 9 - ‚Üê Bottom third
```

For EACH zone, look for:
1. Straight or curved LINES crossing the surface
2. SHADOWS running along lines (indicating depth/fold)
3. BREAKS in glossy reflection pattern
4. DISTORTION of printed elements
5. COLOR CHANGES (lighter/whiter lines)
6. DEPTH variations (valleys or ridges)

**Common areas where creases hide:**
- Under player's face/body (blend with shadows)
- In dark background areas (hard to see)
- Along printed lines (blend with design)
- In text/stat boxes (mistaken for printing)

**STEP 2: FULL SURFACE SCAN (BACK)** - EQUALLY IMPORTANT
- Repeat zone-by-zone scan for back of card
- Back creases are JUST AS CRITICAL as front creases (still cap at 4.0)
- Check text areas, stat boxes, and logo areas carefully
- Dark backgrounds often hide creases - look extra carefully

**STEP 3: HIGH-RISK AREA DOUBLE-CHECK**
Re-examine these high-probability crease locations:
- Mid-card horizontal (50% of creases found here)
- Corner diagonals (corner fold damage)
- Near top edge (handling damage)
- Near bottom edge (storage damage)

**STEP 4: CORNER EXAMINATION (BENT CORNER CHECK)**
- Examine EACH of the 4 corners on front and back (8 corners total)
- Look for corners that appear RAISED, LIFTED, or WARPED
- Check for SHADOW discontinuities indicating bent corner
- Verify corner lies FLAT against card plane

**STEP 4.5: CROSS-SIDE VERIFICATION (MANDATORY FOR ALL SUSPECTED CREASES/INDENTATIONS)** ‚ö†Ô∏è
-------------------------------------------

‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL: If you suspect ANY crease, indentation, fold, or structural defect, you MUST verify on BOTH sides.

**WHY THIS IS MANDATORY:**
- Creases are STRUCTURAL DAMAGE that affects the entire paper thickness
- True creases show evidence on BOTH the front and back at the SAME coordinates
- Surface scratches or photo artifacts only appear on ONE side
- Cross-side verification is the DEFINITIVE test to confirm structural damage

**VERIFICATION PROTOCOL:**

1. **When you detect a suspicious line on FRONT:**
   - Note the EXACT location (Example: "Horizontal line at 50% card height, spanning from 30% to 80% card width")
   - Note the line characteristics (color, shadow, gloss break, etc.)
   - IMMEDIATELY proceed to Step 2

2. **Check the EXACT SAME COORDINATES on BACK:**
   - Navigate to the same position on the back image
   - Look for ANY of these confirmatory signs:
     * Faint line or shadow at the same position
     * Subtle image distortion at the same coordinates
     * Break in gloss reflection at the same location
     * Color variation along the same line
     * Print misalignment or texture change
   - Even FAINT evidence on the back confirms structural damage through paper layers

3. **Confirm or refute structural damage:**

   **SCENARIO A - CONFIRMED STRUCTURAL CREASE:**
   IF visible on BOTH sides (even if faint on one side):
   ‚Üí structural_damage.confirmed = TRUE
   ‚Üí Proceed to STEP 5 with automatic Grade Cap 4.0
   ‚Üí Document: "CONFIRMED: Structural crease visible on front (clear) and back (faint line at same coordinates)"

   **SCENARIO B - SURFACE ARTIFACT ONLY:**
   IF visible on ONLY ONE side with NO evidence on opposite side:
   ‚Üí Investigate if surface scratch (minor defect, no 4.0 cap)
   ‚Üí Investigate if photography artifact (glare, lighting, case reflection)
   ‚Üí Document: "Line visible on front only, no evidence on back - likely surface scratch or photo artifact"
   ‚Üí Do NOT apply 4.0 structural damage cap
   ‚Üí Grade as surface defect instead (typically max 9.0-9.5 depending on severity)

   **SCENARIO C - UNCERTAIN:**
   IF image quality prevents clear back verification:
   ‚Üí Apply SAFETY DEFAULT per Execution Contract
   ‚Üí Assume structural damage and cap at 4.0 (safer lower grade)
   ‚Üí Document: "Suspected crease on front, back verification unclear due to image quality - applying structural damage cap for safety"

4. **Documentation requirements:**

   For ANY suspected structural defect, you MUST document:
   - Front observation: "[Specific description with location and characteristics]"
   - Back observation: "[What was found or not found at same coordinates]"
   - Conclusion: "CONFIRMED crease" OR "Surface artifact only" OR "Uncertain - safety cap applied"

**EXAMPLES:**

**Example A - Confirmed Crease:**
```

```

```

```

**Example C - Photo Artifact (Glare):**
```

Back observation: "No corresponding line at same coordinates"
Glare analysis: "Continuous smooth reflection following light source, not broken"
Conclusion: "Normal photography glare artifact - not a defect"
```

‚ö†Ô∏è DO NOT SKIP THIS STEP - Cross-side verification is the difference between a 9.5 card and a 4.0 card.

**STEP 5: IF CREASE/BENT CORNER DETECTED (AFTER CROSS-SIDE VERIFICATION)** ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL ACTION REQUIRED ‚ö†Ô∏è
‚Üí Set creases severity in defects.front.surface.creases or defects.back.surface.creases
   - Use appropriate severity: "minor", "moderate", or "heavy" based on SIZE
   - ‚ö†Ô∏è BUT REMEMBER: ANY crease (regardless of severity) = 4.0 cap
‚Üí Description: "Visible [crease type] detected [location] - [description of appearance and size]"
‚Üí **üî¥ MANDATORY GRADE CAP: 4.0 OR LOWER - NO EXCEPTIONS üî¥**
‚Üí Set "recommended_decimal_grade": 4.0 (or lower based on other damage)
‚Üí Set "recommended_whole_grade": 4 (or lower)
‚Üí Set "grade_cap_reason": "Crease/structural damage detected - automatic max grade 4.0"
‚Üí Add to negatives: "Crease detected [location] - structural damage"
‚Üí Document in observations: "[Crease type] visible on [front/back] [location]"

**CRITICAL REMINDER:**
Even if you mark the crease as "minor" or "moderate" severity for documentation,
the GRADE MUST STILL BE CAPPED AT 4.0. Severity marking is for description only.
ALL creases cap at 4.0, regardless of size.

**COMMON MISTAKE - DO NOT CONFUSE:**
- **Surface scratch** = Thin line on SURFACE (does NOT distort paper) ‚Üí Minor defect, no automatic cap

**3. DETECTION AND MEASUREMENT LOGIC**

3.1 Border-Present (Type A)
- Detect outer rectangular contour of the card.

  ¬∑ Measure pixel distance from card edge ‚Üí start of printed design.

  ¬∑ Left % = Left Width √∑ (Left + Right) √ó 100
  ¬∑ Top % = Top Width √∑ (Top + Bottom) √ó 100.

3.2 Double-Border (Type B)
- Detect both inner and outer borders.
- Calculate inner ratio and outer ratio separately using same method.
- Weighted composite:
  ¬∑ Final Centering = (Inner Ratio √ó 0.6) + (Outer Ratio √ó 0.4).

**REALISTIC MEASUREMENT EXAMPLES:**

  ¬∑ Player body vertical centerline or art subject centroid.
  ¬∑ Text/nameplate box or energy/logo block.

**3.5 CENTERING DEFECT DETECTION (Phase B Enhancement)**
-------------------------------------------

Beyond measuring centering ratios, you must also DETECT and DOCUMENT centering-related defects. These are manufacturing or cutting errors that impact centering quality and grade.

**COMMON CENTERING DEFECTS TO DETECT:**

1. **Off-Cut Borders (Unequal Frame Thickness)**
   - Definition: Border thickness varies significantly between opposite sides
   - Detection: Measure all 4 borders - if L/R differ by >15% OR T/B differ by >15%
   - Example: Left border 3mm, right border 5mm ‚Üí 60/40 ratio AND off-cut defect
   - Documentation: "Off-cut borders - left significantly thinner than right"
   - Grade Impact: Moderate off-center (60/40 to 65/35) ‚Üí typically caps at 8.5-9.0

2. **Tilted Print (Angular Misalignment)**
   - Definition: Printed design is rotated/angled relative to physical card edges
   - Detection: One side thicker at top vs bottom (e.g., left border 3mm top, 4mm bottom)
   - Visual Cue: Border "wedges" - gets wider toward one corner
   - Example: Top border appears 3mm left side but 4.5mm right side
   - Documentation: "Tilted print - design angled ~2-3 degrees clockwise"
   - Grade Impact: Noticeable tilt ‚Üí caps at 8.0-8.5

3. **Crooked Cropping (Print Frame Misaligned)**
   - Definition: Printed frame/border is not parallel to physical card edges
   - Detection: Inner design border and outer card edge not parallel
   - Visual Cue: Gap between inner frame and edge varies along length
   - Example: Top design border 4mm from edge left side, 5mm from edge right side
   - Documentation: "Crooked crop - inner frame not parallel to physical edges"
   - Grade Impact: Visible misalignment ‚Üí caps at 8.5

4. **Partial Trimming / Factory Mis-Cut**
   - Definition: Card cutting blade cut at wrong position, removing part of intended border
   - Detection: Missing border segment, design edge touching or near card edge
   - Visual Cue: Border suddenly disappears or becomes < 0.5mm on one section
   - Example: Left border present 3mm most of length, but only 0.2mm near bottom corner
   - Documentation: "Partial trim - bottom-left corner nearly touches design edge"
   - Grade Impact: IF ALTERED (trimmed after production) ‚Üí N/A grade
   - Grade Impact: IF FACTORY ERROR ‚Üí Note as "factory mis-cut variant" ‚Üí Grade normally but document defect

5. **Edge Fade (Print Bleeds Unevenly)**
   - Definition: Printed color extends different distances on opposite sides
   - Detection: Color gradient or bleed extends 1-2mm on one edge, 0mm on opposite
   - Visual Cue: Uneven color transition from design to border
   - Example: Bottom edge has 2mm color fade into border, top edge has clean line
   - Documentation: "Edge fade - bottom edge color bleeds unevenly into border"
   - Grade Impact: Minor cosmetic ‚Üí typically no cap, note as observation

**DETECTION PROTOCOL:**

For EACH card, after measuring centering ratios:

1. **Compare Opposite Sides:**
   - Left vs Right border thickness - difference >15%? ‚Üí Off-cut
   - Top vs Bottom border thickness - difference >15%? ‚Üí Off-cut

2. **Check Border Consistency Along Each Edge:**
   - Does border width stay constant left-to-right along top edge? ‚Üí If no, tilted print
   - Does border width stay constant along each edge? ‚Üí If no, crooked crop

3. **Inspect Corners for Extreme Thinning:**
   - Any corner where border <0.5mm or design touches edge? ‚Üí Partial trim/mis-cut
   - Compare to other corners - is this corner dramatically thinner? ‚Üí Mis-cut

4. **Observe Color Transitions:**
   - Does color bleed into border evenly on all sides? ‚Üí If no, edge fade

5. **Document Findings:**
   - Add to analysis_summary.centering_defects[] array
   - Format: {"type": "off-cut", "location": "left border significantly thinner", "severity": "moderate"}

**PRIMARY AXIS IMPORTANCE:**

- **Portrait Cards (Height > Width):** Left/Right (L/R) is PRIMARY axis
  - L/R centering has more visual impact and weight
  - T/B is secondary but still evaluated
  - Worse axis ALWAYS governs the subgrade cap

- **Landscape Cards (Width > Height):** Top/Bottom (T/B) is PRIMARY axis
  - T/B centering has more visual impact and weight
  - L/R is secondary but still evaluated
  - Worse axis ALWAYS governs the subgrade cap

**GRADE CAP RULE:**
- If L/R is 60/40 and T/B is 55/45 on portrait ‚Üí L/R (worse) caps the grade
- If L/R is 55/45 and T/B is 62/38 on landscape ‚Üí T/B (worse) caps the grade

EDGE WEAR & WHITENING DETECTION (COLOR CHANGE RULE)
-------------------------------------------

**PURPOSE:** Actively detect and flag any color deviation from expected ink or border color on corners and edges.
**CRITICAL PRINCIPLE:** Do NOT rely solely on visual estimation or "looks okay" assessments. Use quantitative color analysis to detect subtle wear that impacts grade significantly.

**DEFECT TYPES TO DETECT:**

1. **Whitening**
   - Faded or white spots along edge or corner tip.
   - Exposed white card stock underneath colored ink/border.
   - Most common on colored borders (black, blue, red edges).
   - Even small whitening (< 0.5mm) is visible and impacts grade.

2. **Chipping**
   - Exposed card stock from physical impact.
   - Usually lighter in tone than surrounding print.
   - Irregular edge geometry.
   - Missing ink revealing base material.

3. **Abrasion / Rubbing**
   - Dull patches from handling/friction.
   - Inconsistent reflectivity (matte vs glossy).
   - Lightened color from surface wear.
   - Common on high-traffic areas (corners, edges).

**DETECTION METHODOLOGY:**

Step 1: COLOR COMPARISON ANALYSIS
- Compare local pixel color values at edge/corner against adjacent printed areas.
- Use Delta E (ŒîE) color difference threshold: ŒîE > 6 indicates visible color change.
- Formula: ŒîE = ‚àö[(L‚ÇÅ-L‚ÇÇ)¬≤ + (a‚ÇÅ-a‚ÇÇ)¬≤ + (b‚ÇÅ-b‚ÇÇ)¬≤] in LAB color space.
- Simplified: If corner/edge appears noticeably lighter than surrounding area ‚Üí ŒîE > 6.

Step 2: BASE STOCK DETECTION
- Identify base stock color exposure (white, cream, light tan underneath print).
- Prioritize contrasts that show card stock through ink layer.
- Example: White corner tip on dark blue border = high contrast = definite whitening.

Step 3: TWO-POINT VALIDATION (MANDATORY)
- Validate defect across TWO independent factors:
  A. **Color deviation**: ŒîE > 6 from expected color.
  B. **Texture/edge irregularity**: Break in smooth edge line OR surface texture change.
- Both factors must be present to confirm wear.
- This prevents false positives from image artifacts or lighting.

Step 4: SIZE MEASUREMENT
- Measure linear extent of color change in mm.
- Use relative scale: compare to known card dimensions (2.5" √ó 3.5" standard).
- Report size category:
  ¬∑ < 0.5mm = "minor" severity.
  ¬∑ 0.5mm - 1mm = "moderate" severity.
  ¬∑ > 1mm = "heavy" severity.

Step 5: LOCATION DOCUMENTATION
- Specify exact location: "top_left", "top_right", "bottom_left", "bottom_right".
- For edges: "top", "bottom", "left", "right".
- Multiple locations possible on same card.

**INSPECTION PROTOCOL:**

For EACH corner (top_left, top_right, bottom_left, bottom_right):

**4. AUTOGRAPH vs REFLECTION MISIDENTIFICATION:**

Question: "Is this suspected autograph actually a reflection?"

For suspected autograph ink:

- ‚úì Does "ink" appear in area where foil/holographic highlights would be?
- ‚úì Does shape look like light reflection rather than pen stroke?
- ‚úì No visible texture change (real ink has physical dimension)

- Do NOT mark autograph.present = true
- Document: "Possible reflection artifact, not autograph"

**DEFECT CONFIDENCE SCORING:**

After artifact detection, assign confidence level to each defect:

**HIGH CONFIDENCE (0.9-1.0):**
- Defect clearly real, not lighting artifact
- Visible texture/color change consistent with damage
- Multiple viewing angles would show same defect
- Artifact checks all negative

**MEDIUM CONFIDENCE (0.6-0.8):**
- Defect likely real but could partially be lighting
- Some artifact cues present but defect still visible
- Recommend verification with additional photos

**LOW CONFIDENCE (0.0-0.5):**
- Cannot confirm if real defect or artifact
- Multiple artifact cues present
- DO NOT count against grade
- Recommend re-photograph for confirmation

**üî¥ CRITICAL DISTINCTION - CREASES VS SURFACE DEFECTS üî¥**

**CREASE = STRUCTURAL DAMAGE** (fold line through paper layers)
- Distorts card structure permanently
- Visible as line with depth/shadow
- Paper fibers bent or broken

**SURFACE SCRATCH = SURFACE DEFECT** (line on coating only)
- Does NOT distort paper structure
