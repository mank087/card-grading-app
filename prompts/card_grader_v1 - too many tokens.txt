EDGE WEAR & WHITENING DETECTION (COLOR CHANGE RULE)
===========================================

**PURPOSE:** Actively detect and flag any color deviation from expected ink or border color on corners and edges.

**CRITICAL PRINCIPLE:**
Do NOT rely solely on visual estimation or "looks okay" assessments. Use quantitative color analysis to detect subtle wear that impacts grade significantly.

**DEFECT TYPES TO DETECT:**

1. **Whitening**
   - Faded or white spots along edge or corner tip
   - Exposed white card stock underneath colored ink/border
   - Most common on colored borders (black, blue, red edges)
   - Even small whitening (< 0.5mm) is visible and impacts grade

2. **Chipping**
   - Exposed card stock from physical impact
   - Usually lighter in tone than surrounding print
   - Irregular edge geometry
   - Missing ink revealing base material

3. **Abrasion / Rubbing**
   - Dull patches from handling/friction
   - Inconsistent reflectivity (matte vs glossy)
   - Lightened color from surface wear
   - Common on high-traffic areas (corners, edges)

**DETECTION METHODOLOGY:**

Step 1: COLOR COMPARISON ANALYSIS
- Compare local pixel color values at edge/corner against adjacent printed areas
- Use Delta E (ΔE) color difference threshold: ΔE > 6 indicates visible color change
- Formula: ΔE = √[(L₁-L₂)² + (a₁-a₂)² + (b₁-b₂)²] in LAB color space
- Simplified: If corner/edge appears noticeably lighter than surrounding area → ΔE > 6

Step 2: BASE STOCK DETECTION
- Identify base stock color exposure (white, cream, light tan underneath print)
- Prioritize contrasts that show card stock through ink layer
- Example: White corner tip on dark blue border = high contrast = definite whitening

Step 3: TWO-POINT VALIDATION (MANDATORY)
- Validate defect across TWO independent factors:
  A. **Color deviation**: ΔE > 6 from expected color
  B. **Texture/edge irregularity**: Break in smooth edge line OR surface texture change
- Both factors must be present to confirm wear
- This prevents false positives from image artifacts or lighting

Step 4: SIZE MEASUREMENT
- Measure linear extent of color change in mm
- Use relative scale: compare to known card dimensions (2.5" × 3.5" standard)
- Report size category:
  · < 0.5mm = "minor" severity
  · 0.5mm - 1mm = "moderate" severity
  · > 1mm = "heavy" severity

Step 5: LOCATION DOCUMENTATION
- Specify exact location: "top_left", "top_right", "bottom_left", "bottom_right"
- For edges: "top", "bottom", "left", "right"
- Multiple locations possible on same card

**INSPECTION PROTOCOL:**

For EACH corner (top_left, top_right, bottom_left, bottom_right):
1. Zoom to corner area in image
2. Compare corner color to adjacent border/design color
3. Look for lightened areas, exposed white, or color shift
4. If color change detected → Measure size → Classify severity
5. Check for edge smoothness (texture validation)
6. Document in JSON with severity and description

For EACH edge (top, bottom, left, right):
1. Scan along entire edge length
2. Look for roughness, chipping, or uneven cutting
3. Check for color deviation from expected edge color
4. If irregularity found → Measure extent → Classify severity
5. Document location and characteristics

**EXAMPLES OF VALID DETECTIONS:**

✅ "Top-left corner shows 0.3mm white tip exposure on dark border edge - ΔE ~15 compared to surrounding blue ink" (minor whitening)

✅ "Right edge has 0.8mm chip at midpoint revealing light tan base stock against red border - ΔE ~20" (moderate chipping)

✅ "Bottom-right corner exhibits 1.2mm of rounding with white exposure along curved edge - surface texture shows paper layer" (heavy wear)

✅ "All four corners show micro-whitening < 0.2mm at tips - barely visible but detectable color shift ΔE ~8" (minor wear, multiple locations)

**EXAMPLES OF FALSE POSITIVES (AVOID):**

❌ "Corner appears slightly lighter" (too vague, no measurement)
❌ "Edge looks worn" (no color analysis, no size)
❌ "Possible whitening" (uncertain - validate or rule out)
❌ Reflections or glare mistaken for whitening (check multiple angles)
❌ Border design elements that are intentionally white/light colored

**SEVERITY IMPACT ON GRADING:**

- **Minor whitening (< 0.5mm)** on ONE corner: Max grade 9.5
- **Minor whitening on TWO corners**: Max grade 9.0
- **Minor whitening on THREE+ corners**: Max grade 8.5
- **Moderate whitening (0.5-1mm)** on ANY corner: Max grade 8.5
- **Heavy whitening (> 1mm)** or **chipping**: Max grade 8.0 or lower
- **Edge roughness** without color change: Grade reduction of 0.5 points
- **Abrasion** visible on surface: Grade reduction based on size/prominence

**KEY REMINDERS:**
- Even MINOR whitening (< 0.5mm) is a defect - do not overlook
- Use ΔE > 6 as threshold for "visible" color change
- Two-point validation prevents false positives
- Document all findings with size measurements
- Be thorough - check all 4 corners and all 4 edges systematically

**Segmentation Method:**

   - Check for color deviation (ΔE using context-specific threshold)

   - Measure any defect extent within that segment

3. **Document location precisely:**

4. **Aggregate findings:**
   - If multiple segments on same edge show defects → Document as continuous or multiple discrete defects

  · Exposed white/gray stock underneath
  · Matte appearance where foil is worn away
- Document: "Foil edge shows factory color variance - not damage" OR "Foil worn away revealing stock - genuine wear"

**Centering Axis Importance by Orientation:**

PORTRAIT cards (vertical):
  - Primary axis: Left/Right (horizontal) - MOST VISUALLY IMPORTANT
  - Secondary axis: Top/Bottom (vertical) - LESS CRITICAL

  - Primary axis: Top/Bottom (vertical) - MOST VISUALLY IMPORTANT
  - Secondary axis: Left/Right (horizontal) - LESS CRITICAL
  - When card is wider than tall, vertical centering becomes primary visual concern
  - Reason: Off-center T/B is more noticeable when card is held horizontally

**IMPORTANT:**
- Both axes are measured and graded using the same ratio standards (55/45, 60/40, etc.)
- The worst ratio (either L/R or T/B) still determines the grade cap

- Always output orientation in JSON for frontend display

3. DETECTION AND MEASUREMENT LOGIC

3.1 Border-Present (Type A)
- Detect outer rectangular contour of the card.
- Detect printed content region by color threshold contrast (> 8% luminance difference).
- For each side (L, R, T, B):
  · Measure pixel distance from card edge → start of printed design.
- Compute ratios:
  · Left % = Left Width ÷ (Left + Right) × 100
  · Top % = Top Width ÷ (Top + Bottom) × 100
- Format: "Left/Right = 48/52" → 52% right heavy.

**REALISTIC MEASUREMENT EXAMPLES:**
- Example A: Left border 4.1mm, Right border 3.9mm → 51/49 (excellent, slight left-heavy)
- Example B: Left border 3.7mm, Right border 4.5mm → 45/55 (moderate right-heavy)
- Example C: Left border 4.0mm, Right border 4.0mm → IF truly equal, report 50/50 WITH evidence. Most likely one is 3.95mm and other is 4.05mm → 49/51
- Example D: Top border 3.2mm, Bottom border 4.8mm → 40/60 (significant bottom-heavy, caps grade)
- Example E: Left border 3.9mm, Right border 4.1mm → 49/51 (near-perfect, slight right-heavy)

3.2 Double-Border (Type B)
- Detect both inner and outer borders.
- Calculate inner ratio and outer ratio separately using same method.
- Weighted composite:
  · Final Centering = (Inner Ratio × 0.6) + (Outer Ratio × 0.4).
- Purpose: mitigate border printing variability.

**REALISTIC MEASUREMENT EXAMPLES:**
- Example A: Inner border L/R = 48/52, Outer border L/R = 51/49 → Composite: (48×0.6) + (51×0.4) = 49.2/50.8 → Report as 49/51
- Example B: Inner border T/B = 46/54, Outer border T/B = 47/53 → Composite: (46×0.6) + (47×0.4) = 46.4/53.6 → Report as 46/54
- Example C: Inner border perfectly centered (50/50 with evidence), Outer border 52/48 → Composite: (50×0.6) + (52×0.4) = 50.8/49.2 → Report as 51/49

3.3 Full-Bleed (Type C)
- No defined border; detect design anchors:
  · Player body vertical centerline or art subject centroid.
  · Text/nameplate box or energy/logo block.
- For horizontal: measure distance from subject center to L/R edges.
- For vertical: measure distance from text/logo baseline to T/B edges.
- Ratio output identical format (Left/Right, Top/Bottom).
- Allow up to ±2% subject asymmetry tolerance (for natural image framing).

**REALISTIC MEASUREMENT EXAMPLES:**
- Example A: Player centerline 47% from left edge, 53% from right edge → Report as 47/53
- Example B: Pokemon character center 42% from left, 58% from right → Report as 42/58 (moderate off-center)
- Example C: Text box baseline 51% from top, 49% from bottom → Report as 51/49 (excellent centering)
- Example D: Logo placement 46% from left, 54% from right → Report as 46/54
- Example E: Subject naturally asymmetric (action pose leaning right) - centerline 48% from left, 52% from right → Report as 48/52 (within tolerance)

3.4 Asymmetric Designs (Type D)
- Identify design axes using internal geometry (text column, frame line).
- Measure distance of those fixed elements to card edges.
- Evaluate uniformity between repeated design elements (e.g., border stripe width).
- Compute ratio to nearest mirrored design feature if present; else use geometric card center as reference.
- Flag "method_front": "design-anchor-asymmetric" in JSON.

**REALISTIC MEASUREMENT EXAMPLES:**
- Example A: Text column 52% from left edge, 48% from right edge → Report as 52/48 (design intentionally left-heavy)
- Example B: Frame line geometry 45% from top, 55% from bottom → Report as 45/55
- Example C: Repeating border stripe: Left stripe 3.1mm wide, Right stripe 2.9mm wide → Report as 52/48
- Example D: Asymmetric art with fixed logo anchor: Logo 43% from left, 57% from right → Report as 43/57

3.5 CRITICAL MEASUREMENT VALIDATION (MANDATORY)

**PROBLEM:** AI models tend to default to 50/50 when uncertain. This is UNACCEPTABLE.

**REALITY CHECK:**
- Physical manufacturing cards NEVER have perfect 50/50 centering
- Even "gem mint" cards typically measure 51/49 to 54/46
- True 50/50 centering should occur in < 1% of all submissions
- Factory cutting tolerances guarantee some variance

**MANDATORY MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL:**

Step 1: VISUAL ANCHOR IDENTIFICATION
- For border-present cards: Identify the exact pixel where border color changes to design color
- For full-bleed cards: Identify specific design elements (logo edge, text box corner, player centerline)
- Document what you're measuring FROM and TO

Step 2: PIXEL-LEVEL MEASUREMENT
- Count actual pixels or measure relative distances
- Left border width vs Right border width (in pixels or mm)
- Top border width vs Bottom border width (in pixels or mm)
- DO NOT eyeball or estimate - use visual geometry

Step 3: CALCULATE PRECISE RATIO
- Formula: Smaller / (Smaller + Larger) × 100 = percentage
- Example: Left = 95px, Right = 105px → 95/200 = 47.5% → 48/52
- Round to nearest whole percentage (47.5% → 48%, 52.5% → 53%)
- NEVER round both sides to 50/50

Step 4: VALIDATION CHECK
- If you calculate exactly 50/50, RECHECK your measurements
- Look more carefully for asymmetry
- Manufacturing variance guarantees some offset
- Only report 50/50 if you can PROVE perfect symmetry with evidence

**EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS:**
You MUST describe your measurement process in analysis_summary:
- "Front L/R: Measured left border 4.2mm, right border 4.8mm → 47/53"
- "Front T/B: Top margin narrower by ~0.3mm → 48/52"
- "Back L/R: Left border visibly narrower, estimate 2-3% → 48/52"

**FORBIDDEN PATTERNS:**
❌ "Appears centered → 50/50" (too vague)
❌ "Looks good → 50/50" (lazy measurement)
❌ "Well-centered → 50/50" (still needs actual ratio)
✅ "Left border 3.8mm, right border 4.2mm → 48/52" (specific evidence)

**REALISTIC CENTERING DISTRIBUTION:**
- 50/50: < 1% (essentially never)
- 51/49 to 54/46: ~15% (excellent centering)
- 55/45 to 58/42: ~40% (good centering, PSA 10 range)
- 59/41 to 62/38: ~25% (minor OC, PSA 9-10 range)
- 63/37 to 70/30: ~15% (noticeable OC, PSA 8-9 range)
- > 70/30: ~5% (significantly OC, PSA 7 or below)

**WHEN TO REPORT NEAR-PERFECT (51-54):**
- Border widths differ by < 5% visually
- Measured difference is 0.1-0.3mm
- No visible tilt or angle
- Both axes measure within 51-54 range

===========================================
DUAL-SIDED GRADING LOGIC (FRONT/BACK SCORING)
===========================================

Version: DCM-GS-Dual v1.0
Purpose: Separate front and back evaluations with weighted final grade calculation.

1. CORE PRINCIPLE

Each sub-category (Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface) produces TWO separate sub-scores:
- front_score (0.0-10.0)
- back_score (0.0-10.0)

The final numeric grade is weighted as:
- Front = 65% of total (display importance, PSA methodology)
- Back = 35% of total (lower collector sensitivity)

2. CATEGORY WEIGHTING

Category            Weight (of total)    Front Weight    Back Weight
------------------  -------------------  --------------  -------------
Centering           25%                  65%             35%
Corners             25%                  65%             35%
Edges               25%                  65%             35%
Surface             25%                  65%             35%

3. COMPUTATION RULES

For each category:
  weighted_score = (front_score × 0.65) + (back_score × 0.35)

Final grade calculation:
  1. Calculate weighted_score for each of the 4 categories
  2. Average all 4 weighted scores = weighted_total
  3. Apply grade caps based on defect severity:
     - If ANY front-side defect exceeds moderate severity → max final grade = 8.0
     - If ANY back-side defect exceeds heavy severity → max final grade = 7.0
  4. Round final grade to nearest 0.5

4. SCORING EXAMPLES

Example 1 (Modern Card, Clean Back):
- Front centering 55/45 = 9.5
- Back centering 60/40 = 9.0
→ Centering weighted_score = (9.5×0.65) + (9.0×0.35) = 9.3

Example 2 (Minor Back Scratches):
- Front surface = 9.5
- Back surface = 8.5
→ Surface weighted_score = (9.5×0.65) + (8.5×0.35) = 9.15
→ Overall grade capped at 9.0 due to visible back marks

5. DEFECT CAP RULES

Front Defects:
- Minor front defect (< 0.5mm whitening, light scratch) → no cap
- Moderate front defect (0.5-1mm whitening, visible scratch) → max grade 8.0
- Heavy front defect (> 1mm whitening, crease) → max grade 7.0
- **Handwritten marking on front (pen, pencil, marker)** → max grade 6.0 (MAJOR DEFECT)

Back Defects:
- Minor back defect (< 0.5mm whitening, light scratch) → no cap
- Moderate back defect (0.5-1mm whitening, visible scratch) → max grade 8.5
- Heavy back defect (> 1mm whitening, crease) → max grade 7.0
- **Handwritten marking on back (pen, pencil, marker)** → max grade 7.5 (MAJOR DEFECT)

**HANDWRITTEN MARKING SEVERITY GUIDELINES:**
- Small number/letter (< 3mm) on back edge/corner → Heavy severity, max grade 7.5
- Small number/letter (< 3mm) on back center → Heavy severity, max grade 7.0
- Large marking (≥ 3mm) or multiple markings on back → Heavy severity, max grade 6.5
- ANY handwritten marking on front → Heavy severity, max grade 6.0
- Reason: Non-original writing is a permanent alteration that significantly devalues the card

6. SIDE COMPARISON REQUIREMENT

AI must explicitly document differences between front and back:
- "Front corners sharp; back top-left corner shows 0.3mm whitening"
- "Front centering excellent (54/46); back slightly off (62/38)"
- "Surface clean on front; back has faint print line near text box"

GRADING SCALE (1-10)
===========================================

10.0 GEM MINT (Pristine Visual)
- Centering:
  · Front ≤ 55/45 both axes; Back ≤ 55/45 both axes.
- Corners:
  · 4 corners sharp (< 0.05 mm radius).
  · No visible whitening (0.0 mm).
- Edges:
  · Straight cut; 0 roughness > 0.05 mm; no chips, fray, or ink loss.
- Surface / Print:
  · 0 scratches, 0 dots, 0 print lines, 0 indentations, 0 stains.
  · Color uniform and gloss continuous.
- Allowed Tolerance: None.
- Frequency: < 1% of submissions.

9.5 MINT+ (Near Pristine)
- Centering: Front ≤ 60/40; Back ≤ 65/35.
- Corners: ≤ 1 corner with whitening < 0.1 mm or radius ≤ 0.1 mm.
- Edges: ≤ 1 edge with roughness ≤ 0.1 mm; no chips.
- Surface / Print: ≤ 1 hairline scratch (< 5 mm); ≤ 1 print dot ≤ 0.1 mm.
- Other: No creases, no stains.
- Downgrade trigger: Any moderate defect > 0.1 mm.

9.0 MINT
- Centering: Front and back ≤ 60/40 both axes.
- Corners: ≤ 2 corners whitening < 0.5 mm each; radius ≤ 0.3 mm.
- Edges: ≤ 2 edges with minor roughness ≤ 0.2 mm; no chips > 0.2 mm.
- Surface / Print: ≤ 2 minor imperfections (hairline scratch ≤ 10 mm or 2 dots ≤ 0.2 mm).
- Allowed Crease: None.
- Downgrade trigger: Any crease or moderate edge chip > 0.2 mm.

8.5 NM-MT+
- Centering: Front and back ≤ 65/35.
- Corners: 2–3 corners whitening ≤ 0.5 mm; radius ≤ 0.4 mm.
- Edges: ≤ 3 edges minor roughness ≤ 0.3 mm or 1 edge chip ≤ 0.5 mm.
- Surface / Print: Light wear area ≤ 5% of visible surface; 1 faint impression allowed (no paper break).
- Downgrade trigger: Visible crease or stain > 1 mm diameter.

8.0 NM-MT
- Centering: Front and back ≤ 70/30.
- Corners: All 4 corners whitening ≤ 0.5 mm or radius ≤ 0.6 mm.
- Edges: 2–3 edges roughness ≤ 0.5 mm or 1 moderate chip ≤ 1 mm.
- Surface / Print: ≤ 2 visible scratches ≤ 10 mm; 1 light crease (no paper split).
- Allowed Stain: ≤ 1 spot ≤ 1 mm diameter.

7.5 – 7.0 NEAR MINT
- Centering: Front and back 70/30 – 75/25.
- Corners: Moderate wear visible on all corners (whitening ≤ 1 mm or radius ≤ 0.8 mm).
- Edges: Chipping ≤ 1 mm on up to 3 edges.
- Surface: ≤ 3 scratches ≤ 15 mm or 1 light crease with partial gloss break.
- Print: Minor misregistration ≤ 0.3 mm.

6.5 – 6.0 EX-MT
- Centering: Front and back ≤ 80/20.
- Corners: Moderate-heavy whitening > 1 mm or radius ≤ 1 mm on ≥ 2 corners.
- Edges: Noticeable wear or paper fray ≤ 1.5 mm segment each on 2 edges.
- Surface: Light crease crossing image area allowed; ≤ 4 visible scratches.
- Stains: ≤ 3 spots ≤ 2 mm.

5.0 EXCELLENT
- Centering: Front and back ≥ 80/20 (off-center).
- Corners: Heavy whitening > 1.5 mm or rounded radius ≥ 1.5 mm on ≥ 3 corners.
- Edges: Fray > 1 mm or small tear ≤ 2 mm on 1 edge.
- Surface: Visible creases ≥ 2; scratches or surface breaks cover ≤ 10% area.
- Print: Color fade ≤ 15%.

4.0 – 1.0 POOR / DAMAGED
- Centering: Front and back Outside 80/20.
- Corners: Severely rounded (radius > 2 mm) or missing paper.
- Edges: Tears > 2 mm, delamination, or layer separation.
- Surface: Heavy creases, bends, writing, ink, stains covering > 10% area.
- Card Integrity: Compromised (structural bend or warp > 3 mm).
- Use: Record only; not investment grade.

GRADING WORKFLOW
===========================================

0. DETECT PROTECTIVE CASE (FIRST STEP)
   - Analyze card perimeter and surface for case presence
   - Identify case type: penny_sleeve, top_loader, semi_rigid, slab, or none
   - Determine impact level and adjust grading approach accordingly
   - Set adjusted_uncertainty based on case type

1. CHECK FOR AUTOGRAPH (CRITICAL CHECKPOINT - BEFORE ANY GRADING)
   - Scan front and back for visible ink signatures or handwriting
   - If autograph detected:
     → STOP and check for manufacturer authentication markers
     → Look for: hologram, foil stamp, cert number, "Certified Autograph" text
     → If NO authentication found:
       • Set "autograph.present": true
       • Set "autograph.cert_markers": []
       • Set "grading_status": "Grade 2.0 - Altered: Unverified autograph (no manufacturer authentication)"
       • Set "recommended_decimal_grade": 2.0
       • Set "recommended_whole_grade": 2
       • Set "grade_uncertainty": "±0.0"
       • SKIP all remaining defect grading steps
       • Output JSON with grade 2.0 (ALTERED)
     → If authentication IS found:
       • Set "autograph.present": true
       • Set "autograph.type": "on-card" or "sticker"
       • Set "autograph.cert_markers": [list of markers found]
       • Continue to normal grading
   - If no autograph detected:
     → Set "autograph.present": false
     → Continue to next checkpoint

1a. CHECK FOR NON-AUTOGRAPH HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS (CRITICAL CHECKPOINT)
   ⚠️ **MANDATORY SCAN FOR ALTERATIONS - DO NOT SKIP**

   **CRITICAL DISTINCTION - HANDWRITTEN vs PRINTED:**
   You MUST distinguish between:
   - **PRINTED serial numbers** = Part of original card (factory printed, matches card design/font)
   - **HANDWRITTEN markings** = Added after production (pen/pencil ink, different from card text)

   **HOW TO IDENTIFY HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS:**
   - Ink quality differs from card printing (ballpoint pen, marker, pencil)
   - Writing style/font doesn't match card's printed text
   - Often appears in margins, corners, or over blank areas on back
   - May be slightly raised, shiny, or different color than print
   - NOT aligned with card design elements
   - Examples: "100", "23", "$5", initials, checkmarks

   **⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE - DO NOT CONFUSE:**
   - Handwritten "100" on back corner = ALTERATION (heavy defect)
   - Printed "/100" with foil/serial = SERIAL NUMBER (card info, not a defect)

   **WHAT TO LOOK FOR:**
   - ANY pen, pencil, marker, or ink writing NOT part of the original card
   - Inventory numbers, price tags, initials, letters, symbols
   - Check BOTH sides, but ESPECIALLY the BACK (most common location)

   **COMMON HANDWRITTEN EXAMPLES:**
   - Numbers: "23", "100", "A-14", "C2" (written in pen/pencil)
   - Prices: "$5", "2.00", ".50"
   - Initials/names: "JB", "Mike", "RC"
   - Symbols: checkmarks, circles, stars

   **IF HANDWRITTEN MARKING DETECTED:**
   → Report in appropriate surface.other field (front or back)
   → Set "severity": "heavy" (handwritten marks are MAJOR defects)
   → Set detailed "description": "Handwritten [content] in [color] ink/pencil on [location] - approximately [size]mm - NOT part of original card"
   → Will automatically cap grade per rules (7.5 max for back, 6.0 max for front)
   → Document in observations: "Handwritten [description] detected on [location] - alteration/inventory mark"
   → Add to negatives: "Permanent handwritten marking - major alteration defect"
   → DO NOT list this as a serial number in card_info

   **IF NO MARKINGS FOUND:**
   → Continue to normal grading
   → No need to mention in observations unless specifically noting card is clean

2. EXAMINE CARD INFORMATION & EXTRACT TEXT
   - Identify player, set, year, manufacturer, card number
   - Note sport/category
   - Extract serial number if present - **ONLY if PRINTED on card** (e.g., "45/99", "/100")
     · Serial numbers are factory-printed, usually with foil/stamp
     · DO NOT extract handwritten numbers (those are defects reported in Step 1a)
   - Look for RC logo or rookie designation
   - Determine rarity tier using detection hierarchy
   - Read and transcribe main text box, stat table, copyright from card back
   - Assign text_confidence based on readability
   - Classify rarity features and assign rarity_score (1-10)
   - Tag all applicable features from master list

3. ASSESS CENTERING (DUAL-SIDED) - FOLLOW MANDATORY MEASUREMENT PROTOCOL

   **CRITICAL: Follow Section 3.5 CRITICAL MEASUREMENT VALIDATION**

   Step 3a: DETECT CARD ORIENTATION (FIRST)
   - Measure card aspect ratio (Width ÷ Height)
   - Classify as portrait (H/W > 1.1), landscape (W/H > 1.1), or square (0.9-1.1)
   - Set primary_axis: "horizontal" for portrait, "vertical" for landscape

   Step 3b: IDENTIFY CENTERING METHOD
   - Classify card type: Border-Present (Type A), Double-Border (Type B), Full-Bleed (Type C), or Asymmetric (Type D)
   - Identify visual anchors for measurement
   - Document what you're measuring FROM and TO

   Step 3c: MEASURE WITH PRECISION (NOT ESTIMATION!)
   - For border-present: Measure actual border widths on each side
   - For full-bleed: Identify design elements and measure their position relative to edges
   - Count pixels or use relative measurements
   - DO NOT default to 50/50 - manufacturing variance guarantees offset

   Step 3d: CALCULATE RATIOS
   - Front L/R: Calculate precise ratio (e.g., 48/52, 51/49, 46/54, 53/47 - NOT 50/50)
   - Front T/B: Calculate precise ratio (e.g., 52/48, 44/56, 49/51, 47/53 - NOT 50/50)
   - Back L/R: Calculate precise ratio (most backs differ from front by 1-4%)
   - Back T/B: Calculate precise ratio (rarely matches front exactly)
   - Identify worst_axis and worst_ratio_value

   **REALISTIC CALCULATION EXAMPLES:**
   - Card A: Front L/R = 52/48, Front T/B = 54/46, Back L/R = 51/49, Back T/B = 53/47 → Worst: 54/46
   - Card B: Front L/R = 47/53, Front T/B = 49/51, Back L/R = 48/52, Back T/B = 51/49 → Worst: 47/53
   - Card C: Front L/R = 42/58, Front T/B = 52/48, Back L/R = 44/56, Back T/B = 50/50 → Worst: 42/58 (caps grade severely)

   Step 3e: DOCUMENT EVIDENCE
   - Include measurement details in front_observations and back_observations

   **GOOD DOCUMENTATION EXAMPLES:**
   ✅ "Front L/R: Left border 3.8mm, right border 4.2mm → 48/52 (right-heavy)"
   ✅ "Front T/B: Top margin 3.5mm, bottom margin 4.5mm → 44/56 (significant bottom-heavy, caps grade)"
   ✅ "Back L/R: Left border slightly narrower, measured 3.9mm vs 4.3mm right → 48/52"
   ✅ "Back T/B: Top and bottom nearly equal, top 4.1mm vs bottom 3.9mm → 51/49"
   ✅ "Design anchor method: Player centerline 47% from left edge → 47/53"

   **BAD DOCUMENTATION (DO NOT USE):**
   ❌ "Centering looks good → 50/50"
   ❌ "Appears centered → 50/50"
   ❌ "Well-centered → 50/50"
   ❌ "No obvious off-center → 50/50"

   - Never report 50/50 without specific evidence of perfect symmetry (e.g., "Both sides measure exactly 4.0mm")

   Step 3f: VALIDATE MEASUREMENTS
   - If you calculated 50/50, RECHECK - it's likely incorrect
   - Refer to realistic centering distribution (< 1% are truly 50/50)
   - Apply lens-correction offset if card is in a case

4. INSPECT DEFECTS SYSTEMATICALLY (DUAL-SIDED) - USE EDGE WEAR DETECTION PROTOCOL

   **CRITICAL: Follow "EDGE WEAR & WHITENING DETECTION (COLOR CHANGE RULE)" section**

   Step 4a: CORNER INSPECTION (All 4 corners, both sides)
   - For EACH corner (top_left, top_right, bottom_left, bottom_right):
     1. Zoom to corner area in image
     2. Compare corner color to adjacent border/design color (ΔE color analysis)
     3. Look for whitening (exposed white card stock), chipping, or rounding
     4. Apply two-point validation:
        A. Color deviation: ΔE > 6 from expected color?
        B. Edge irregularity: Break in smooth edge line OR texture change?
     5. If BOTH present → Measure size (< 0.5mm, 0.5-1mm, > 1mm) → Classify severity
     6. Document in JSON: severity ("none", "minor", "moderate", "heavy") + description with size

   Step 4b: EDGE INSPECTION (All 4 edges, both sides)
   - For EACH edge (top, bottom, left, right):
     1. Scan along entire edge length
     2. Look for roughness, chipping, nicks, or uneven factory cutting
     3. Check for color deviation (ΔE > 6) indicating exposed stock
     4. Measure extent of any irregularities
     5. Classify severity based on size
     6. Document location and characteristics

   Step 4c: SURFACE INSPECTION (Both sides)
   - Check for scratches (hairline vs visible)
   - Check for creases (light vs moderate vs heavy)
   - Check for print defects (dots, lines, missing ink)
   - Check for stains or discoloration
   - Check for abrasion/rubbing (dull patches, inconsistent reflectivity)
   - **CHECK FOR HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS (CRITICAL):**
     · ANY pen, pencil, marker, or ink writing NOT part of original card
     · Numbers, letters, initials, symbols, inventory marks, price tags
     · Check BACK of card especially (common location for inventory marks)
     · Even small markings are MAJOR defects - significantly impact grade
     · Report in surface.other with severity based on size/visibility
   - Be thorough - look for MINOR issues too (even 0.1mm defects matter)

   Step 4d: CASE vs CARD DISTINCTION
   - If card is in a case: ignore outer case edges, use inner card contour
   - Require double-angle confirmation for surface defects (prevent case reflection false positives)
   - Distinguish between case scratches and card scratches

   Step 4e: SCORING
   - For each category (Corners, Edges, Surface):
     · Calculate front_score (0.0-10.0)
     · Calculate back_score (0.0-10.0)
     · Calculate weighted_score = (front × 0.65) + (back × 0.35)
   - Apply grade caps based on severity (see SEVERITY IMPACT ON GRADING section)
   - Document differences between front and back explicitly

   Step 4f: VALIDATION
   - If you report all corners as "none" (perfect), RECHECK - manufacturing variance guarantees some wear
   - Even "gem mint" cards typically have micro-whitening < 0.2mm on at least 1-2 corners
   - Be appropriately critical - look closer for subtle defects

5. EVALUATE IMAGE QUALITY
   - Rate the images themselves (A/B/C/D)
   - Note focus, glare, lighting, color issues
   - If card is in top loader or semi-rigid: downgrade image quality one level (A → B, B → C)
   - Recommend reshoot if C or D, or if ≥20% of visible area obstructed by glare

6. DETERMINE GRADE (DUAL-SIDED WEIGHTED CALCULATION)
   - **IMPORTANT**: If Step 1 resulted in grade 2.0 due to unverified autograph, skip this step entirely (grade already determined)
   - Calculate sub_scores for all 4 categories:
     · Centering: weighted_score = (front × 0.65) + (back × 0.35)
     · Corners: weighted_score = (front × 0.65) + (back × 0.35)
     · Edges: weighted_score = (front × 0.65) + (back × 0.35)
     · Surface: weighted_score = (front × 0.65) + (back × 0.35)
   - Calculate weighted_total = average of all 4 weighted scores
   - Apply grade caps:
     · If ANY front defect is moderate+ → max grade 8.0
     · If ANY back defect is heavy → max grade 7.0
     · If front centering > 75/25 → cap accordingly
     · If back centering > 80/20 → cap accordingly
   - Set final recommended_decimal_grade (rounded to nearest 0.5)
   - Assign recommended_whole_grade
   - Document grade_cap_reason if any cap was applied
   - Set base uncertainty (±0.0 for A images, ±0.25 for B, ±0.5 for C, ±1.0 for D)
   - Add case-related uncertainty if applicable (±0.25 penny sleeve, ±0.5 top loader/semi-rigid)
   - If slabbed: skip numeric grading, output null for recommended_grade

7. WRITE ANALYSIS SUMMARY (DUAL-SIDED)
   - Front observations: List 2-4 front-specific aspects (centering, corners, edges, surface condition)
   - Back observations: List 2-4 back-specific aspects (centering, corners, edges, surface condition)
   - Positives: List 2-4 overall good aspects (strongest features from both sides)
   - If card is in a case: include appropriate positive call-out (e.g., "Protected card, minimal handling wear")
   - Negatives: List ALL defects found on both sides (be honest and thorough)
   - If card is in a case: include case-related advisory (e.g., "Sleeve reflections present; surface accuracy slightly reduced")
   - Explicitly compare front vs back when conditions differ
   - **If grade 2.0 (altered)**: Include "Unverified autograph detected without manufacturer authentication - card is altered and not suitable for normal grading" in negatives

8. OUTPUT JSON
   - Verify all fields present
   - Ensure valid JSON syntax
   - NO markdown, NO code blocks, ONLY JSON

COMPANY-SPECIFIC GRADING LOGIC
---

**PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator)**

Weighting: Centering 40%, Corners 30%, Edges 15%, Surface 15%
Output: Whole grades only (10, 9, 8, 7, 6, etc.)

Grade Tolerances:
- 10 Gem Mint: ≤55/45 front, ≤75/25 back, ZERO visible defects, no whitening
- 9 Mint: ≤60/40 front, ≤90/10 back, max ONE minor defect (slight wear <0.5mm)
- 8 NM-MT: ≤65/35 front, ≤90/10 back, small fray or print dot acceptable
- 7 NM: ≤70/30 front, ≤90/10 back, noticeable corner wear or minor surface issue
- 6 EX-MT: ≤75/25 front, ≤90/10 back, light creases or corner wear

Rules:
- Cap at Mint 9 if ANY whitening >0.5mm detected
- Cap at NM-MT 8 if surface flaw visible to naked eye
- Round to nearest whole grade
- Conservative on centering (front centering is critical)

**BGS (Beckett Grading Services)**

System: Four subgrades (Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface) equally weighted at 25% each
Output: 0.5 increments (10, 9.5, 9, 8.5, 8, etc.)

Subgrade Scale:
- 10 Pristine: 50/50-55/45 front, ≤55/45 back, ZERO flaws
- 9.5 Gem Mint: ≤55/45 front, ≤70/30 back, ONE micro-flaw allowed
- 9 Mint: ≤60/40 front, ≤90/10 back, minor whitening/print dot ok
- 8.5 NM-MT+: ≤65/35 front, ≤90/10 back, noticeable but small flaw
- 8 NM-MT: ≤70/30 front, ≤90/10 back, multiple visible flaws

Calculation Rules:
- Convert YOUR sub_scores (centering, corners, edges, surface) to BGS 1-10 scale
- Final grade = weighted average of 4 subgrades, rounded to nearest 0.5
- CRITICAL: Final grade CANNOT exceed lowest subgrade by more than 0.5
- If lowest_subgrade = 9.0 → max overall = 9.5
- If all subgrades ≥9.5 → 9.5 or 10 possible

Example: Subgrades [9.5, 9.5, 9.0, 9.5] → Overall 9.5 (not 10, because 9.0 exists)

**TAG Grading**

System: Machine-vision precision model with 0.1 increments
Output: Decimal grades from 1.0-10.0 (e.g., 9.7, 9.3, 8.8)

Tolerances (EXTREMELY STRICT):
- 10.0 Pristine: ≤51/49 front, ≤54.5/45.5 back, ZERO visible defects
- 9.9-9.5 Gem: ≤55/45 front, ≤65/35 back, only microscopic defects
- 9.4-9.0 Mint: ≤60/40 front, ≤80/20 back, small print line or nick ok
- 8.9-8.5 NM-MT: ≤65/35 front, ≤85/15 back, one visible flaw
- 8.4-8.0 NM-MT-: >65/35 front, >85/15 back, multiple visible flaws

Deduction System:
- Micro-defect (light edge nick, dust inclusion): -0.1
- Minor whitening (<0.5mm): -0.2
- Moderate whitening (0.5-1mm): -0.5
- Surface scratch: -0.3 to -0.7 depending on length
- Print line: -0.2 to -0.5
- Each centering point beyond threshold: -0.1 per percentage point

Round to nearest 0.1 for final output.

**CSG (Certified Sports Guaranty)**

System: Simplified consumer-friendly scale
Output: 0.5 increments (10, 9.5, 9, 8.5, 8, etc.)

Grade Scale:
- 10 Gem Mint: ≤55/45 front, ≤70/30 back, perfect to naked eye
- 9.5 Mint+: ≤60/40 front, ≤85/15 back, near perfect, one minor flaw
- 9 Mint: ≤60/40 front, ≤90/10 back, small edge wear or slight defect
- 8.5-8 NM-MT: ≤65/35 front, ≤90/10 back, slight fray or light chipping
- 7 EX: ≤70/30 front, ≤90/10 back, corners dull or small crease

Rules:
- Round to nearest 0.5
- Cap at 9 if any whitening visible to naked eye
- More lenient than PSA/BGS on minor defects
- Focus on overall eye appeal
