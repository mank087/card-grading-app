  * More visible on dark-colored edges
  * Created by micro-chipping exposing white cardstock
- **Common locations:** Bottom edge segments (B2, B3, B4), corner-adjacent segments
- **Severity levels:**
  * **1-2 dots, each <0.2mm:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **3-5 dots or 0.2-0.5mm dots:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Multiple clusters or >0.5mm dots:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**ğŸ”¬ MICRO WHITE DOT DETECTION PROTOCOL (<0.1mm dots):**

**âš ï¸ CRITICAL:** Micro white dots on edges are the MOST COMMONLY MISSED defects. These tiny dots disqualify 10.0 grade but are frequently overlooked.

**What micro white dots look like:**
- **Appear as tiny bright spots along edge line** (like pinpoints of light)
- **Size: 0.05-0.15mm diameter** (1-3 pixels at high resolution)
- **MOST visible on dark edges** (black, navy, dark red, dark blue borders)
- **May appear as isolated single dots OR cluster of 2-4 micro-dots**
- **Located ON the edge line** (not on surface, but at the physical edge)
- **Create tiny "break" or "gap" in the edge color continuity**

**How to detect micro white dots (CRITICAL TECHNIQUE):**

1. **ZOOM IN to Level 3 on edge segment** - edge should fill horizontal/vertical view
2. **Scan SLOWLY left-to-right (or top-to-bottom)** along edge line
3. **Look for ANY brightness discontinuities** - tiny light spots against edge color
4. **Watch for "sparkle" effect** - micro-dots catch light like tiny stars
5. **Compare edge segments** - does this segment have lighter spots vs adjacent segments?
6. **Check corner-adjacent areas** - micro-dots commonly appear near corners
7. **Spend full 10-15 seconds per segment** - rushing = missing micro-dots

**Visual comparison:**
```
PERFECT EDGE (Grade 10.0):
- Edge line is continuous, uniform color
- No brightness changes or light spots
- Completely smooth color from end to end

MICRO WHITE DOTS (Grade 9.5):
- 1-3 tiny white/light spots along edge (0.05-0.15mm)
- Small brightness discontinuities in edge line
- Appear as "breaks" in the color continuity
```

**Detection tips by edge color:**
- **Black edges:** Micro white dots appear as BRIGHT white spots (EASIEST to see)
- **Navy/dark blue edges:** Look for light blue or white micro-spots
- **Dark red/burgundy edges:** Look for pink or light spots
- **Yellow/gold edges:** Look for very light yellow or white spots
- **White edges:** HARDEST - look for slightly brighter areas or texture changes

**Where micro white dots commonly appear:**
- **Bottom edge center segments (B2, B3, B4):** MOST COMMON location (60-70% of edge defects)
- **Top edge near corners (T1, T5):** Second most common
- **Corner-adjacent segments:** Any segment next to a corner with whitening
- **Edge midpoints:** Where card contacts surfaces during handling

**Common mistakes when detecting micro white dots:**
âŒ "Bottom edge looks clean" â†’ You MUST zoom to Level 3 and scan segment by segment
âŒ "I don't see any dots" â†’ Scan SLOWER, look for tiny brightness changes
âŒ "The edge looks uniform" â†’ Compare to adjacent segments - are there ANY lighter spots?
âŒ "Maybe it's image noise" â†’ Trust what you see - micro-dots are real defects
âŒ "It's too small to count" â†’ Even ONE dot 0.1mm = Max grade 9.5, not 10.0

**Statistical reality:**
- **60-70% of cards have micro white dots on bottom edge**
- **40-50% of cards have micro white dots on top edge**
- **Grade 10.0 requires ZERO dots on all 40 edge segments**
- **If you're finding zero edge defects â†’ re-scan bottom edge B2, B3, B4 immediately**

**Confirmation bias warning:**
- When a card "looks perfect," your brain wants to confirm it's perfect
- This causes you to scan FASTER and MISS micro-defects
- **Counter this:** Assume defects exist until proven otherwise
- **Force yourself to scan slowly** even when card looks mint

**CONTINUOUS WHITENING (Linear Edge Wear)**
- **Description:** White or light-colored line/band running along edge length
- **Detection method:** Look for consistent linear whitening (not isolated dots)
- **Visual cues:**
  * Appears as stripe or band along edge
  * May be thin hairline or wider band
  * Consistent along segment length (not isolated)
  * Caused by rubbing, fraying, or wear
- **Measurement:** Estimate width (thin <0.3mm, moderate 0.3-1mm, wide >1mm) and length
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Thin line <0.3mm wide, <3mm long:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Moderate line 0.3-1mm wide or >3mm long:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Wide band >1mm or multiple edges affected:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**EDGE CHIPPING (Material Loss)**
- **Description:** Missing edge material creating irregular, jagged edge profile
- **Detection method:** Look for uneven edge line where material is absent
- **Visual cues:**
  * Edge line appears jagged, not smooth
  * Visible "bites" or chunks missing from edge
  * Creates white areas larger than dots (exposed cardstock)
  * May show depth/shadow indicating material loss
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Minor chipping:** 1-2 small chips <0.5mm - Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Moderate chipping:** Multiple chips or 0.5-1mm - Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Heavy chipping:** Extensive material loss >1mm - Grade impact: Max 8.0 or lower

**EDGE ROUGHNESS (Uneven Cut)**
- **Description:** Edge line is not perfectly straight, has texture/irregularity
- **Detection method:** Check straightness of edge line along segment
- **Visual cues:**
  * Edge appears wavy, bumpy, or uneven
  * May be factory defect (poor cut) or handling wear
  * Edge texture varies along length
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Slight roughness:** Barely perceptible - Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Moderate roughness:** Clearly visible unevenness - Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Heavy roughness:** Significantly jagged edge - Grade impact: Max 8.5

**COLOR DEVIATION (Exposed Cardstock)**
- **Description:** Edge color differs from printed surface color
- **Detection method:** Compare edge pixels to adjacent printed area
- **Visual cues:**
  * Edge appears lighter than expected for printed color
  * White or off-white showing through
  * Î”E > 4 indicates exposed cardstock (whitening)
- **Measurement:** Estimate color difference perceptually
- **Severity levels:** Severity correlates with amount/visibility of deviation

**STEP 2: MULTI-STAGE SURFACE SCAN**

**STAGE 1: INITIAL GLOSS/REFLECTIVITY ASSESSMENT (Level 1 - Normal View)**

Before detailed zone scanning, perform overall gloss check:

5. **Assess severity** (minor, moderate, heavy)

**STEP 4: SURFACE DEFECT CATEGORIES**

**CATEGORY 1: SCRATCHES & ABRASIONS**

**Hairline Scratches**
- **Description:** Very fine lines, often <0.1mm wide, barely visible
- **Detection method:** Look for thin lines that catch light at certain angles
- **Visual cues:**
  * Appears as thin line disrupting surface texture
  * May only be visible when light reflects at specific angle
  * Often appears slightly lighter or darker than surface
  * Most visible on high-gloss or solid-color areas
- **Common locations:** Center zone (Zone 5), high-traffic handling areas
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Single hairline <5mm long:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Multiple hairlines OR >5mm long:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Hairline across critical area (player face):** Grade impact: Max 9.0

**Visible Scratches**
- **Description:** Clear linear marks >0.1mm wide, visible under normal lighting
- **Detection method:** Easily spotted during normal viewing
- **Visual cues:**
  * Linear marks clearly disrupting surface
  * May show as white line (if through coating) or dark line (if in coating)
  * Catch light and are obvious
- **Measurement:** Estimate length and width
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Single visible scratch 5-10mm:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Multiple scratches OR >10mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Deep scratch or across face/critical area:** Grade impact: Max 8.0

**Abrasion Patches**
- **Description:** Areas of surface wear, appear as dull or matte finish in localized area
- **Detection method:** Look for loss of reflectivity compared to surrounding surface
- **Visual cues:**
  * Dull or matte patch in otherwise glossy surface
  * May appear as "rubbed" or "worn" area
  * Light doesn't reflect evenly from this area
  * Surface texture differs from surrounding area
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Small patch <3mm:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Moderate patch 3-10mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Large patch >10mm or multiple patches:** Grade impact: Max 8.0

---

**CATEGORY 2: PRINT DEFECTS**

**Print Dots/Spots**
- **Description:** Small colored or white dots that don't match card design
- **Detection method:** Look for pixels/dots that appear out of place
- **Visual cues:**
  * Most visible on solid color backgrounds
  * White dots in dark areas OR dark dots in light areas
  * Size typically 0.1-1mm diameter
  * May be single dot or cluster
  * Clearly not part of printed design
- **PSA Note:** PSA 10 criteria allows "slight printing imperfection if it doesn't impair overall appeal"
- **Severity assessment:**
  * **Single tiny dot <0.3mm in non-critical area, doesn't impair appeal:** May still allow 10.0 per PSA standards
  * **Single dot 0.3-1mm OR in critical area OR multiple dots:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Multiple obvious dots or cluster:** Grade impact: Max 9.0

**Print Lines**
- **Description:** Linear printing imperfections (roller marks, ink streaks, drag lines)
- **Detection method:** Look for lines that don't belong to card design
- **Visual cues:**
  * Straight or slightly curved lines
  * Often lighter or darker than surrounding area
  * May run horizontally, vertically, or diagonally
  * Typically thin (<0.5mm wide) but vary in length
- **Types:**
  * Roller marks: Consistent parallel lines from printing roller
  * Ink streaks: Irregular ink distribution
  * Drag lines: Lines from printing process drag/smear
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Single faint line <5mm, non-critical area:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Obvious line >5mm OR multiple lines:** Grade impact: Max 9.0

**Hickeys (Print Donuts)**
- **Description:** Circular printing defects, appear as ring or donut shape
- **Detection method:** Look for circular patterns with missing or doubled ink
- **Visual cues:**
  * Circular or oval shape
  * Center may be lighter/darker with ring around it
  * Appears as halo or donut pattern
  * Size typically 1-3mm diameter
- **Cause:** Debris on printing plate/roller
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Single small hickey <1mm, non-critical area:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Obvious hickey >1mm OR multiple:** Grade impact: Max 9.0

**Registration Errors**
- **Description:** Color layers misaligned during printing
- **Detection method:** Look for "ghost" images or rainbow effects at edges
- **Visual cues:**
  * Text or images have colored halo/shadow
  * Colors don't line up perfectly
  * May appear as double image slightly offset
  * Most visible on text and sharp graphic edges
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Very slight misalignment, barely perceptible:** Grade impact: Max 9.5
  * **Noticeable misalignment:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Severe misalignment affecting readability:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**Out-of-Focus Printing**
- **Description:** Printed elements lack sharp definition, appear blurry
- **Detection method:** Compare sharpness to similar cards; text and images should be crisp
- **Visual cues:**
  * Text edges appear soft or blurred
  * Images lack fine detail
  * Overall "fuzzy" appearance to printed elements
- **Comparison:** Similar cards should have sharp, crisp printing
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Slight softness, still readable:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Obvious blur, reduced readability:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**CATEGORY 3: STAINS & DISCOLORATION**

**Wax Stains**
- **Description:** Cloudy, oily, or translucent spots (common on card backs from pack wax)
- **Detection method:** Look for areas with different translucency or sheen
- **Visual cues:**
  * Appears as cloudy or oily spot
  * May be slightly translucent
  * More common on backs than fronts
  * Often near edges or corners
  * May show as darker area when viewing at angle
- **Common locations:** Back surface, especially near top edge
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Very slight wax stain on back only:** PSA 9 allows this per standards
  * **Obvious wax stain on back:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Wax stain on front OR heavy stain:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**Water Damage**
- **Description:** Warping, rippling, or color bleeding from moisture exposure
- **Detection method:** Look for surface ripples, color runs, or warping
- **Visual cues:**
  * Card surface appears rippled or wavy
  * Colors may have bled or run together
  * Paper may be expanded/contracted creating waves
  * May show tide lines or water marks
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Minor stain, no warping:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Visible warping or color bleeding:** Grade impact: Max 7.5 or lower

**Ink Marks (Foreign Ink)**
- **Description:** Ink or pen marks not original to card (may be accidental marks vs intentional writing)
- **Detection method:** Look for ink that doesn't match card design/font
- **Visual cues:**
  * Ink color/texture differs from printed elements
  * Often appears as accidental smudge or small mark
  * Distinguish from HANDWRITTEN MARKINGS (which trigger grade cap in Stage 1)
- **Critical distinction:**
  * **Handwritten inventory numbers, names, etc.:** Already caught in Stage 1, severe grade cap
  * **Accidental ink smudge/spot <1mm:** Minor defect, document here
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Very minor ink spot <0.5mm:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Visible ink mark >0.5mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.5

**Yellowing/Border Discoloration**
- **Description:** Age-related or environmental discoloration, especially on white borders
- **Detection method:** Compare border color to expected white/off-white
- **Visual cues:**
  * Borders appear yellowed, browned, or discolored
  * May be uniform or have darker spots
  * More apparent on white/light-colored borders
  * May indicate age, sunlight exposure, or humidity damage
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Very slight off-white tint:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Obvious yellowing:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Heavy discoloration/browning:** Grade impact: Max 8.0 or lower

**CATEGORY 4: SURFACE DAMAGE**

**Surface Indentations**
- **Description:** Depressions in card surface without breaking surface layer
- **Detection method:** Look for shadowing or light catchment indicating depression
- **Visual cues:**
  * Visible depression or "valley" in surface
  * Casts shadow depending on light angle
  * Surface layer intact (no break/crack)
  * Often caused by pressure from objects stacked on card
- **Measurement:** Estimate size and depth perception
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Small indentation <2mm:** Grade impact: Max 9.0
  * **Obvious indentation 2-5mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Large indentation >5mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.0

**Surface Dents**
- **Description:** Compressed areas with visible surface disruption
- **Detection method:** Look for compressed surface, may show whitening from layer compression
- **Visual cues:**
  * Visible dent or crater in surface
  * Surface layer disrupted (may see whitening)
  * Often caused by dropping object on card or impact
  * May combine characteristics of indentation + whitening
- **Severity levels:**
  * **Minor dent <2mm with slight whitening:** Grade impact: Max 8.5
  * **Moderate dent 2-5mm:** Grade impact: Max 8.0
  * **Heavy dent >5mm or multiple dents:** Grade impact: Max 7.5 or lower

**Creases**
- **Description:** Fold lines in card (should be caught in Stage 1, but mention here for completeness)
- **Detection:** Look for linear disruption with visible fold, raised ridge, or valley
- **Note:** Creases typically trigger maximum grade cap (4.0 or lower per Stage 1 rules)
- **If crease detected:** Note in surface inspection, but grade cap already applied in Stage 1
