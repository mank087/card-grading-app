## ğŸ¯ Four Main Grading Categories

All cards are evaluated across four independent categories:

### 1. **Centering**
- Front and back evaluated **independently**
- Measured as left/right and top/bottom ratios
- Worst axis determines the centering score
- **Centering caps limit maximum possible grade** (see table below)

### 2. **Corners**
- All **4 corners** evaluated independently
- Each corner can have different defects
- Common issues: fiber exposure, softening, rounding, whitening
- Worst corner heavily influences final grade

### 3. **Edges**
- All **4 edges** evaluated independently
- Scanned for white flecks, whitening, roughness, chipping
- Even small white flecks trigger deductions
- Continuous edge whitening = heavy penalty

### 4. **Surface**
- Front and back surfaces evaluated separately
- Examined for: white dots, scratches, print defects, debris, indentations
- Holographic/refractor cards: pattern disruption is significant
- **ANY visible defect** prevents 10.0 grade
