# TASK 1: IMAGE ANALYSIS & CARD DETECTION

## Step 1A: Detect Card Boundaries

1. Locate the actual card boundaries in both uploaded images (front and back)
2. Verify standard trading card aspect ratio ≈ 2.5×3.5 (0.714 ratio)
3. Note any obstructions (shadows, glare, hands, sleeves, holders)

## Step 1B: Assess Image Quality

**CRITICAL: Default to Tier A or B unless there are OBVIOUS quality issues.**

Evaluate image quality tier based on these factors:

**Quality Tier A (Optimal):** ← **DEFAULT for good photos**
- Resolution ≥1000px, straight-on angle (0-5° deviation)
- Clear boundaries, good lighting, no obstructions
- No perspective distortion
- Sharp focus, can see card details clearly
- **NO GRADING CAP**

**Quality Tier B (Good):** ← **Use for minor issues only**
- Resolution 700-1000px, minor angle deviation (5-15°)
- Minor lighting issues or slight obstructions
- Minimal perspective distortion
- **NO GRADING CAP**

**Quality Tier C (Fair):** ← **ONLY use if photo has OBVIOUS problems**
- Resolution 500-700px, moderate angle (15-30°)
- **Noticeable blur/glare affecting ability to see defects**
- Moderate perspective distortion making measurements difficult
- **⚠️ GRADING CAP: Maximum grade = 9.0**
- **Use ONLY if you cannot confidently assess defects due to photo quality**

**Quality Tier D (Poor):** ← **RARELY use - only for unusable photos**
- Resolution <500px, severe angle (>30°)
- **Significant obstructions/blur making grading impossible**
- Severe perspective distortion
- **⚠️ GRADING CAP: Maximum grade = 7.0**
- **Use ONLY if photo is nearly unusable**

**IMPORTANT RULE**: If you can clearly see the card, identify text, and assess defects, use **Tier A or B**. Only use Tier C/D if the photo quality genuinely limits your ability to grade accurately.

## Step 1C: Document Detection Confidence

**Detection Confidence Levels:**
- **High:** Clear boundaries, excellent visibility
- **Good:** Minor visibility issues
- **Moderate:** Some factors affecting measurement accuracy
- **Low:** Significant limitations on accurate assessment
- **None:** Unable to detect card boundaries

Record all factors that may affect grading accuracy (angle, lighting, obstructions, resolution).

## Step 1D: Identify Card Border Color (for Defect Context)

**CRITICAL: Identify the card's factory border design to prevent false defect flags**

Examine both front and back images and determine the factory border characteristics:

**Border Color Types:**
- **White Borders**: Card has white borders as factory design
- **Black Borders**: Card has black borders as factory design
- **Colored Borders**: Card has colored borders (blue, red, gold, etc.)
- **Photo/Borderless**: No visible borders, image extends to edges
- **Mixed**: Front and back have different border colors

**Store this information - you will reference it in Task 4 defect inspection**

**Examples:**
- "White borders on front and back"
- "Black borders on front, white on back"
- "Borderless design with holographic edges"
- "Gold parallel borders on both sides"

This border identification prevents false positives like flagging white corners on white-bordered cards.

## Step 1E: Identify Card Finish Type (for Surface Defect Context)

**CRITICAL: Identify the card's factory finish to prevent false surface defect flags**

Examine both front and back images and determine the factory surface finish:

**Finish Types:**
- **Glossy**: Shiny, reflective surface with smooth finish
- **Matte**: Non-reflective, flat finish with no shine
- **Holographic/Refractor**: Rainbow-like reflective patterns, prismatic effects
- **Textured**: Raised or embossed patterns, non-smooth surface
- **Foil**: Metallic reflective finish (gold, silver, etc.)
- **Mixed**: Different finishes on front vs back, or combination finishes

**Store this information - you will reference it in Task 4 surface defect inspection**

**Examples:**
- "Glossy finish on front and back"
- "Holographic refractor finish on front, matte on back"
- "Textured finish with raised player image"
- "Gold foil borders with glossy center"

**Why This Matters:**
- Matte cards shouldn't be flagged for "appearing dull" (that's their design)
- Holographic cards have intentional refraction lines (not scratches)
- Textured cards have intentional surface variance (not scuffs)
- Foil cards show natural pattern variations (not color variance)

# TASK 2: CARD IDENTIFICATION

## Step 2A: Extract Basic Card Information

Identify the following from card images:

**Card Name:** Full card name as printed (e.g., "2023 Panini Prizm Justin Herbert")

**Player(s)/Character(s):** Full name(s) of featured player(s)

**Card Set:** Complete set name (e.g., "2023 Panini Prizm")

**Manufacturer:** Card manufacturer (e.g., "Panini", "Topps", "Upper Deck")

**Release Date:** Year of release (e.g., "2023")

**Category:** Sport type (e.g., "Football", "Basketball", "Baseball")

## Step 2B: Identify Card Number vs Serial Numbering

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION:**

**Card Number (Product Identifier):**
- Set number identifying this card design (e.g., "#250", "RTS-MLD", "C-15")
- Found on back or bottom edge near copyright/manufacturer info
- Same for ALL copies of this card design
- **IGNORE:** Jersey numbers and years—these are NOT card numbers

**Serial Numbering (Individual Card Identifier):**
- Limited print run numbers (e.g., "25/99", "123/500", "/25")
- ONLY on numbered parallels/limited editions
- Makes each individual card unique
- Usually prominent on front or back
- **If not visible, use "N/A"—do NOT fabricate numbers**

## Step 2C: Identify Special Features

**Autographed:** Yes/No (is there a visible signature?)

**Autograph Authentication:** If autographed, look for manufacturer authentication:
- Holograms, authentication stickers, serial numbers
- "AUTHENTIC" or "AUTO" text printed on card
- **WITHOUT visible authentication: This is an alteration, not a certified autograph**

**Memorabilia:** Yes/No (jersey pieces, patches, fabric swatches)

**Parallel/Insert Type:** Specific type (e.g., "Prizm Base", "Gold Parallel", "Silver Prizm")

**Rookie/First Print:** Yes/No/N/A (based on "RC" or "Rookie" designation visible on card)

## Step 2D: Determine Detailed Rarity

**MANDATORY: Provide detailed rarity descriptions with specific features**

**Format Examples:**
- "Base/Common - Standard base set card with no special features"
- "Insert/Subset - Rookie Ticket with memorabilia piece and rookie designation"
- "Premium (/25-/99) - Numbered parallel with holographic foil and autograph"
- "Elite (/10-/24) - Low-numbered parallel with special border design"
- "Ultra Premium (/5-/9) - Single-digit numbered with autograph and memorabilia"

**NEVER use short classifications alone** (e.g., just "Insert/Subset")

**ALWAYS cite specific trigger features:** serial numbering, autograph, memorabilia, rookie status, parallel design, case hit designation

# TASK 3: CENTERING MEASUREMENT

## Step 3A: Measure Border Ratios

**CRITICAL: You must use ONLY the standard ratios listed below. Do not create custom ratios.**

Examine border thickness on both front and back images. Compare the thicker border to the thinner border and select the closest standard ratio from the approved list.

### Standard Front Centering Ratios (ONLY use these):
- **50/50** - Perfect centering
- **55/45** - Excellent centering (Grade 10 threshold)
- **60/40** - Very good centering (Grade 9 threshold)
- **65/35** - Good centering (Grade 8 threshold)
- **70/30** - Fair centering (Grade 7 threshold)
- **75/25** - Off-center (Grade 6 threshold)
- **80/20** - Significantly off-center (Grade 5 threshold)
- **85/15** - Very off-center (Grade 4 threshold)
- **90/10** - Severely off-center (Grade 3 threshold)
- **>90/10** - Miscut territory (Grades 1-2)

### Standard Back Centering Ratios (ONLY use these):
- **50/50** - Perfect centering
- **60/40** - Excellent centering (Grade 10 threshold)
- **70/30** - Good centering
- **80/20** - Fair centering (Grade 9 threshold)
- **85/15** - Off-center (Grade 8 threshold)
- **90/10** - Significantly off-center (Grade 7 threshold)
- **95/5** - Very off-center (Grades 2-4)
- **>95/5** - Miscut territory (Grade 1)

### How to Measure:

1. **Identify the worse axis** (left/right OR top/bottom) for each side
2. **Visually estimate OR use provided measurements** - if OpenCV measurements are provided in additional_instructions, use those as reference
3. **CRITICAL: Map to nearest standard ratio** - round to the closest approved ratio from the lists above
4. **Report the standard ratio** that is closest to your observation or measurement
5. **Measure both front and back separately** - they will usually differ

**MAPPING RULES:**
- 50/50 to 52/48 → Report **50/50**
- 53/47 to 57/43 → Report **55/45**
- 58/42 to 62/38 → Report **60/40**
- 63/37 to 67/33 → Report **65/35**
- 68/32 to 72/28 → Report **70/30**
- 73/27 to 77/23 → Report **75/25**
- 78/22 to 82/18 → Report **80/20**
- 83/17 to 87/13 → Report **85/15**
- 88/12 to 92/8 → Report **90/10**

**EXAMPLES:**
- OpenCV reports 51/49 → Map to **50/50**
- OpenCV reports 56/44 → Map to **55/45**
- OpenCV reports 63/37 → Map to **65/35**
- Visual estimate shows moderate imbalance → Report **80/20**

**FORBIDDEN:**
- Using exact OpenCV ratios like "51/49", "56/44", "63/37"
- Custom ratios not in the standard tables
- Ratios not listed in the standard tables above

## Step 3B: Determine Centering Starting Grade

Use the WORSE ratio between front and back to determine starting grade:

### Grading Thresholds:

| Starting Grade | Front Centering Threshold | Back Centering Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **10** (Pristine) | 50/50 or 55/45 | Up to 60/40 |
| **9** (Mint) | Up to 60/40 | Up to 80/20 |
| **8** (NM-MT) | Up to 65/35 | Up to 85/15 |
| **7** (Near Mint) | Up to 70/30 | Up to 90/10 |
| **6** (EX-MT) | Up to 75/25 | Up to 90/10 |
| **5** (Excellent) | Up to 80/20 | Up to 90/10 |
| **4** (VG-EX) | Up to 85/15 | Up to 95/5 |
| **3** (Very Good) | Up to 90/10 | Up to 95/5 |
| **2** (Good) | Up to 90/10 | Up to 95/5 |
| **1** (Poor-Fair) | Worse than 90/10 | Worse than 95/5 |

### Grading Logic:

1. Determine front centering ratio using standard ratios only
2. Determine back centering ratio using standard ratios only
3. Identify the WORSE of the two ratios
4. Use the worse ratio to determine starting grade from table above
5. Front centering is weighted MORE heavily (stricter thresholds)

**EXAMPLE:**
- Front: 55/45 → Would qualify for Grade 10
- Back: 85/15 → Would qualify for Grade 8
- **Worse ratio is back (85/15) → Starting Grade = 8**

**This starting grade is your anchor for all subsequent calculations.**

# TASK 5: GRADE CALCULATION

## Step 5A: Count Defects

Count every defect you marked YES in Task 4. This is your **Total Defect Count**.

## Step 5B: Calculate Base Final Grade

**FORMULA:** Final Grade = Centering Starting Grade - Total Defect Count

**CRITICAL: The "Final Grade (After Deductions)" field MUST match your calculation in the Grade Analysis Summary.**

**EXAMPLES:**
- Starting Grade 10, Defects 0 → Final = 10 - 0 = **10** ← Use this EXACT number
- Starting Grade 10, Defects 2 → Final = 10 - 2 = **8** ← Use this EXACT number
- Starting Grade 9, Defects 1 → Final = 9 - 1 = **8** ← Use this EXACT number
- Starting Grade 8, Defects 3 → Final = 8 - 3 = **5** ← Use this EXACT number

**IMPORTANT:** If you calculate "10 - 0 = 10" in your Grade Analysis Summary, you MUST put **10** (not 9, not 9.5, not anything else) in the "Final Grade (After Deductions)" field.

**VERIFY YOUR MATH BEFORE CONTINUING - Both fields must match!**

## Step 5C: Apply Image Quality Cap (if needed)

**CRITICAL: Most cards should NOT have a cap applied.**

From Task 1B, check your Image Quality Tier:

- **Tier A:** NO CAP - use calculated grade
- **Tier B:** NO CAP - use calculated grade
- **Tier C:** If Final Grade > 9.0, reduce to 9.0 (ONLY if you marked Tier C due to photo problems)
- **Tier D:** If Final Grade > 7.0, reduce to 7.0 (ONLY if you marked Tier D due to severe photo problems)

**IMPORTANT**: If you can see the card clearly and confidently assessed all defects, you should have marked Tier A or B, which means NO CAP applies.

**The cap is ONLY for genuinely poor quality photos that limited your grading accuracy.**

## Step 5D: Apply Alteration Override (if needed)

If you marked **altered_writing**, **altered_trimmed**, **altered_recolored**, **altered_pressed**, **altered_bleached**, or **altered_rebacked** as YES:
- **OVERRIDE:** Final Grade = 2.0 (regardless of all other factors)

# REFERENCE DEFINITIONS

## False Positive Prevention Guide

### Common False Positive Scenarios

This reference helps you avoid incorrectly flagging factory card features as defects.

#### **Scenario 1: White-Bordered Cards**

**Card Type**: Card with factory white borders on front and/or back

**Common False Positives**:
- Flagging white corners as "corner whitening" when they match the factory border
- Flagging white edges as "edge whitening" when they are part of the design

**Correct Approach**:
- ✅ Mark NO for corner/edge whitening if corners match uniform factory white borders
- ✅ Only mark YES if you see ADDITIONAL damage/wear beyond the factory border color
- ✅ Look for discoloration, roughness, or damage that differs from the clean factory white

**Visual Clues for Actual Damage**:
- Corners show roughness or fraying beyond smooth border
- White appears dingy, dirty, or discolored vs clean factory white
- Visible cardboard fibers or layer separation at corners/edges

---

#### **Scenario 2: Black-Bordered Cards**

**Card Type**: Card with factory black borders

**Common False Positives**:
- Flagging edge whitening when card naturally has black borders showing slight factory variance

**Correct Approach**:
- ✅ Only flag whitening if you see white card stock REVEALED through damage
- ✅ Black borders showing slight lightness = not a defect unless clearly damaged
- ✅ Look for white card stock visible through worn areas

---

#### **Scenario 3: Colored/Foil-Bordered Cards**

**Card Type**: Card with colored (blue, red, gold) or foil/holographic borders

**Common False Positives**:
- Flagging corner/edge whitening too aggressively on colored borders

**Correct Approach**:
- ✅ Flag whitening when white card stock is visible through the colored layer
- ✅ Slight color variance in foil = not necessarily damage
- ✅ Look for actual white cardboard showing through colored surface

---

#### **Scenario 4: Borderless/Photo Cards**

**Card Type**: Card with image extending to edges (no visible border)

**Correct Approach**:
- ✅ These cards ARE susceptible to edge/corner whitening
- ✅ Any white showing at edges/corners = likely damage
- ✅ More aggressive whitening detection is appropriate for borderless designs

---

## Corner Rounding Reference (for Task 4)

**What is corner rounding?**

A corner is considered "rounded" when it shows **visible wear or damage beyond the factory cut**.

**Factory Tolerance Rule:**
- Modern cards have slight corner radius from factory cutting
- If ALL corners show uniform, small-radius cuts → This is normal → Mark NO
- Only flag YES when corners show ADDITIONAL rounding/wear beyond factory norms

**Mark YES (rounded) when:**
- One or more corners are visually MORE curved than the others
- Corner angle is flattened/curved enough that it no longer forms a crisp point
- Corners show uneven wear (some sharp, some worn)
- Corner radius is inconsistent across the four corners

**Mark NO (not rounded) when:**
- All four corners show uniform factory-cut radius
- Corners are consistent with each other
- No visible additional wear or damage
- Uncertain due to glare, sleeve, or image resolution

**When in doubt → Mark NO**

---

## Soft Corner Reference (for Task 4)

**What is a soft corner?**

A soft corner shows compression or flattening while the edge angle remains sharp (not rounded).

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Corner appears "squashed" or "pressed down"
- Edge remains sharp with preserved 90° angle (NOT rounded)
- One corner noticeably different from the others in flatness
- Often accompanied by slight bending or creasing
- Looks like pressure was applied to corner

**Distinguish from Rounding:**
- **Soft Corner**: Sharp edge, flattened appearance
- **Rounded Corner**: Edge itself is curved/worn, not sharp

**Factory Tolerance:**
- If all 4 corners look identical → factory cutting → mark NO
- Only flag if corner compression is isolated to 1-2 corners

**When in doubt → Mark NO**

---

## Fraying Reference (for Task 4)

**What is fraying?**

Visible paper fibers extending from corner or edge that are clearly distinguishable in the uploaded photo.

**Visibility Threshold:**
- Must be visible without zooming or enhancement
- Multiple fibers, not just one microscopic strand
- Extends noticeably beyond the card edge
- Can see individual paper fibers

**Factory Tolerance:**
- Minor fiber remnants from factory cutting consistent across ALL corners = NOT fraying
- Uniform microscopic edge texture = normal factory cut

**When to Flag:**
- One or more corners/edges show OBVIOUS fraying different from others
- Paper separation visible in normal photo lighting
- Fibers clearly extending beyond edge line

**When in doubt → Mark NO**

---

## Surface Wear Reference (for Task 4)

**What is surface wear?**

Areas where the surface finish has degraded from handling, showing loss of original luster or texture.

**By Finish Type (from Task 1E):**

**Glossy Cards:**
- Look for areas that lost shine compared to unworn spots
- Dull patches where gloss has been rubbed away
- Compare to pristine areas on same card

**Matte Cards:**
- Look for areas MORE matte or rougher than intentional finish
- Do NOT flag entire matte surface as wear
- Only flag if degradation visible beyond factory matte

**Holographic/Refractor Cards:**
- Look for areas where holo pattern is worn/faded
- Do NOT flag normal refraction variance
- Pattern should be visibly disrupted, not just angled differently

**Foil Cards:**
- Look for areas where foil finish is worn down
- Do NOT flag intentional pattern variations
- Actual wear shows dulling or loss of metallic sheen

**Visibility Threshold:**
- Must see clear degradation in normal lighting
- Compare worn area to pristine areas on same card
- If entire card appears uniform, mark NO (likely factory finish)

**When in doubt → Mark NO**

## Autograph Authentication Reference (for Task 2C & Task 4)

**Certified Autograph Indicators:**
- Hologram stickers on card
- Authentication serial numbers printed on card
- "AUTHENTIC" or "AUTO" text printed on card front/back
- Manufacturer authentication markings

**WITHOUT these indicators:**
- Describe as "card with added signature" or "altered with signature"
- Mark `altered_writing` as YES in Task 4
- NEVER use terms like "factory-certified autograph" or "authentic autograph"
