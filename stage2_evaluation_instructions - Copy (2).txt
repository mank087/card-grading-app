## MASTER SCALE (Decimal-Based → Whole-Number Conversion)

### Decimal Scoring Bands
- **10.0 Pristine**  
  Perfect centering: 50/50 front, 50/50 back (≤55/45 front, ≤60/40 back).  
  No visible flaws under 10x magnification. Sharp corners, flawless edges, pristine gloss.

- **9.9 – 9.8 Near-Pristine**  
  Nearly flawless, microscopic flaw possible under magnification.  
  Centering ≤55/45 front, 65/35 back.

- **9.7 – 9.5 Gem Mint**  
  Virtually perfect to naked eye. Minor micro imperfection possible.  
  Centering ≤55/45 front, 70/30 back.

- **9.4 – 9.0 Mint**  
  Strong corners, one may show hint of wear.  
  Centering ≤60/40 front, 80/20 back.

- **8.9 – 8.5 Near Mint-Mint+**  
  Corners sharp, slight fraying. Minor print or surface flaws.  
  Centering ≤65/35 front, 85/15 back.

- **8.4 – 8.0 Near Mint-Mint**  
  Corners show slight fuzzing, small edge chips.  
  Centering ≤65/35 front, 90/10 back.

- **7.9 – 7.0 Near Mint**  
  Noticeable wear on multiple corners, minor surface issues.  
  Centering ≤70/30 front, 90/10 back.

- **6.9 – 6.0 Excellent-Mint**  
  Rounded corners, visible edge wear, light surface scratches.  
  Centering ≤75/25 front, 90/10 back.

- **5.9 – 5.0 Excellent**  
  Noticeable corner/edge wear, small creases. Gloss reduced.

- **4.9 – 4.0 Very Good-Excellent**  
  Multiple rounded corners, edge wear, creases visible.

- **3.9 – 3.0 Very Good**  
  Heavy corner wear, pronounced creases, staining possible.

- **2.9 – 2.0 Good**  
  Severe wear, staining, gloss loss, heavy creases.

- **1.9 – 1.0 Poor to Fair**  
  Extreme damage, tears, writing, trimming, heavy creases.

## GRADING LOGIC

1. **Start with Base Centering Grade**
   - Use the worst centering ratio (front or back).  
   - Assign decimal starting grade from the Master Scale.  
   - Example: 56/44 front = 9.5 start; 65/35 = 8.5 start.

2. **Apply Deductions for Defects**
   - Each visible defect deducts from the decimal grade:  
     - Minor corner whitening: –0.1 to –0.3  
     - Noticeable corner wear: –0.5  
     - Edge chip / nick: –0.2 to –0.5  
     - Significant edge separation: –1.0  
     - Light surface scratch/print line: –0.2 to –0.4  
     - Multiple scratches/creasing: –0.5 to –1.0  
     - Heavy crease, stain, major alteration: auto cap at ≤5.0  

3. **Apply Grade Caps**
   - Certain flaws enforce maximum possible grades:  
     - Visible crease: max 6.0  
     - Paper loss / delamination: max 5.0  
     - Alteration detected: max 1.0  

4. **Finalize Decimal Grade**
   - Subtract deductions from starting grade.  
   - Floor cannot go below 1.0.  

5. **Map to Whole Number**
   - Convert decimal into 1–10 whole band per mapping table.  

6. **Output Final JSON**
